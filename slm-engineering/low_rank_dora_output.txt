low_rank_dora.py - reference impl (LoRA + DoRA from scratch).
Numbers below feed LOW_RANK_DORA.md.  torch = 2.12.1

Concept: LoRA learns a low-rank patch BA; DoRA splits the update
into a magnitude vector m and a low-rank direction V/||V||_col,
decoupling 'how much' from 'which way' at ~LoRA param cost.

Reading the table:
- full-FT grows as d*k (quadratic in the width); LoRA/DoRA grow as
  r*(d+k) (LINEAR in the width). So the savings explode at scale:
  at d=k=64, r=4, full=4096 but LoRA=512 and DoRA=576 -- ~8x fewer.
- DoRA costs exactly k MORE params than LoRA (the magnitude vector).
  At real scale (d=k=4096, r=8) that is +4096 out of ~65536 -- about
  +6%, i.e. the ~0.2% overhead the DoRA paper reports. Negligible.
- When r >= min(d,k) the 'low-rank' bet stops paying (LoRA can exceed
  full-FT, e.g. d=k=8, r=8 -> 128 > 64). Real use keeps r << min(d,k).

========================================================================
  SECTION E: why decouple magnitude and direction (the DoRA claim)
========================================================================
STEP 1 -- any weight matrix factors as magnitude odot direction.
W = m odot (W / ||W||_col),  where m = ||W||_col (length k) and
W/||W||_col has unit columns (each column is a direction). This is
Salimans & Kingma's weight normalization (arXiv:1602.07868), which
DoRA reuses per column.

direction = W / ||W||_col  (unit columns)  (shape (4, 4))

STEP 2 -- LoRA CONFLATES magnitude and direction; DoRA frees them.
Give B learned values (same as Section A's trained B):

Decompose LoRA's merged W' = W + BA the same way:

| col j | ||W|| (before) | ||W+BA|| (after) | magnitude ratio | direction cosine |
|-------|---------------|------------------|-----------------|------------------|
| 0     |        1.0262 |           1.2373 |          1.2057 |           0.9370 |
| 1     |        1.5708 |           1.8730 |          1.1924 |           0.9836 |
| 2     |        0.8753 |           1.2229 |          1.3971 |           0.9743 |
| 3     |        1.5522 |           1.5192 |          0.9788 |           0.9977 |

LoRA's single patch BA shifts BOTH the magnitude (||W+BA|| != ||W||)
AND the direction (cosine < 1) at once -- you cannot ask LoRA to
rescale a column WITHOUT also turning it. That is the limitation
DoRA targets: full fine-tuning updates magnitude and direction
INDEPENDENTLY, and LoRA's proportional coupling can't mimic it.

STEP 3 -- DoRA keeps them independent by construction.
direction(V) = V/||V||_col does NOT depend on m at all. So in DoRA:
  - moving m  -> rescales each column's MAGNITUDE only (direction
    unchanged, because m never enters V/||V||_col);
  - moving BA -> changes the DIRECTION only (low-rank, cheap), then
    m re-scales it back to the learned magnitude.

========================================================================
  LINEAGE RECAP
========================================================================
| method   | merged weight                 | trainable params | magnitude / direction |
|----------|-------------------------------|------------------|------------------------|
| full-FT  | W + dW  (all weights)         | d*k              | both move, independently |
| LoRA     | W + (alpha/r)BA               | r(d+k)           | both move, PROPORTIONALLY (tied) |
| DoRA     | (m / ||V||_col) odot V        | r(d+k) + k       | magnitude (m) and direction (BA) FREE |

DoRA matches full-FT's *behaviour* (independent magnitude/direction
updates) at ~LoRA's *cost* (only +k params). Empirically it often
beats LoRA at the same rank, and even matches LoRA at HALF the rank
(Liu et al 2024, arXiv:2402.09353).
