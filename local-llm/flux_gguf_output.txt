flux_gguf.py - Running Flux.1 [dev] locally with GGUF quantization.
Pure Python stdlib. Numbers below feed FLUX_GGUF.md.

Story: 12B DiT model (23 GB FP16) -> GGUF block quant -> fits 8 GB GPU

==========================================================================
  SECTION A: FLUX.1 ARCHITECTURE - DiT, not U-Net
==========================================================================
Flux.1 [dev] (Black Forest Labs, Aug 2024) is a Diffusion Transformer
(DiT). The denoising network is a transformer, NOT the U-Net used by
SD 1.5 / SDXL. This matters for quantization (Section C).

Component breakdown:
  DiT denoiser   : 12.0B params  (the big one, 12B)
  T5-XXL encoder : 4.7B params  (256-token context)
  CLIP-L encoder : 0.4B params  (small, always fp16)
  VAE            : decodes latent -> pixel (always fp16/fp32)

Why a transformer? Flux uses flow matching (not DDPM): a straighter
sampling trajectory that needs fewer steps (20-28 vs SDXL's 30-50).
The DiT processes the noisy latent + text embeddings via attention.

Full-precision weight sizes:
  DiT  fp16 = 24.0 GB   (12B x 2 bytes)
  T5   fp16 = 9.4 GB    (4.7B x 2 bytes)
  Total     = 33.4 GB (before VAE + activations)

==========================================================================
  SECTION B: VAE COMPRESSION - pixel space to latent space
==========================================================================
The VAE compresses a 1024x1024 RGB image into a small latent that the
DiT actually denoises. The DiT NEVER sees pixels directly - it works
in compressed latent space, which is why 12B params is affordable at all.

Pixel space : 1024x1024x3 =  3,145,728 values
Latent space: 128x128x16 =    262,144 values

Spatial downsample = 1024/128 = 8x per axis
  -> 8x8 = 64x fewer spatial positions
Channel expansion   = 16/3 = 5.33x
Net compression     = 64/5.33 = 12.0x

Attention sequence length:
  if DiT saw pixels : 1,048,576 tokens  (1024x1024)
  in latent space   :   16,384 tokens  (128x128)
  -> 64x shorter sequence = 4096x less attention compute

GOLD (for FLUX_GGUF.html):
  compression = 1024*1024*3 / (128*128*16)
             = 3,145,728 / 262,144
             = 12.0x

==========================================================================
  SECTION C: THE SIZE PROBLEM - 23 GB won't fit a consumer GPU
==========================================================================
Flux DiT at FP16 = 12B params x 2 bytes = 24 GB (actual checkpoint: 23 GB)

Consumer GPU VRAM tiers (2024-2026):
  GPU               VRAM  Fits FP16?
  ---------------- -----  ----------
  GTX 1060            6GB          no
  RTX 3060/4060       8GB          no
  RTX 3060 12GB      12GB          no
  RTX 4070           12GB          no
  RTX 4080           16GB          no
  RTX 3090/4090      24GB         YES

Only 24 GB GPUs (3090/4090, ~$1000+) can hold the FP16 model + T5 +
VAE + activations. Everyone else is stuck... unless we quantize.
[check] only 24GB+ GPUs fit FP16 Flux :  OK

==========================================================================
  SECTION D: GGUF QUANT LEVELS - same block format as LLMs
==========================================================================
ComfyUI-GGUF (by city96) applies the SAME GGUF block quantization that
llama.cpp uses for LLMs (see quant_types.py) to the Flux DiT weights.
Each level packs more weights per byte at a quality cost.

| level    | file size | eff bpw | quality loss | min GPU |
|----------|-----------|---------|--------------|---------|
| FP16     |  23.0 GB |  15.33 |   ~0.0%      |  24 GB  |
| Q8_0     |  12.0 GB |   8.00 |   ~0.5%      |  12 GB  |
| Q4_K_M   |   7.0 GB |   4.67 |   ~1.8%      |   8 GB  |
| Q4_K_S   |   6.5 GB |   4.33 |   ~2.0%      |   8 GB  |
| Q3_K_S   |   5.5 GB |   3.67 |   ~4.0%      |   6 GB  |

Effective bpw = file_size_GB * 8 / 12B_params. It is slightly below
the STRUCTURAL bpw from quant_types.py (Q8_0=8.5, Q4_K_M=4.84) because
the checkpoint keeps a few sensitive layers (embeddings, output) at
higher precision, pulling the average down.

Why GGUF works for Flux but NOT for SDXL:
  Flux = DiT (transformer):   dense matmuls, very quant-tolerant.
  SDXL = U-Net (conv2d):      conv kernels degrade fast below 8-bit.
  => 4-bit Flux is great; 4-bit SDXL is mush. Same bytes, different arch.

==========================================================================
  SECTION E: VRAM BUDGET - the full accounting per quant level
==========================================================================
File size is NOT the VRAM cost. You also need memory for the text
encoder (T5), the VAE, and the intermediate activations during sampling.

VRAM = DiT_weights + T5_encoder + VAE + activations/overhead

T5 can be loaded at full precision OR quantized separately (also GGUF).
  T5 fp16 = 9.4 GB    (best text adherence)
  T5 Q4   = 2.0 GB    (saves 7.4 GB, minor text quality loss)

Fixed terms: VAE = 0.5 GB, activations+CLIP-L = 2.0 GB

| DiT quant | DiT   | T5    | VAE  | act  | TOTAL  | fits GPU |
|-----------|-------|-------|------|------|--------|----------|
| FP16      | 23.0G |  9.4G | 0.5G | 2.0G |  34.9G |    24 GB |
| Q8_0      | 12.0G |  9.4G | 0.5G | 2.0G |  23.9G |    24 GB |
| Q4_K_M    |  7.0G |  2.0G | 0.5G | 2.0G |  11.5G |    12 GB |
| Q4_K_S    |  6.5G |  2.0G | 0.5G | 2.0G |  11.0G |    12 GB |
| Q3_K_S    |  5.5G |  2.0G | 0.5G | 2.0G |  10.0G |    12 GB |

Key takeaway: pairing a Q4 DiT with a Q4 T5 lets Flux run on an 12 GB
GPU (RTX 3060 12GB). Q8 DiT + fp16 T5 still needs 24 GB. The DiT is
the dominant term, but the T5 choice swings the budget by ~7 GB.

==========================================================================
  SECTION F: FP8 vs GGUF - two paths to lower VRAM
==========================================================================
There are two ways to shrink Flux below FP16. They optimize for
different things:

|              | FP8                          | GGUF                        |
|--------------|------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| format       | 8-bit float (e4m3/e5m2)      | block quant (Q4-Q8)         |
| VRAM         | ~12 GB (same as Q8_0)        | 6.5-12 GB (pick your level) |
| speed        | FASTER (native fp8 matmul)   | SLOWER (dequant on the fly) |
| hardware     | Ada/Hopper+ ONLY (RTX 40+)   | ANY GPU (even old Maxwell)   |
| quality      | ~1% loss (good)              | <1%-4% (depends on level)    |
| comfyUI      | built-in (LoadDiffusionModel) | ComfyUI-GGUF custom node     |

The tradeoff in one line:
  fp8  = fast + needs new hardware + one size (12 GB).
  GGUF = slower + works everywhere + you pick the size (6.5-12 GB).

Rule of thumb:
  - RTX 4090/4080/5090 (Ada/Hopper, fp8 HW): use fp8 (faster, same VRAM).
  - RTX 3060/4060/2060/1060 or AMD/Mac:       use GGUF (only option).
  - Want the SMALLEST possible?                GGUF Q3_K_S (5.5 GB DiT).

| Your GPU     | DiT quant | T5 quant | Total VRAM | Quality     |

| 12 GB (3060 12G) | Q3_K_S    | Q4      |    10.0 GB | good (headroom) |

The sweet spot for most users: Q4_K_M DiT + Q4 T5 on an 8-12 GB GPU.
~7 GB DiT file, ~10-12 GB total VRAM, <2% quality loss vs FP16.
