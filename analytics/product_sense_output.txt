========================================================================
  SECTION 0: the scenario  (clarify scope, 2 min)
========================================================================
The worked example for every section below.

  prompt            : Facebook engagement is declining in the 18-24 cohort
  segment           : users aged 18-24
  platform          : mobile (iOS + Android)
  goal              : engagement (growth + retention)
  constraint        : ad-supported -- must not regress revenue materially
  PRIMARY metric    : 18-24 DAU / MAU ratio
  GUARDRAIL metric  : ad revenue per DAU (ARPU)
  COUNTER metric    : self-reported satisfaction (NPS-like, -10..10)

  Clarifying questions asked FIRST (max 3-4):
    Q1 'engagement' = DAU? time-in-app? interactions? -> DAU/MAU ratio
    Q2 recent trend or long-term? -> 3-quarter decline, accelerating
    Q3 isolated to 18-24 or cross-demographic? -> isolated to 18-24 mobile
    Q4 any surfaces off-limits? -> feed is in-play; ads cannot regress >2%

  Scope locked. Now segment, map the journey, then solve -- in order.

========================================================================
  SECTION 1: the 8-step framework  (problem -> users -> metrics -> tradeoffs)
========================================================================
Run these IN ORDER. Each step gates the next. Jumping ahead is trap #1.

  step                  | time | what to do
  ----------------------|------|-----------------------------------------
  1. Clarify scope       | 2 min | segment, platform, goal, constraints. Max 3-4 questions.
  2. User segments       | 3 min | 2-3 segments (primary/secondary/power). Name the underserved one.
  3. User journey        | 3 min | Awareness->Acquisition->Activation->Engagement->Retention. Drop-off each.
  4. Pain points         | 3 min | Name EACH with a data signal (NPS, tickets, recordings, churn).
  5. Solutions           | 5 min | 3-5, breadth-first. ONE per root cause -- not all in one area.
  6. Prioritize          | 3 min | RICE / ICE + Impact x Feasibility. State what you DEPRIORITIZE.
  7. Success metrics     | 2 min | PRIMARY + GUARDRAIL + COUNTER. Name the gaming path.
  8. Tradeoffs & risks   | 2 min | Suppression trap, segment-split risk, what you are NOT building.

  9/10 vs 6/10 separator: the 9/10 names DATA SIGNALS at step 4 and
  names the SUPPRESSION TRAP at step 8 unprompted. Everything else is
  structure that a 6/10 also has.

========================================================================
  SECTION 2: the user journey  (drop-off at each stage)
========================================================================
The 18-24 mobile funnel, per 100,000 reached. The step with the
largest drop is where investment earns the most.

  stage        |  users  | step conv | drop-off | definition
  -------------|---------|-----------|----------|---------------------------
  Awareness    |  100000 |    ---    |   ---    | saw install surface / ad
  Acquisition  |   42000 |     42.0% |    58.0% | installed the app
  Activation   |   27300 |     65.0% |    35.0% | first session > 60s (meaningful)
  Engagement   |   13650 |     50.0% |    50.0% | weekly active (>=3 sessions/wk)
  Retention    |    5460 |     40.0% |    60.0% | active on day 30 (D30)

  overall (Awareness -> Retention) = 5.5%

  drop-off ranking (where to invest):
    Engagement     -> Retention    :  60.0% lost
    Awareness      -> Acquisition  :  58.0% lost
    Activation     -> Engagement   :  50.0% lost
    Acquisition    -> Activation   :  35.0% lost

  BIGGEST drop: Engagement -> Retention (60.0%). That transition
  is the highest-leverage place to spend. Sections 3-5 solve around it.

========================================================================
A 6/10 says 'users are frustrated'. A 9/10 names the SIGNAL and the
journey transition it lives at. Each pain -> a different solution.

  # | pain                          | sev | maps-to transition        | data signal
  --|-------------------------------|-----|---------------------------|---------------------------
  2 | Content format mismatch       |  5  | Activation -> Engagement  | time-in-app -14% QoQ; photo interactions down, video up off-platform
  4 | Competitive displacement (TikTok) |  5  | Engagement -> Retention   | panel data: 18-24 mobile time-share -9pp; opens/day -12%
  1 | Onboarding friction           |  4  | Acquisition -> Activation | session recordings: 35% bail at step 3; support tickets

  Every pain is testable from the signal: pull NPS by cohort, read
  session recordings at the named transition, count support tickets by
  topic, check panel time-share. NO pain is asserted without a signal.

========================================================================
  SECTION 4: scoring model  (RICE + ICE for feature prioritization)
========================================================================
Two independent scoring models over the same solutions. RICE weights
Reach x Impact x Confidence / Effort (favors cheap, broad wins). ICE
averages Impact + Confidence + Ease on 1-5 (favors easy, sure things).
Disagreement between them is a signal, not a problem.

  # | solution                          |   RICE |  ICE | RICE rank
  --|-----------------------------------|--------|------|----------
  1 | Short-form video feed (Reels)     |   6552 | 3.67 |    #2
  2 | Groups discovery (interest graph) |   3733 | 3.67 |    #4
  3 | Streamlined 3-step onboarding     |  24570 | 4.33 |    #1
  4 | Collaborative content creation    |   1024 | 2.67 |    #5
  5 | Algorithmic feed re-rank (video-1st) |   5801 | 3.67 |    #3

  RICE ranking: Streamlined 3-step onboarding > Short-form video feed (Reels) > Algorithmic feed re-rank (video-1st) > Groups discovery (interest graph) > Collaborative content creation
  ICE  ranking: Streamlined 3-step onboarding > Short-form video feed (Reels) > Groups discovery (interest graph) > Algorithmic feed re-rank (video-1st) > Collaborative content creation

  RICE top: Streamlined 3-step onboarding (score 24570) -- cheap, broad,
  high-confidence. The scoring model's instinct is 'ship the quick win'.
  BUT see Section 5: scoring is an INPUT to judgment, not a substitute.

RICE says onboarding first. The 9/10 answer overrides with STRATEGIC

root cause of the 18-24 decline is FORMAT MISMATCH (pain #2, severity

strategic engagement bet (week 4-12). Deprioritize collab explicitly.

  2 | Groups discovery (interest graph) |    2.0 | 0.70 |    3.0 |  2.0/4
  3 | Streamlined 3-step onboarding     |    2.0 | 0.90 |    2.0 |  3.0/4
  4 | Collaborative content creation    |    3.0 | 0.50 |    8.0 |  0.0/4
  5 | Algorithmic feed re-rank (video-1st) |    2.0 | 0.85 |    4.0 |  1.0/4

  DECISION (sequenced roadmap):
    Phase 1 (ship first):  #3 Streamlined onboarding  -- quick win,

    Phase 2 (strategic):   #1 Reels                    -- the engagement

  SECTION 6: metric tradeoff analysis  (the suppression trap)
========================================================================
Each solution's estimated impact on PRIMARY (DAU%), GUARDRAIL (Rev%),
COUNTER (Sat pts). NET blends them with weights 0.5/0.3/0.2.

  # | solution                          |  DAU% |  Rev% |  Sat |   NET | read
  --|-----------------------------------|-------|-------|------|-------|-------------------------
  1 | Short-form video feed (Reels)     | +8.5  | -3.2  | +6.0 |  4.49 | SUPPRESSION TRAP
  2 | Groups discovery (interest graph) | +3.1  | +1.0  | +4.0 |  2.65 | 
  3 | Streamlined 3-step onboarding     | +5.2  | +0.8  | +2.0 |  3.24 | 
  4 | Collaborative content creation    | +2.0  | -0.5  | +8.0 |  2.45 | SUPPRESSION TRAP
  5 | Algorithmic feed re-rank (video-1st) | +6.0  | -4.5  | -2.0 |  1.25 | SUPPRESSION TRAP

  Reads:
    #1 Reels  : big DAU + Sat lift, but Rev DOWN -> the suppression trap.
                Ship ONLY with a revenue guardrail + 21-day test (Section 7).
    #5 Re-rank: DAU up but Sat DOWN -> the primary is being gamed. Hold.
    #3 Onboard : all positive, cheap -> safe quick win (Phase 1).

  NET ranking: Short-form video feed (Reels)(4.49) > Streamlined 3-step onboarding(3.24) > Groups discovery (interest graph)(2.65) > Collaborative content creation(2.45) > Algorithmic feed re-rank (video-1st)(1.25)
  Note: NET can flip the RICE order. Reels ranks #1 on NET despite the
  revenue drag, because the DAU + Sat lift outweighs it at these weights.
  Change the weights (e.g. guardrail 0.5) and Reels drops below onboarding.
  The weights encode the business priority -- state them out loud.

========================================================================
  SECTION 7: scenario walkthrough  (Reels A/B -- branching decisions)
========================================================================
Week-2 readout tree for the Reels experiment. Each leaf carries the
DAU/Rev/Sat deltas; the recommendation is COMPUTED from a rule that
encodes the suppression trap and the gaming test.

[0] ROOT: ship Reels A/B, 14-day minimum
    (primary=18-24 DAU, guardrail=Revenue, counter=Sat)
    note: pre-registered plan; powered for DAU; revenue guardrail added (ad surface)
  [1] Branch A: clean win
      readout: DAU +8.5%  Rev +0.3%  Sat +6.0  ->  NET +5.54
      -> SHIP
      (SHIP at day 14)
  [2] Branch B: SUPPRESSION TRAP
      readout: DAU +8.5%  Rev -3.2%  Sat +6.0  ->  NET +4.49
      -> ITERATE (suppression trap)
      (ITERATE: extend to 21d, throttle ad load in the Reels surface)
    [3] Branch B1: revenue recovers
        readout: DAU +8.5%  Rev -0.5%  Sat +6.0  ->  NET +5.30
        -> SHIP
        (SHIP with throttled ad load)
    [4] Branch B2: revenue still down
        readout: DAU +7.0%  Rev -4.0%  Sat +4.0  ->  NET +3.10
        -> ITERATE (suppression trap)
        (KILL or hold out power users)
  [5] Branch C: primary failed
      readout: DAU +0.5%  Rev -3.2%  Sat -1.0  ->  NET -0.91
      -> KILL (primary failed)
      (KILL -- guardrail regression with no primary win)
  [6] Branch D: segment split
      readout: DAU +9.0%  Rev +1.0%  Sat +7.0  ->  NET +6.20
      -> SEGMENTED ROLLOUT
      (SEGMENTED ROLLOUT to 18-24 only)

  decision rule (recommend()):
    seg_split                     -> SEGMENTED ROLLOUT
    DAU <= 2%                     -> KILL (primary failed)
    DAU > 2% AND Rev <= -1%       -> ITERATE (suppression trap)
      unless Sat < 0              -> KILL (suppression + gaming)
    DAU > 2% AND Sat < 0          -> HOLD (gaming signal)
    otherwise                     -> SHIP

  The whole point: a 'good' primary is NOT enough. The guardrail and
  counter tests gate the ship decision. Branch B is the suppression trap
  -- the most common real-world Reels/Feed outcome -- and it does NOT
  ship at day 14. It iterates, because revenue is a hard constraint here.

========================================================================
  SECTION 8: the five interview traps  (and the fix)
========================================================================
  trap                                       | failure mode                                 | fix
  -------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|---------------------------
  Jumping to solutions without user research  | Proposing features before naming the problem  | Open with clarifying questions; map the journey first
  Building for the 'average user'             | There is no average user; headline metrics hide segments | Always segment (primary / secondary / power) before diagnosing
  Proposing a single metric as success        | Any metric can be gamed; 'DAU' alone hides quality | Primary + guardrail + counter; name the gaming path
  Forgetting feasibility                      | An 18-month ML rebuild is not an interview answer | Score Effort; prefer sequencing (quick win -> strategic bet)
  Not stating what you're NOT building        | Deprioritization IS the tradeoff; silence reads as no thinking | Explicitly kill 1-2 solutions and say why

  The five traps map onto the 8 steps: 1<-trap1, 2<-trap2, 3<-none,
  4<-none, 5<-trap4, 7<-trap3, 8<-trap5. A 6/10 hits 2-3; a 9/10 hits none.
