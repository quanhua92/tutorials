depth_vs_width_reference.txt -- web provenance log for DEPTH_VS_WIDTH.md
Every formula, year, author, and behavioral claim below is traced to >=2 sources.
Full audit trail; the .md "## Sources" section is its public face.

================================================================================
[1] https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.14905
    Liu et al. 2024 "MobileLLM: Optimizing Sub-billion Parameter Language
    Models for On-Device Use Cases" (ICML 2024, Meta AI -- official).
    Verifies: deep-and-thin ("deep and thin architectures") is the optimal
    sub-billion profile; "embedding sharing and grouped-query attention"; an
    "immediate block-wise weight-sharing approach with no increase in model
    size and only marginal latency overhead" => MobileLLM-LS, +0.7%/0.8%
    accuracy over MobileLLM 125M/350M; the layer-sharing concept this bundle
    implements. (NOTE: the brief cited 2402.14952 -- a typo; the correct id is
    2402.14905, confirmed on the arXiv abstract page.)

[2] http://proceedings.mlr.press/v49/telgarsky16.html
    Telgarsky 2016 "benefits of depth in neural networks", COLT 2016,
    PMLR 49:1517-1539 (official).
    Verifies: the depth-expressivity gap. "For any positive integer k, there
    exist neural networks with Theta(k^3) layers, Theta(1) nodes per layer, and
    Theta(1) distinct parameters which can not be approximated by networks with
    O(k) layers unless they are exponentially large -- they must possess
    Omega(2^k) nodes." => depth gives exponential expressivity per parameter
    vs. width's polynomial; the theoretical reason deep-narrow beats
    shallow-wide at equal param count.

[3] https://arxiv.org/abs/1606.05336
    Raghu, Poole, Kleinberg, Ganguli, Sohl-Dickstein 2016/2017 "On the
    Expressive Power of Deep Neural Networks" (ICML 2017 -- official).
    Verifies: "The complexity of the computed function grows exponentially
    with depth." Independent corroboration of the Telgarsky depth-expressivity
    result (finding (1) of the abstract). Confirms depth yields exponential
    expressivity; the second source for the deep-narrow > shallow-wide claim.

[4] https://github.com/facebookresearch/mobilellm
    facebookresearch/MobileLLM (official training code, Meta AI).
    Verifies: MobileLLM-125M/350M config tables -- deep-and-thin layer counts
    (e.g. many layers at small H for 125M), embedding sharing (input embedding
    reused as the output head), and the grouped/recurrent block-wise weight
    sharing implementation that depth_vs_width.py Section C models in miniature.

[5] https://developer.nvidia.com/blog/mastering-llm-techniques-inference-optimization/
    NVIDIA Developer Blog "Mastering LLM Techniques: Inference Optimization".
    Verifies: "Model execution is frequently memory-bandwidth bound -- in
    particular, bandwidth-bound in the weights." Decoding one autoregressive
    token reads every weight once -> latency floor ~= unique params / memory
    bandwidth (the decode-traffic model in Section B/D).

[6] https://www.databricks.com/blog/llm-inference-performance-engineering-best-practices
    Databricks "LLM Inference Performance Engineering: Best Practices".
    Verifies: decode (batch-1 autoregressive generation) is memory-bandwidth
    bound; the operator intensity of the weight-matrix load dominates over
    compute for small batch / on-device inference. Second source for the
    decode-is-memory-bound claim that makes unique-param count (not depth)
    the latency floor.

[7] https://raw.githubusercontent.com/mlresearch/v235/main/assets/liu24ce/liu24ce.pdf
    Liu et al. 2024 MobileLLM (ICML proceedings PDF, mlresearch v235).
    Verifies: "Embedding Sharing. In sub-billion scale language models, the
    embedding layers constitute a significant portion of the parameter count:
    20% in a 125M model" and "by sharing the embedding, we reuse the input
    embedding weights as the output fully connected layer weights." Source for
    the Section E embedding-tax numbers (V*H eating ~20%+ of a 125M budget) and
    the MobileLLM embedding-sharing detail.

[8] https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.15556
    Hoffmann et al. 2022 "Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models"
    (Chinchilla, DeepMind -- official).
    Verifies: the compute-optimal parameter/token budget N that depth-vs-width
    trades off WITHIN. Provides the context (the "budget N" of Section A) and
    the depth/width scaling sub-analysis that motivates why sub-billion models
    are overtrained and architecture-sensitive. Cross-refs SCALING_LAWS.md.
