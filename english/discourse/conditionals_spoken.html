5Writing task

Write one of each: a real (1st), an unreal (2nd), and an unreal-past (3rd) conditional. Then reveal the model answer.

Model (one of each — check the tense shift):

1st (real): If it rains tomorrow, I'll stay home. if + present → will · likely future

2nd (unreal now): If I had more time, I'd learn the guitar. if + past → would · unreal present (past verb = unreality, not time)

3rd (unreal past): If I had known about the sale, I would have bought it. if + past perfect → would have · regret (cannot change it)

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
No tense backshift for unreality — Vietnamese nếu…thì keeps the same tense, so you say "If I will have time, I go."Drop will from the if-clause. Real → "If I have time, I'll go." Unreal → "If I had time, I'd go." The past verb marks the unreality.
Puts would in both clauses — "If I would have time, I would go."would lives only in the result clause, never in the if-clause: "If I had time, I would go."
"If I was you" instead of "If I were you" — Vietnamese has no subjunctive formUse the irrealis were in the fixed advice chunk: "If I were you, I'd…"
Avoids the third conditional — switches to plain past ("I didn't know so I didn't call")Learn one frame cold: "If I had known, I would have + past participle." It expresses regret/hindsight the plain past cannot.
"would of" for "would have" — hears /ˈwʊdəv/ and back-spells itThe sound is /ˈwʊdəv/ but the spelling is would've (= would have). Say "would have," never "would of."
Over-pronounces contractions — "I would have" in four full words, sounding stiffUse the spoken contractions: "I'd" /aɪd/, "I'll" /aɪl/, "would've" /ˈwʊdəv/. Full forms sound bookish.
Confuses the time meaning — thinks "If I had time" means the past"If I had time, I'd help" = now. The past verb is the unreality marker, not a time marker.
Misses the had in the if-clause — "If I knew, I would have called" (mixing 2nd & 3rd)Third conditional needs past perfect in the if-clause: "If I had known, I would have called."
