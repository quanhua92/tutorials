========================================================================
SECTION A - P458 Poor Pigs  (information theory, base tests+1)
========================================================================

Each pig is one DIGIT in base (tests+1). With `tests` testing
rounds, a pig either dies in round 0..tests-1 or survives all
rounds - that is tests+1 distinct observable states. So `pigs`
pigs in parallel distinguish (tests+1)^pigs buckets. Find the
smallest pigs with (tests+1)^pigs >= buckets.

The +1 in the base is the whole trick: it is the implicit
'survived every round' state.

--- LC example 1: 1 round ---
  buckets=4, minutesToDie=15, minutesToTest=15
  tests = 15 // 15 = 1   -> base = tests+1 = 2

  pigs | (tests+1)^pigs | >= buckets? | decision
  -----+---------------+------------+-------------------------
   0   | 2^0 = 1          | <  4          | too small, +1 pig
   1   | 2^1 = 2          | <  4          | too small, +1 pig
   2   | 2^2 = 4          | >= 4          | RETURN 2

  => poor_pigs(4, 15, 15) = 2

--- LC example 2: 2 rounds ---
  buckets=4, minutesToDie=15, minutesToTest=30
  tests = 30 // 15 = 2   -> base = tests+1 = 3

  pigs | (tests+1)^pigs | >= buckets? | decision
  -----+---------------+------------+-------------------------
   0   | 3^0 = 1          | <  4          | too small, +1 pig
   1   | 3^1 = 3          | <  4          | too small, +1 pig
   2   | 3^2 = 9          | >= 4          | RETURN 2

  => poor_pigs(4, 15, 30) = 2

--- stress: 1000 buckets, 4 rounds ---
  buckets=1000, minutesToDie=15, minutesToTest=60
  tests = 60 // 15 = 4   -> base = tests+1 = 5

  pigs | (tests+1)^pigs | >= buckets? | decision
  -----+---------------+------------+-------------------------
   0   | 5^0 = 1          | <  1000        | too small, +1 pig
   1   | 5^1 = 5          | <  1000        | too small, +1 pig
   2   | 5^2 = 25         | <  1000        | too small, +1 pig
   3   | 5^3 = 125        | <  1000        | too small, +1 pig
   4   | 5^4 = 625        | <  1000        | too small, +1 pig
   5   | 5^5 = 3125       | >= 1000        | RETURN 5

  => poor_pigs(1000, 15, 60) = 5

--- more canonical checks ---
  poor_pigs(1, 1, 1)   = 0    (1 bucket needs 0 pigs)
  poor_pigs(125, 1, 4) = 3    (5^3=125, base 5)
  poor_pigs(25, 1, 1)  = 5    (2^4=16<25<=2^5=32)

SECTION B - P478 Random Point in a Circle  (rejection sampling)
========================================================================

Sample uniformly inside a disk. The trap: do NOT pick a radius
uniformly in [0, r] - area grows as r^2, so a uniform radius
crowds points near the center. Instead sample a point uniformly
in the bounding SQUARE [-1,1]^2 and REJECT it if it falls
outside the unit disk (x*x + y*y > 1). Every interior point is
equally likely to be proposed, so the survivors are uniform.

Acceptance probability = area(disk) / area(square)
                      = (pi*r^2) / (2r)^2 = pi/4 ~ 0.7854
So ~21.5% of proposals are thrown away - a fine price for
correctness.

--- first 8 proposals inside the unit square (seed=42) ---
  attempt | x       | y       | x^2+y^2 | decision
  --------+---------+---------+---------+----------
     1   | -0.4953 | -0.8237 | 0.9239  | ACCEPT
     2   | +0.1546 | -0.5549 | 0.3318  | ACCEPT
     3   | -0.2487 | -0.9487 | 0.9618  | ACCEPT
     4   | -0.1054 | -0.7631 | 0.5934  | ACCEPT
     5   | +0.7476 | +0.9893 | 1.5376  | reject
     6   | +0.7064 | -0.0006 | 0.4990  | ACCEPT
     7   | +0.2840 | +0.7229 | 0.6033  | ACCEPT
     8   | +0.1929 | -0.8185 | 0.7072  | ACCEPT

--- empirical acceptance over 20000 proposals (seed=42) ---
  accepted = 15735 / 20000 = 0.7867
  theory   = pi/4 = 0.7854
  relative error = 0.17%

--- 3 accepted points for a disk of radius 2 centered at (1, 3) ---
  randPoint #1 -> (-0.0449, +4.6540)   (offset from center: (-1.0449, +1.6540), |offset|<=2)
  randPoint #2 -> (+1.4500, +4.7079)   (offset from center: (+0.4500, +1.7079), |offset|<=2)
  randPoint #3 -> (+0.4439, +1.2124)   (offset from center: (-0.5561, -1.7876), |offset|<=2)

--- why NOT to sample radius uniformly ---
  If r ~ Uniform(0,1) and theta ~ Uniform(0,2*pi): the point density
  at radius rho is proportional to rho * (1/rho) d rho... no - the
  Jacobian of (r, theta) is r, so density ~ 1/(2*pi) * 1 (constant
  in r on [0,1]) gives a 2D density ~ 1/(2*pi*r): BLOW UP at center.
  Fix if you must use polar: r = sqrt(uniform(0,1)), not uniform(0,1).

SECTION C - P479 Largest Palindrome Product  (constructive mirroring)
========================================================================

Find the largest palindrome equal to a*b with a, b both n-digit.
Do NOT scan all ~10^2n products. GENERATE palindromes largest-
first: pick the upper half `left` (from 10^n-1 downward), MIRROR
it into a 2n-digit palindrome, then ask 'does some n-digit right
divide it?', stopping once right*right < palindrome (no factor
can exist below sqrt). The first factorable palindrome wins.

n=1 is special: 9 = 3*3 (mirroring is meaningless for 1 digit).

--- n=2: scan palindromes from left=99 downward ---
  left | palindrome | right scan      | factorable?
  -----+------------+-----------------+------------
   99  | 9999       | right=99..| no (right^2 < pal before any divisor)
   98  | 9889       | right=99..| no (right^2 < pal before any divisor)
   97  | 9779       | right=99..| no (right^2 < pal before any divisor)
   96  | 9669       | right=99..| no (right^2 < pal before any divisor)
   95  | 9559       | right=99..| no (right^2 < pal before any divisor)
   94  | 9449       | right=99..| no (right^2 < pal before any divisor)
   93  | 9339       | right=99..| no (right^2 < pal before any divisor)
   92  | 9229       | right=99..| no (right^2 < pal before any divisor)
   91  | 9119       | right=99..| no (right^2 < pal before any divisor)
   90  | 9009       | right=99..| YES -> 99 x 91 *

  => largest palindrome = 9009 = 99 x 91
     9009 mod 1337 = 987   (LeetCode wants the result mod 1337)

--- full table n=1..8 ---
  n | largest palindrome = factors        | mod 1337
  --+-------------------------------------+---------
  1 | 9 = 3 x 3                            | 9
  2 | 9009 = 99 x 91                                        | 987
  3 | 906609 = 993 x 913                                    | 123
  4 | 99000099 = 9999 x 9901                                | 597
  5 | 9966006699 = 99979 x 99681                            | 677
  6 | 999000000999 = 999999 x 999001                        | 1218
  7 | 99956644665999 = 9998017 x 9997647                    | 877
  8 | 9999000000009999 = 99999999 x 99990001                | 475

--- canonical LeetCode check ---
  largest_palindrome(1) = 9    (expected 9)
  largest_palindrome(2) = 987   (expected 987)

--- correctness: every reported palindrome IS a palindrome ---
  all palindromes for n=2..8 verified palindrome OK

SECTION D - Complexity, killer gotchas, problem table
========================================================================

Complexity
----------
  Problem                                Time              Space
  -------------------------------------- ----------------- ------
  P458 Poor Pigs (base counting)         O(log buckets)    O(1)
  P478 Random Point (rejection sample)   O(1) expected     O(1)
     each point ~ 4/pi ~ 1.27 proposals
  P479 Largest Palindrome (mirroring)    O(10^n) worst     O(1)
  Closed-form / formula math generally   O(1) or O(log n)  O(1)
  Digit construction (palindrome/perm)   O(n) or O(10^n)   O(1)

Killer gotchas
--------------
  1. THE +1 IN POOR PIGS. The base is (tests+1), NOT tests. The +1
     encodes 'survived every round' - a real, observable state.
     Forgetting it under-counts buckets by a factor of (tests+1)/tests.
  2. UNIFORM RADIUS CLUSTERS AT CENTER. Sampling r ~ U(0,R) gives a
     2D density ~ 1/(2*pi*r) that blows up at the origin. Either use
     rejection sampling on the bounding square, OR polar with
     r = R*sqrt(U(0,1)). NEVER r = R*U(0,1).
  3. n=1 SPECIAL CASE FOR PALINDROMES. Mirroring a 1-digit prefix is
     meaningless; the answer for P479 n=1 is 9 (=3*3) directly. A
     general mirror loop would skip or miscompute it.
 4. STOP THE FACTOR SCAN AT sqrt. When checking 'does n-digit right
     divide palindrome?', stop once right*right < palindrome: no
     divisor smaller than sqrt can pair with one <= sqrt. This prunes
     the inner loop from O(10^n) toward O(sqrt(palindrome)).
  5. OVERFLOW / MOD. Python handles bignums, but P479 demands the
     answer mod 1337. Apply the mod ONLY at the return, never inside
     the factor test (you must test the real palindrome for divisibility).
  6. FORMULA EDGE CASES. Math formulas break at n=0, n=1, single
     digits, and exact powers of 10. Always test those boundaries.
