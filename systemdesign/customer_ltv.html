Customer LTV — interactive 📖 CUSTOMER_LTV.md 📄 customer_ltv.py

📖 Read the full guide — architecture diagrams, decision tables, killer gotchas, cheat sheet. This page is the interactive companion.
A customer is worth the discounted sum of every future payment, each weighted by the probability they're still alive to make it. Draw a Weibull survival curve and watch D1/D7/D30/D90 retention fall out, feed it into a CLV calculator (historical vs discounted NPV), eyeball the cohort heatmap, score churn with a hand-trained logistic model, sweep the discount rate, then segment into Whale/Dolphin/Minnow — every number matches customer_ltv.py exactly.

① Retention curve plotter — Weibull survival S(t) = exp(−(t/λ)^k)

What to do: drag λ (scale, days) and k (shape). k<1 = hazard decreasing (sticky customers). The curve is the fraction still active at day t — the weight on every future cash flow. Markers show D1/D7/D30/D90. Try the cohort presets (they also feed the CLV calculator below).

② CLV calculator — historical (undiscounted) vs predictive (discounted NPV)

What to do: the survival curve from ① (current λ/k) feeds both CLV formulas. Historical = ARPU×gm×E[lifespan] (undiscounted, 120mo). Predictive = Σ ARPU×gm×S(30m)/(1+r)m (the production bid value). Drag the discount rate and horizon to feel the gap (customer_ltv.py Sections 3-4).

③ Cohort heatmap — retention by cohort × day

What to do: each cell is S(t) for a cohort (greener = higher retention). Premium's flat row is why its CLV lives far in the future — and why it's the most discount-sensitive (customer_ltv.py Section 1).

④ Churn score calculator — 6 features → logistic-regression P(churn)

What to do: churn scoring is a rank, not a dollar. Each feature is normalized (capped 1.0), then fed to a logistic regression trained by batch gradient descent (lr=0.5, 2000 epochs) — the same model in customer_ltv.py Section 2. Try the presets.

⑤ Discount-rate sensitivity — NPV vs annual rate

What to do: pick a cohort, watch CLV fall as the discount rate rises. At r=0 NPV = undiscounted sum. The discount rate is a lever: overestimating it undervalues loyal customers and underbids (customer_ltv.py Section 5).

⑥ Segmentation — Whale / Dolphin / Minnow by LTV:CAC

What to do: 1M customers scored by predictive CLV, tiered by LTV:CAC (≥3 Whale, ≥1 Dolphin, <1 Minnow). Whales are bid on aggressively; minnows lose money to acquire (customer_ltv.py Section 6).
