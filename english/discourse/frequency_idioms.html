6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Translates idioms literally — "một miếng bánh" for a piece of cake, "trên cùng một trang" for on the same pageTreat the idiom as one inseparable chunk. Learn the figurative meaning whole; literal translation is nonsense by design.
Maps thành ngữ onto English idioms — expects a 1:1 Vietnamese idiom for eachMost don't map. Learn each English idiom on its own terms, not via an L1 gloss.
Over-studies rare/dramatic idioms — raining cats and dogs, kick the bucketFrequency-first: only top-band idioms. Spend the rest of your time on phrasal verbs + collocations.
Wrong register — break a leg to wish a surgeon luck; a piece of cake in a formal reportMatch the idiom to the setting. a piece of cake / break a leg = casual; on the same page = professional. Or use plain words.
Breaks the fixed form — "a cake of piece", "on same page" (drops the)Idioms are frozen: keep exact words, order, articles, prepositions. on the same page, not on same page.
Drops the final consonant — "hit the nail on the hea", "a piece of ca"Release every final. 8 of 10 end in a cluster VN has no slot for (cake /keɪk/, court /kɔːrt/, hand /hænd/).
Forces idioms into every sentence to sound "advanced"Idioms are seasoning, not the meal. One well-placed idiom per few turns sounds native; three in a row sounds like a phrasebook.
Misses the stress — equal weight on every word (on THE same PAGE)Stress content words: on the SAME PAGE, a PIECE of CAKE, once in a BLUE MOON.
