5Writing task

Rewrite a flat sentence as a cleft for emphasis (It was… that… / What I want is…). Then reveal the model answer.

Flat original: "I sent the report yesterday." — rewrite it to foreground who sent it, then to foreground when.

Model (foreground the focus element):

It was me who sent the report yesterday. — it-cleft, foregrounds the person (stress me)

It was yesterday that I sent the report. — it-cleft, foregrounds the time (stress yesterday)

All I needed was to hit the deadline. — wh-cleft, foregrounds "the only thing"

What mattered was the deadline, and I met it. — wh-cleft, foregrounds the outcome

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
No cleft construction → flat, equal emphasis: "John called yesterday." (no element pops)Use an it-cleft: "It was John who called yesterday." Stress the focus element.
Emphasis = louder/longer only; you shout the whole sentenceEmphasis in English is structural. A cleft moves the focus to a dedicated slot; only that element gets stress, the rest de-stresses.
Topic-comment default ("John thì…") → flat English word orderMap the thì/chính emphasis onto a cleft: "chính John gọi" → "It was John who called."
Inserts an extra verb → "It was happened that…" / "It is John is the one…"Drop the second copula. Frame = It + be + [focus] + that/who… — one be, then the relative clause.
Drops that/who and loses the thread → "It was John called"Keep that (or who for people) until the frame is automatic. The who/that is the hinge that marks the split.
Wrong relative / doubled subject → "It was John that he called"For a person use who (always safe); that works for anything. Never insert a second subject.
No stress contrast → stresses It was instead of the focus → the point vanishesThe focus element takes primary stress. "It was JOHN who called" — hit John, glide through It was who.
Avoids clefts entirely (stays at "I think… / I want…")Clefts are spoken, not formal (Cambridge: used "especially in speaking"). Deploy them for emphasis/correction.
