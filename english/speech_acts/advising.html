5Writing task

Write one advice email line starting with You might want to… or If I were you, I'd…. Then reveal the model.

Model (same advice, four rungs up the ladder):

Soft: You might want to send the deck the night before. /juː maɪt ˈwɒnt tə/ — gentlest, no pressure

Soft (question): Have you tried sending it the night before? /həv juː traɪd/ — advice as curiosity

Question: Why don't you send it the night before? /waɪ ˈdəʊnt juː/ — collaborative

Strong: If I were you, I'd send it the night before. /ɪf aɪ wɜː juː aɪd/ — committed, kind

Notice: You should send it… is one rung above all four — save it for authority or urgency.

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Transfers nên ("should") as You should… in every register — sounds like a lecture or an orderSwap should for a conditional: I'd suggest… / If I were you, I'd…. Reserve You should… for genuine authority or urgency.
Uses the bare imperative ("đi khám đi" = "go see the doctor") — caring in VN, an order in EnglishSoften with a modal or question: You might want to see a doctor. / Have you seen a doctor?
Gives unsolicited advice — the L1 khuyên caring reflex; offers You should… the moment a problem is mentionedPreflight first: Do you want a suggestion? If yes → soft rung. If no → empathy only.
Skips the empathy step — goes straight from "I hear your problem" to You should… (cold/fixer energy)Two-beat pause: empathy first (That sounds really tough) → then advice on a soft rung.
Uses should for high-stakes advice (quitting a job) — too blunt for the weightStep up to If I were you, I'd… — commits you (I would) while keeping their ownership (in your shoes).
Says If I was you (regular past) instead of If I were youUse were for the second conditional in advice. Was is casual speech; were is the prescribed form (Cambridge Grammar).
Drops the -ing after worth → "It might be worth try"worth takes the -ing form: It might be worth trying. (Oxford worth sense 2.)
Uses to after suggest/recommend → "I suggest to go"suggest/recommend take -ing or a that-clause, never to: I'd suggest going. / I'd suggest that we go.
Drops finals on the advice → "You migh' want to", "I'd sugges'", "Why don' you"Release every final: the /t/ in might, the /st/ in don't, the /d/ in I'd carry the chunk's meaning.
Vietnamese /θ/ → /t/ in worth /wɜːθ/ → "wut"worth ends in /θ/ — tongue between teeth. The /t/-substitution flips it toward word/wart.
Stresses re-COM-mend on the wrong syllablerecommend is stressed on the final syllable: /ˌrekəˈmend*/ (Oxford). Wrong stress is more audible than wrong sounds.
Uses advise where suggest/recommend fits — advise claims authority and "may cause offence"Default to suggest (helpful) or recommend (mild expertise). Reserve advise for genuine professional authority.
