## A. Acknowledge-then-counter (validate before "but")

The professional-English engine of diplomatic disagreement: name the other
person's point first (positive-face work), *then* introduce the counter with a
soft connector (`however` / `but` / `actually`). Cambridge records the core idiom
`take someone's point` (= "I understand that what you are saying is important")
and the standalone `point taken`; the `I see/hear your point` family is the
same pragmatic move. These are **face-saving** openers — they do not concede
agreement, they only concede hearing.

| I take your point, but… | I accept what you say is valid (then counter) | /aɪ ˈteɪk jɔː ˈpɔɪnt bʌt/ UK · /aɪ ˈteɪk jɔːr ˈpɔɪnt bʌt/ US | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/point | (phrase; "take someone's point" in CALD) | US/UK |

## B. Tentative / indirect counter — frame the disagreement as a question

The "soft power" set: turn the counter into a **hedged question** so the
disagreement lands as a collaborative probe, not an attack. This is negative-face
politeness (Brown & Levinson 1987) — the question form gives the hearer an exit,
treating the counter as a shared investigation. `I wonder if…` is Oxford's
"polite way of asking / suggesting"; `Might it be worth…?` and `Have we
considered…?` recast the objection as an option the group can adopt without
losing face.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| I wonder if we could… | A tentative, indirect counter framed as a polite suggestion | /aɪ ˈwʌndə(r) ɪf wi kʊd/ UK · /aɪ ˈwʌndər ɪf wi kʊd/ US | https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/wonder_1 | (phrase; "wonder" = polite ask/suggest, OALD) | US/UK |
| Might it be worth considering…? | A very hedged counter: maybe we should think about X | /maɪt ɪt bi ˈwɜːθ kənˈsɪdərɪŋ/ UK · /maɪt ɪt bi ˈwɜːrθ kənˈsɪdərɪŋ/ US | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/consider | (phrase; "worth doing" + "consider", CALD) | US/UK |
| Have we considered…? | A counter shaped as an inclusive group question | /hæv wi kənˈsɪdəd/ UK · /hæv wi kənˈsɪdərd/ US | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/consider | (phrase, present-perfect question, CALD) | US/UK |

> **Verification note:** Oxford Learner's `wonder` (verb) documents *"I wonder
> if…" / "I was wondering whether…"* as the polite-request/suggestion pattern;
> Cambridge's `consider` gives /kənˈsɪdə(r)/ and the `worth + -ing` collocation
> (`it's worth considering`) is recorded across Cambridge + Oxford. `might` as a
> polite modal (`Might I…?` / `Might it be…?`) is in both CALD and OALD. YouGlish
> returns real clips for `I wonder if we could`, `Have we considered`, and
> `Might it be worth considering` (HTTP 200, verified 2026-06-23).

## C. Supporting lexis (the connective tissue of a diplomatic counter)

The function words that carry the "softening": `actually` (down-tones a blunt
"No"), `worth` (frames an option as valuable, not imposed), `take` (a noun =
"viewpoint"). Vietnamese learners under-use all three.

| take | a particular view or interpretation of something (n.) | /teɪk/ | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/take_2 | ≈#90 (of *take*) | US/UK |
| worth | having a specified value; good enough to justify (adj.) | /wɜːθ/ UK · /wɜːrθ/ US | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/worth_1 | ≈#500 (of *worth*) | US/UK |
| point taken | I accept what you say (concession — can precede a counter) | /ˈpɔɪnt ˈteɪkən/ | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/point-taken | (phrase, CALD idiom) | US/UK |
