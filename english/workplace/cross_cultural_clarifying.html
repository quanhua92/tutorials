5Writing task

Write a polite clarification line. Use "Just to make sure I understood…" / "Do you mean…?". Then reveal the model answer.

Model (polite clarification — hedge first, then the confirm):

Just to make sure I understood — do you mean we should ship the sign-up step first, before the rest of the flow? /dʒʌst tə meɪk ʃʊr aɪ ˌʌndərˈstʊd/ — the diplomatic confirm-back frame + yes/no check

Sorry, I didn't quite catch that. Could you say that again? /aɪ ˈdɪdnt kwaɪt kætʃ ðæt/ — apology + the *quite* hedge + repeat request

I'm not familiar with that term. Could you spell that, please? /aɪm nɑːt fəˈmɪl.i.jɚ wɪθ ðæt tɜːrm/ — admit unfamiliarity → ask to spell

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Smiles and nods to save face (thể diện) when you didn't understand an accentIn ELF, explicit clarification is the norm. Use Sorry, could you say that again? Faking understanding causes costly miscommunication.
Internalizes "I should understand everything" → shame; blames self, not the accent/lineEven native speakers ask for repetition in ELF calls. Could you slow down a bit? signals professionalism, not weakness.
Says a bare "What?" — the Vietnamese "Cái gì?" instinct, which sounds impatient/rudeReplace with "Sorry?" (rising) or Sorry, could you say that again? The sorry + could licenses it politely.
Stays silent instead of interrupting a senior — interrupting feels disrespectfulFrame it as a question: Sorry, could you…? The apology-softener makes a mid-sentence clarification courteous.
Drops the hedging quite / drops final /tʃ/ on catch → "I didn't catch dat"Keep the quite (it shares the blame) and release the cluster: catch /kætʃ/, not "ca".
Goes imperative — "Slow down" / "Repeat" — sounds bossy in EnglishAlways frame as a modal question: Could you slow down a bit? The could you is negative politeness.
Mispronounces familiar → wrong stress or flattens /fəˈmɪl.i.jɚ/ → /feˈmilija/Stress the second syllable: fa-MIL-i-ar /fəˈmɪl.i.jɚ/. Drill the /ljɚ/ cluster slow.
Fears "I'm not familiar with that term" loses face → pretends, then guesses wrongUse the admit → ask → read-back loop: I'm not familiar with that term. Could you spell that, please? Let me read that back to you.
