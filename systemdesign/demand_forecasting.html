Demand Forecasting — interactive DEMAND_FORECASTING.md demand_forecasting.py

A demand series is trend + seasonality + residual. Decompose it, forecast each layer, reassemble. Here you decompose a synthetic series, sweep the Holt smoothing parameters and watch the forecast, compare accuracy metrics (MAE/RMSE/MAPE/WMAPE) against the seasonal-naive baseline, scrub the moving-average window and seasonal indices, then walk the architecture — every number matches demand_forecasting.py exactly.

① Time-series decomposition plotter — trend + seasonality + residual

What to do: drag the data-generating parameters. The series is rebuilt as y(t) = (100 + slope·t) + amp·seasonal[dow] + noise·residual[t], then classical additive decomposition recovers the three layers: trend = centered MA(7), seasonal = detrended averaged per day-of-week, residual = y − trend − seasonal (demand_forecasting.py Section 2).

② Forecast explorer — Holt’s linear smoothing (level + trend)

What to do: train on the first 7 weeks, forecast the held-out last week. Drag alpha (level weight) and beta (trend weight). Note how Holt knows nothing about weekly seasonality — when the window ends on a Sunday trough the trend estimate goes negative and it forecasts a declining line (demand_forecasting.py Section 5).

③ Accuracy metrics calculator — MAE, RMSE, MAPE, WMAPE

What to do: every real model must beat the seasonal-naive baseline (y(t−7) = last week’s same day). Pick a horizon and compare four metrics across Holt, seasonal-naive, and random-walk. WMAPE is the production metric (volume-weighted, robust to zero demand); MAPE explodes near y=0 (demand_forecasting.py Section 6).

④ Moving average smoother — window vs lag

What to do: a trailing SMA(n) smooths noise but lags. A window of 7 = one full week, so the seasonal swing cancels and SMA(7) tracks the trend. Larger n → smoother but laggier (demand_forecasting.py Section 3).
—

⑤ Seasonal index inspector — deseasonalize to expose the trend

What to do: the recovered day-of-week indices quantify each slot’s typical swing (sum = 0 for an additive model). Deseasonalizing (y − indexdow) strips the weekly shape so a trend model sees a clean signal — the core of SARIMA / Prophet / Holt-Winters (demand_forecasting.py Section 4).
