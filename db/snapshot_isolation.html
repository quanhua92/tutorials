① The frozen snapshot — drag T2's start; its read flips at T1's commit

What to do: T1 writes R=100 and commits at t=30. Drag T2 start. While T2 starts before t=30, its snapshot is blind to T1 (it reads R=0). Once T2 starts at or after t=30, T1 is in its snapshot (it reads R=100). This is the visibility rule Tj sees Ti ⇔ Ti.commit < Tj.start — the engine behind every SI guarantee.

② Write skew — two doctors; toggle SSI to close the gap

What to do: both doctors are on call; rule = ≥1 on call. T1 reads count=2 and sets Alice off; T2 reads count=2 and sets Bob off. Under SI, disjoint writes slip past first-committer-wins → both commit → count 0 → rule violated. Flip to SSI: SSI records the predicate reads, sees the bidirectional rw-conflict, and aborts T2 → Bob stays on call → rule holds.
