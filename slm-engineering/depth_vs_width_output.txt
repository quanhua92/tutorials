========================================================================
  SECTION B: decode traffic -- UNIQUE params read per token (memory-bandwidth bound)
========================================================================
Decoding ONE autoregressive token streams EVERY unique weight from
DRAM once -> latency floor ~= (unique params) / (memory bandwidth).
Effective DEPTH does NOT change the weight-read term: the same block,
applied K times, is read K times but UNIQUE weights are counted once
per block. What depth DOES add: KV-cache memory (one K,V per layer)
and a little attention compute (re-scored over more layers).

H = 512, per-layer 12*H^2 = 3,145,728 params.

| config                 | unique layers | unique params | effective depth | weight-read/token | KV-cache (rel) |
|------------------------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|----------------|
| deep-narrow (no share) |            40 |   125,829,120 |              40 |       125,829,120 |             40 |
| layer-shared K=2       |            20 |    62,914,560 |              40 |        62,914,560 |             40 |
| layer-shared K=4       |            10 |    31,457,280 |              40 |        31,457,280 |             40 |
| layer-shared K=8       |             5 |    15,728,640 |              40 |        15,728,640 |             40 |

  deep-narrow (no share): effective depth 40 (== 40? True), weight-read 125.83M  ->  1.0x less decode traffic than no-share.
  layer-shared K=2      : effective depth 40 (== 40? True), weight-read  62.91M  ->  2.0x less decode traffic than no-share.
  layer-shared K=4      : effective depth 40 (== 40? True), weight-read  31.46M  ->  4.0x less decode traffic than no-share.
  layer-shared K=8      : effective depth 40 (== 40? True), weight-read  15.73M  ->  8.0x less decode traffic than no-share.

  ==> All four keep effective depth 40 (quality), but UNIQUE params
      drop with the share factor K -- so decode latency drops with K
      too. That is the edge win: depth-quality at shallow latency.

========================================================================
  SECTION E: the embedding tax V*H -- subtracted before counting layers
========================================================================
Embeddings are NOT layers: V*H params sit in the token table and steal
capacity from the stack. Layer count L = (N_total - V*H) / (12*H^2).

| V (vocab) | H   | embed tax V*H | layers L @ 125M (post-tax) | tax % of 125M |
|-----------|-----|---------------|----------------------------|---------------|
|     49152 | 512 |    25,165,824 |                    31.7364 |         20.1% |
|     49152 | 1024 |    50,331,648 |                     5.9341 |         40.3% |
|    128000 | 512 |    65,536,000 |                    18.9031 |         52.4% |
|    128000 | 1024 |   131,072,000 |                    -0.4826 |        104.9% |

  Read the table: at H=1024 a 128k vocab eats 1.07x the ENTIRE 125M
  budget as embeddings -- L goes negative (no layers left!). This is
  WHY MobileLLM reuses input embeddings as the output head (embedding
  sharing) and picks a modest V. See 🔗 SHARED_EMBEDDINGS.md and
  🔗 VOCAB_RATIONALIZATION.md for the full embedding-budget story.
  [check] post-tax depth < pre-tax depth at any V>0 (the tax steals layers): OK
  [check] H=1024, V=128k: embedding tax exceeds 100% of 125M: OK
