  SECTION A: frequency vector  theta_j = base^(-j / (D/2))
========================================================================
D = 8,  D/2 = 4,  base = 10000

| j  | inner = j/(D/2) | theta_j = base^(-inner) | meaning           |
|----|----------------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| 0  | 0.0000         | 1.000000                 | FAST rotation (local position) |
| 1  | 0.2500         | 0.100000                 | FAST rotation (local position) |
| 2  | 0.5000         | 0.010000                 | SLOW rotation (long-range position) |
| 3  | 0.7500         | 0.001000                 | SLOW rotation (long-range position) |

Key: small j -> theta near 1 -> rotates a LOT per step (high freq)
     large j -> theta near 0 -> barely rotates per step (low freq)

========================================================================
  SECTION H: WHY RoPE encodes RELATIVE position (proof by numbers)
========================================================================
Fixed raw Q and K (one head, D=8). We place Q at position m_q and
K at position m_k, then take the dot product Q·K (the attention
score numerator). The claim: the score depends ONLY on (m_q - m_k).

| m_q | m_k | RELATIVE = m_q - m_k |   Q·K score  |
|-----|-----|--------------------|--------------|
| 2   | 1   | 1                  | +0.514498 |
| 5   | 4   | 1                  | +0.514498 |
| 10  | 9   | 1                  | +0.514499 |
| 2   | 0   | 2                  | +0.285792 |
| 5   | 3   | 2                  | +0.285792 |
| 10  | 8   | 2                  | +0.285792 |

Observe: all three pairs with relative distance = 1 give the SAME score:
  (2,1)=+0.514498  (5,4)=+0.514498  (10,9)=+0.514499
Likewise relative distance = 2 all match:
  (2,0)=+0.285792  (5,3)=+0.285792  (10,8)=+0.285792

This is the magic: the rotation angle that survives the dot product
is (m_q - m_k)*theta_j, so absolute positions cancel out.
