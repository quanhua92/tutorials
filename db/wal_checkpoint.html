② Buffer pool & disk — crash it, then recover with ARIES

What to do: hit 💥 CRASH (the buffer pool is lost; only disk + WAL survive), then step through the three recovery phases. REDO re-applies every logged change (idempotent); UNDO rolls back the loser T2 by walking its prev_lsn chain and appending CLRs.
After UNDO the database is {1:'A'} only — T1's committed insert kept (redo), T3's committed delete honored, T2's uncommitted insert + update reversed (undo). That is the ARIES correctness contract. (WAL_CHECKPOINT.md §4)

③ Checkpoint frequency — fast recovery vs low overhead (the dial)

What to do: drag the checkpoint interval. Shorter = fast recovery but high I/O overhead; longer = the reverse. The vertical marker is PostgreSQL's checkpoint_timeout = 300s default. The two curves cross at the sweet spot.
WAL rate 1,000 rec/s · replay 5,000 rec/s · flush cost 0.5s (matches wal_checkpoint.py Section E)
Fuzzy checkpoint: a background writer flushes continuously, so the redo point becomes min(recLSN in DPT) instead of the checkpoint LSN — no stall, slightly more redo. Postgres/InnoDB/DB2 all do this. (WAL_CHECKPOINT.md §5)
