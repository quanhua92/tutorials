Write 3 CV bullets — each = action verb + task + quantified result. Then reveal the model.

Model — action verb + task + quantified result:

Spearheaded a cross-team migration to a new CI pipeline, reducing build times by 40%. /ˈspɪəhedɪd/ — verb + task + %-result

Built a customer-feedback dashboard from scratch, serving 12 product teams. /bɪlt/ — verb + task + scale

Optimized the checkout flow, resulting in a 15% lift in conversion. /ˈɒptɪmaɪzd/–/ˈɑːptɪmaɪzd/ — verb + task + pinned connector + %-result

Formula (MIT PAR): "Start each bullet with a strong action verb, followed by the PROJECT, then the RESULT." — capd.mit.edu

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Duty list, not achievement list — "responsible for marketing"Flip every duty to verb + task + number: "Led 5 campaigns, increasing engagement 30%."
No quantification — omits numbers out of modesty ("khiêm tốn")Add a metric to every bullet: %, $, count, scale. Recruiters scan for the number first.
Passive voice — "was responsible for", "duties included"Lead with an active past-tense verb: Spearheaded, Built, Optimized.
Weak / hedged verbs — "helped with", "assisted in"Upgrade to a verb that owns the action: Led, Drove, Delivered.
No tense marking → past/present mixed under one jobEnforce: past job = past tense (Led), current job = present (Manage). Never mix under one heading.
Drops the -ed even in writing → "Manage a team" under a past jobWrite Managed, Improved, Optimized. The -ed carries the tense.
Translates "chịu trách nhiệm" as "responsible for" and stopsRewrite as the achievement: what you did + the result. "Owned X" beats "responsible for X".
Round-number vagueness — "increased sales a lot"Quantify precisely: "by 30%", "serving 12,000 users", "saving $20k/year".
