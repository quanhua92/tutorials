① The pipeline: topic + persona + style → a synthetic textbook section

How to use: a Cosmopedia prompt has three parts — the topic (a seed/cluster label), the persona (audience/voice), and the style (format). Pick all three and watch the deterministic template expand a dry topic into a structured mock textbook. The persona's adjective pool is what drives vocabulary density — switch from baseline_bot (4 words) to patient_professor (8 words) and watch the TTR climb.

② Why the persona matters: TTR across voices (topic = your pick)

The headline invariant: a richer persona (bigger adjective pool) yields a measurably higher type-token ratio. baseline_bot cycles its 4 adjectives and repeats; patient_professor spreads 8 distinct adjectives and rarely repeats. This is the cheapest possible quality signal for a synthetic corpus — if your "college" persona doesn't beat your "terse bot" baseline on vocabulary density, your prompt is broken.
