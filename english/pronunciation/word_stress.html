6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Vietnamese has no word stress — meaning rides on lexical tone, so you flatten every syllable to equal weightPick one syllable and make it longer + louder + higher; let the unstressed syllable shrink.
Keeps both vowels "full" — no schwa, so record sounds like "RE-CORD" with two clear vowelsLet the unstressed vowel collapse to /ə/ or /ɪ/: REcord /ˈrek.əd/, reCORD /rɪˈkɔːd/ (the /rɪ/ is tiny).
Frozen first-syllable habit — uses noun stress on a verb: "I'll REcord it"Read the grammar first. Noun/adj → front, verb → back. Decide stress from part of speech, not memory.
Wrong stress = wrong word — flattens DEsert / deSERT into one formDrill the minimal pair as opposites: DEsert (sandbox) vs deSSERT (cake). Two different words to a native.
Tone carries over — you rise/fall on the wrong syllable (pitch like a tone) and natives hear odd intonationStress is pitch plus length plus loudness. Also stretch the stressed vowel in time.
Adds a full vowel to keep syllables even: "RE-cor-duh"English is stress-timed: the unstressed syllable should be smaller and faster. Shorten it; no trailing vowel.
Carries Vietnamese level pitch onto both syllables → "RE - CORD" as two equal monosyllablesExaggerate the length ratio first: make the stressed syllable twice as long, then relax to natural.
Doesn't reduce function syllables — about /æˈbaʊt/ instead of /əˈbaʊt/Reduce the unstressed vowel to schwa: aBOUT /əˈbaʊt/, toDAY /təˈdeɪ/. 🔗 Reductions + Sentence Stress.
