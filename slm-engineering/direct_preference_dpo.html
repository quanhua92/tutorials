📖 Read the full guide — the RLHF → DPO lineage, the eq.7 loss, β pitfalls, the cheat sheet. This page is the interactive companion.
The implicit-reward idea: the policy DEFINES a reward relative to a frozen reference, r̂ = β·(logπ − logπ_ref). DPO's loss just asks the chosen completion to win that relative race: L = −log σ(r̂_chosen − r̂_rejected). Drag the five sliders and watch the reward-difference bars and the loss react. Every number is recomputed in JS from the identical formula in direct_preference_dpo.py.  |  compare with KTO (unpaired) →

① The DPO loss calculator: drag the 4 logπ sliders + β → watch the margin and loss react

How to read: the four logπ sliders are the policy's and the frozen reference's log-probabilities of the chosen and rejected completions. The implicit reward of each completion is r̂ = β·(logπ − logπ_ref) — positive when the policy likes it MORE than the reference did, negative when it likes it less. The loss fires when the chosen's reward is NOT clearly above the rejected's. (Sliders are pinned to the GOLD anchor at the bottom.)
r̂ chosen (implicit reward of chosen) r̂ rejected (implicit reward of rejected) margin z = r̂_c − r̂_l (the sigmoid's input) zero line (policy == reference)
What to do: push logπ chosen up and logπ rejected down → the teal margin bar grows positive → the loss drops below log 2 ≈ 0.6931. Reverse them and the margin goes negative → the loss blows up (the gradient will fire hard to fix it). At β→0 the margin collapses and the loss returns to log 2 (the reference is ignored).

② One DPO training loop: step the slider → watch chosen go UP, rejected DOWN (relative to the frozen ref)

How to read: the policy STARTS at the reference (grey bars), so the margin is 0 and the loss is log 2. Each gradient-descent step (lr=1.0, β=0.1) pushes logπ chosen UP and logπ rejected DOWN, while the reference (grey) never moves. The margin grows monotonically; the loss falls. This is the DPO update in miniature — a relative re-ranking anchored to the SFT checkpoint.
policy logπ_c/logπ_l move; reference (−2.5, −2.2) is FROZEN
reference logπ (FROZEN — never moves) policy logπ chosen (goes UP each step) policy logπ rejected (goes DOWN each step)
What to do: drag the step slider 0 → 5. Notice the chosen bar slides RIGHT (higher log-prob = the policy likes it more) and the rejected bar slides LEFT, while the grey reference bars stay exactly where they started. That gap IS the alignment signal.

③ β controls the signal strength AND the KL budget (the sweep, fixed quadruple)

How to read: same GOLD quadruple as Panel 1, but β varies. The log-ratio difference is FIXED at +1.30, so the margin z = β·1.30 scales linearly with β. Small β → margin ≈ 0 → loss ≈ log 2 (weak signal, reference ignored). Large β → strong signal but big drift from πref. The highlighted row is the DPO default β=0.1.
