① The prospect-theory value function — asymmetric S-curve (losses loom larger by λ)

How to read: the value function is concave in gains (diminishing sensitivity) and steeper in losses by factor λ (loss aversion). The canonical human λ ≈ 2.25 (Tversky & Kahneman 1992). Drag λ and watch the loss (red) branch steepen relative to the gain (green) branch. KTO replaces the unstable power with a logistic sigmoid that has the same shape — both curves are drawn.

③ The two branches — same r, opposite demand (the sign-flip)

How to read: for the SAME relative reward r, the desirable and undesirable branches demand opposite behaviour. r=+0.5 → loss 0.38 on a 👍 (good) but 0.62 on a 👎 (bad). That sign-flip inside the sigmoid is the whole mechanism. Numbers are β=1, λ=1, z₀=0 (Section B of the .py).
