A HashMap (O(1) lookup) and a doubly linked list (O(1) recency) point at each other, so get/put are both O(1). The list runs MRU (head) ↔ LRU (tail); a hit moves a node to the head, an overflow cuts the tail. ① Access-sequence trace — the DLL reorders, the tail gets evicted

What to do: press step / play to feed [1,2,3,4,1,2,5,1] one access at a time. A hit lifts its node to head (MRU); a miss inserts at head and, once size > cap, evicts the tail (LRU). The evicted key flies off the right end. Reset to replay.

② get(key) — O(1) HashMap lookup + move-to-head on a hit

What to do: pick a key to get. A hit runs map.get then 6 pointer writes (_remove + _add_head) to lift the node to MRU. A miss returns −1 and leaves the list untouched. The arrow traces the move.

③ put(key,value) — insert at head; evict tail when size > cap

What to do: drag capacity and press put to insert fresh keys 1,2,3,4,5,... When size > cap, the tail (LRU) is cut to make room. Re-put an existing key instead updates + refreshes it (no eviction). Watch the eviction log grow.

④ LRU vs FIFO vs LFU vs Random (and OPT) — hit rate by workload

What to do: pick a workload + capacity. W1 CYCLIC thrashes (no locality); W2 UNIFORM has no locality (all policies cluster near cap/8); W3 LOCALITY has a hot set that fits (LRU & LFU pull ahead). OPT (Belady) is the unimplementable ceiling. Bars are recomputed live in JS.

LRU exploits temporal locality; FIFO is blind to re-access; LFU wins on stable hot sets but goes stale when the working set shifts; Random is ~free and within a few % of LRU when locality is weak; OPT (evict farthest-future-use) is the benchmark ceiling.
