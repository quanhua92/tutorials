Spot Grid — park vehicles, watch spots fill

The lot facade presents candidate spots; each spot's park() wins or loses its own per-spot lock. Pick a vehicle type and park — the grid finds the first compatible free spot (spot-upsize: a Car may take a Large). Open tickets can exit and free their spot.

Fee Calculator — Strategy: Hourly · Daily cap · Premium

Three strategies behind one FeeStrategy interface. Hourly is rate×hours; Daily composes Hourly and floors it at a per-vehicle-type cap; Premium multiplies Hourly. Switching the rule is a class swap at the exit gate — no if/else.

Inputs

3.0h
Rates/hr: Motorcycle 1.0 · Car 2.0 · Truck 3.0. Daily caps: Motorcycle 8 · Car 15 · Truck 25. Premium multiplier 1.5.

Fees (rate × hours)

Hourly — 2.0/h × 3.0$6.00
Daily — min(hourly, cap 15)$6.00
Premium — hourly × 1.5$9.00

Compatibility Matrix — can_fit() lives on the spot

Each ParkingSpot subclass owns its can_fit() rule — the vehicle hierarchy knows nothing about spots. This is what makes spot-upsize a property of the spot, not the vehicle. Click a row/column cell to highlight.

Vehicle × Spot type

yes = the spot's can_fit() returns true. Note how Large accepts everything (last-resort upsize target) and Motorcycle is motorcycle-only.
