5Writing task

Write one polite interruption line + one 'hold the floor' line. Match the marker + verb chunk. Then reveal the model answer.

Model (marker + verb as one chunk, right register for the room):

Interrupt (formal meeting): Could I add something? /kʊd aɪ æd ˈsʌmθɪŋ/ — marker "Could I" + verb "add"

Interrupt (casual): Mind if I jump in? /maɪnd ɪf aɪ dʒʌmp ɪn/ — marker "Mind if" + phrasal verb "jump in"

Hold the floor (soft): If I could just finish… /ɪf aɪ kʊd dʒʌst ˈfɪnɪʃ/ — "just" does the softening

Hold the floor (direct): Let me finish my point. /let miː ˈfɪnɪʃ maɪ pɔɪnt/ — direct, used when you really need the point out

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Stays silent when you should speak — deference to elder/status means you wait for the senior to finishDrill the marker + verb opener as one chunk ("Sorry to interrupt…") and fire it within 2 seconds. Silence reads as disengaged, not respect.
Interrupts bluntly with no marker — "I want to say", "Stop, I talk"Always prepend a marker: Sorry to… / Could I… / Mind if I…. The marker is non-optional in English.
Freezes when interrupted — senior starts talking, deference makes you yield, your point is lostDrill "If I could just finish…" as a muscle reflex. A brief polite floor-hold is expected, not rude.
Translates L1 hierarchy over-literally — "Excuse me, can I speak?" / "Please permit me to say…"Match the room. With peers: Mind if I jump in? With senior clients: Could I add something?
Drops the final /t/ on interrupt → "innerrup"Hold the tongue on the /t/ ridge and release audibly. 🔗 See FINAL CONSONANTS.
/θ/ → /t/ in thought → "I want to finish dis tot"Tongue-between-teeth for /θ/. 🔗 See TH SOUNDS.
Stresses the wrong syllable in interrupt → "IN-ter-rupt"Stress the last syllable: /ˌɪn·tə·ˈrʌpt/. Touch the table on the stressed beat.
Translates "làm ơn" too heavily → "If you please, allow me to interrupt"Use just as the softener instead: If I could just finish…. "Just" is the English politeness particle.
Skips the discourse marker on resume → "So, I talk about the budget…"Always resume with "As I was saying…" — it signals "I'm reclaiming my turn", not starting a new topic.
Misreads a 1-second pause as a yield — stops mid-pointA pause is not an invitation to stop. Watch for the breath-in / hand-raise cue as the real turn-yield signal; keep going until someone takes the floor with a marker.
