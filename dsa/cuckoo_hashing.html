Cuckoo Hashing — interactive CUCKOO_HASHING.md cuckoo_hashing.py

Each key has exactly two nests: T1[h1(k)] and T2[h2(k)]. A lookup reads at most those two slots (worst-case O(1)); an insert cuckoo-kicks the occupant to its other slot, recursively, until an empty nest is found. Slide through the kick chains, trigger the 3-key cycle, and dial the d-ary load factor. Every number is recomputed in JS from the identical formulas as cuckoo_hashing.py, gold-checked below.

① Insert with kick chains — an occupied slot evicts its tenant to the other table

What to do: drag keys inserted up to 8 and watch key 80's 3-deep displacement chain (gold = just placed, red = evicted this step). Each key always lands in one of its two candidate slots.

② Lookup reads BOTH slots only — worst case 2 probes, always

What to do: click a key (try 10, found in T2 after 2 probes, or the absent key 99). The blue outline marks each slot probed in order; green = hit. A miss still costs exactly 2 probes — there is no chain to walk.
Chaining / linear probing give expected O(1) but worst case O(N); cuckoo hashing guarantees worst-case 2 probes — the whole reason it exists.  © Section B

③ Cycle detection — 3 keys in one slot-pair loop forever → rehash

What to do: toggle before rehash to watch {33,42,143} — all aimed at the same pair (1,1) — ping-pong forever (cycle detected). Then toggle after rehash: fresh hashes (7,3)/(7,5) scatter key 33 away and all three fit with a single kick.
MAX_KICKS = 8
A slot-pair holds at most 2 keys; the 3rd aimed at it cannot nest — its chain is an infinite 143→42→33 loop. Capping the chain at MAX_KICKS detects it; rehashing redraws the cuckoo graph and breaks the jam.  © Section D

④ Load factor dial — 2 nests top out ~50%, d-ary reaches ~90%+

What to do: pick the number of nests d and the target load α. Below the threshold c(d) you are safe; above it the cuckoo graph tangles into cycles and rehash storms. Watch the usable-capacity bar fill toward 100% as d grows.

c(d) is the max α at which a random instance is still placeable (Fotakis et al. 2005). d=3 is the sweet spot: ~91% load, ≤3 probes per lookup. Cuckoo keeps worst-case O(1) lookup at every α < c(d), unlike linear probing's expected O(1).  © Section E
