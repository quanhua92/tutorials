## A. The 8 core social phrasal verbs (the survival set)

These are the high-frequency, informal verbs that make **social English sound
native**. Vietnamese has no phrasal-verb structure at all (every verb is one
word), so learners reach for the stiff Latinate single verb (*accompany*,
*contribute*, *visit*) instead of the chunk (*tag along*, *chip in*, *drop by*).
The result sounds formal and bookish in a casual setting. Each verb below is
**figurative/idiomatic**: its meaning is NOT the literal sum of verb + particle
— *catch up* is not "grab upward," *chip in* is not "break a piece inward."

## B. Literal vs figurative — why these are NOT verb + particle "in pieces"

A phrasal verb is a **single semantic unit**. The particle (*out/up/by/in/along*)
does not keep its literal spatial meaning. This is the core trap: a learner who
translates word-by-word gets nonsense.

| Phrasal verb | Literal reading (WRONG) | Real social meaning (RIGHT) | One-line cue |
|---|---|---|---|
| hang out | to suspend something outdoors | to spend casual time with/with someone | *spend time* |
| catch up | to grab something upward (physically) | to exchange news; update each other | *update each other* |
| drop by | to let something fall near | to visit briefly, unannounced | *visit briefly* |
| chip in | to break a chip inward | to contribute money/effort together | *contribute* |
| show up | to display upward | to arrive / appear | *arrive* |
| meet up | to encounter upward | to meet (socially) | *meet* |
| tag along | to label along a line | to accompany (often uninvited) | *accompany* |
| get together | to obtain togetherness | to meet socially (pre-arranged) | *gather socially* |

> **Vietnamese L1 note:** Vietnamese has **no verb+particle structure** — every
> verb is a single morpheme (*gặp* = meet, *đóng góp* = contribute, *ghé* =
> drop in). So there is no mental model for "two words = one verb." Learners
> either (a) translate the verb alone and drop the particle, or (b) reach for
> the formal one-word synonym. Both sound stiff. The chunk is the unit.
