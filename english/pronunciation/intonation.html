5Writing task

Write 6 sentences and mark every tone with ↘ (fall) or ↗ (rise). State the effect of each. Then reveal the model answer.

Model (mark the tone, then state the effect):

She's a teacher.↘ — fall → statement, finality ("this is a fact")

She's a teacher?↗ — rise → yes/no question / surprised echo (same words!)

Where do you live?↘ — fall → Wh-question (speaker expects an answer)

Do you want coffee?↗ — rise → yes/no question (open, answerable by yes/no)

Could you help me?↗ — rise → softens the request into politeness (a fall would sound like an order)

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Vietnamese is a lexical-tone language — pitch lives on each syllable distinguishing words; no sentence-level intonation to transfer.Re-train pitch as a phrase-level event: steady pitch on most of the sentence, then one movement (↘/↗) on the nucleus.
Monotone English — the per-syllable tone habit flattens the melody; statements & questions sound the same.Exaggerate the fall on statements and the rise on yes/no questions until the contrast is obvious; then relax. Record yourself.
Carry-over of Vietnamese tones — a rising word-tone (sắc/ngã) lands on an English syllable and distorts the intended contour.Treat English words as tone-less; assign tone only at phrase level, on the nucleus. Drill "teacher" with no word-tone, only the sentence tone.
Statement ends on a rise → sounds unsure / like still asking.End statements and Wh-questions on a fall ↘ — the "I'm telling you" signal.
Yes/no question ends on a fall → sounds blunt / like a demand ("You want coffee.↘").End yes/no questions on a rise ↗ — the "over to you" signal.
No listing contour — every list item gets the same flat tone → list sounds unfinished.Rise on each item except the last, fall on the last: apples ↗, oranges ↗, and grapes ↘. The final fall says "that's everything".
Requests delivered on a fall → sound like commands / rude.Soften requests with a rise: Could you help me?↗. A fall on a request reads as an order.
No fall-rise (↘↗) for implication — flattens "It's good↗…" to "It's good.↘", removing the "…but".Learn the fall-rise as the "…but" tone: pitch down then up on the nucleus (It's ↘good↗). Core hedging/disagreement tool.
Focus stress never moves — nucleus always on the last word, so "I saw a man" can't distinguish "whom?" from "what happened?".Practise shifting the nucleus to the new info: I saw a ↘MAN (whom?) vs I ↘SAW a man (what did you do?). De-stress what the listener already knows.
