5Writing task

Write a 3-part live-feedback script: permission + what-worked + one-tweak. Then reveal the model answer.

Model (permission → specific praise → one forward tweak):

① Can I give you some feedback? /kæn aɪ ɡɪv juː səm ˈfiːdbæk/ — ask first

② What worked well was your opening line — it grabbed the room. /wɒt wɜːkt wel wəz/ — specific, not "good job"

③ One thing to tweak: next time, try pausing after the headline. /nekst ˈtaɪm traɪ/ — one tweak, then stop

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Avoids live critical feedback entirely — "it was fine," "good"Deliver it, but ask permission first (Can I give you some feedback?) — the ask redresses face so content can be honest.
Skips the preamble and goes blunt — "your slides too long," no softenerUse the three-part arc: ask → what worked → one tweak. The "what worked" step isn't padding; it makes the tweak land.
Vague praise — "good job" (translated, unspecific)Be specific: What worked well was your opening line. Name the exact thing.
Delivers feedback days late (email next week)Make it real-time: in the moment or right after. Email is the written SBI bundle's job.
Piles up the tweaks — "and slides, and voice, and timing…" liveOne tweak, then stop. One thing to tweak… is singular on purpose.
Fear of offending a senior live → says nothing, or over-hedges to nothingCan I share some quick feedback? gives them the choice to say no — lowers risk for both.
"You should…" as the suggestion (sounds like an order)Use the forward hedge: Next time, try… / One thing to tweak…
Drops final consonants — "twi" for tweak, "neil" for nailedRelease every final: tweak /twiːk/, nailed /neɪld/.
No past-tense marking on praise — "You nail it" (present)Enforce the past: You nailed it /neɪld/, I loved how you… /lʌvd/.
