## B. STAR response frames (the candidate's answer skeleton)

Once you hear a cue, you answer in **STAR** order: Situation ‚Üí Task ‚Üí Action ‚Üí
Result. These four lead-in phrases are the spoken "signposts" that let the
interviewer follow your story. The STAR model is confirmed in The Muse, MIT
CAPD, and Indeed; the four lead-in frames are the standard spoken realizations
recommended by those same sources.

## C. Reflection phrases (closing a STAR answer + the "R+" layer)

A strong behavioral answer often adds a one-line reflection after the Result ‚ÄĒ
the "what I learned" layer. This signals self-awareness and growth, which
English-language interview culture rewards. Confirmed as recommended closings
in The Muse, Indeed, and MIT CAPD STAR guides.
