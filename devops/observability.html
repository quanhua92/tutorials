Three pillars tied together by one trace_id. Metrics aggregate the system, Logs explain an event, Traces show where the time went. Click a trace_id in the log stream to see its span waterfall. What to look at: the same request (trace_id a4f9…) shows up in all three. Metrics aggregate many requests into a rate; logs give the discrete events; the trace's span self-time (not raw duration!) tells you what to optimize.

② USE (resources) vs RED (services) — pick the right lens

What to do: toggle between the two checklists. They ask different questions of different things. USE the metal (CPU/disk/NIC), RED the traffic (services/endpoints). Run BOTH: a saturated CPU (USE) explains a service's rising Duration (RED).

③ SLO error-budget calculator + burn-rate alerting

What to do: set the SLO target, then enter how much you have actually failed. The error budget is 1 − SLO; burn rate is error_rate ÷ budget. Above 14.4× in 1 h → PAGE.
