5Writing task

Insert appropriate discourse markers into the flat transcript below. One per slot — don't over-stack. Then reveal the model answer.

Flat transcript (no markers — sounds robotic):

"Are you free tonight? I was thinking we could grab dinner. I don't know, Italian? It's cheap. We haven't talked in ages."

Model (one marker per slot — each doing a distinct job):

"So, are you free tonight? — conversation-starter (so, sense 4)

I was thinking we could grab dinner. Well, I don't know — Italian? — response-shift / buying a beat (well)

It's actually pretty cheap. — contrast with expectation (actually, sense 2)

To be honest, we haven't talked in ages, you know? — sincerity preface + shared-knowledge appeal

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Drops markers entirely — "I am tired. I will go home." (correct, translated, robotic)Add the glue: "Well, I'm kind of tired, so I think I'll head home, you know?" Aim for one marker per turn, in the right slot.
Over-uses so in every slot (opening, result, transition, filler) — because so maps closely to VN nên / vậy / thìLearn the distinct jobs: result → so; reflective opening → well; topic reset → anyway; summary → so, that's it. Rotate; don't stack.
Reads "you know?" as a literal question the hearer must answer — because VN biết không IS a real questionTreat you know as rhetorical glue (shared-knowledge appeal) or a filler. Do NOT pause for an answer.
Translates "well" → "tốt" and refuses to use it, because the dictionary meaning doesn't fitLearn well as a function word: it opens a response when a blunt yes/no is too direct. Drill "Well, I'm not sure."
Never self-repairs — restarts the whole sentence from scratchUse I mean to fix in-flight: "He's really… I mean, he's pretty talented." One marker, keep going.
Mistakes "right?" for the adjective "correct" — feels redundantUse right? as a standalone agreement-check at the end: "So that's twenty of each, right?" No "it is" needed.
Uses "actually" only as "in fact" — misses the polite-correction jobUse actually to soften corrections: "We're not American, actually. We're Canadian." — gentler than the bare denial.
Says "I understand" everywhere (textbook over-formal) where a native says "I see (what you mean)"Swap: when you've just followed someone, say "I see what you mean" / "Right, I see." Save I understand for a topic/skill.
Confuses to be honest (warm) with honestly (can sound accusatory)Use to be honest (or TBH in text) for the confessional move: "To be honest, I didn't love it." Reserve honestly? for a real question.
Stresses every marker equally — "WELL. SO. I MEAN." (clipped, syllable-timed)De-stress the marker: it's a weak beat. well → /wel/ (low, short); I mean → /aɪ mən/ in fast speech. 🔗 SENTENCE STRESS.
