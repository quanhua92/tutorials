vram_estimator.py - LLM-inference VRAM budget (weights + KV cache + overhead).
Pure Python stdlib. Numbers below feed VRAM_ESTIMATOR.md.
Sources: llama.cpp memory math + the per-model config.json head/layer counts.

Lineage: rule-of-thumb (params*B/8) -> exact (weights + KV + overhead).

==========================================================================
  SECTION A: WEIGHTS term - params x bpw / 8 (the dominant cost)
==========================================================================
The weights term is the size of the resident .gguf tensors. It dwarfs
everything else at short context and is set ENTIRELY by the quant type.

Formula (the rule of thumb, made exact):
  weights_GB = params_in_billions * bits_per_weight / 8
  (the 1e9 from params and the 1e9 bytes->GB cancel)

bpw per quant type (cross-ref QUANT_TYPES.md):
| quant   | bpw  | bytes/weight | note                              |
|---------|------|--------------|-----------------------------------|
| FP16    | 16.0 |       2.0000 | unquantized half precision
| Q4_K_M  |  4.5 |       0.5625 | rule-of-thumb (pure Q4_K block)
| Q8_0    |  8.5 |       1.0625 | near-lossless int8 block

Worked: Llama-3-8B (8.03B params) across the three quants:
| quant   | params | bpw  | weights_GB |
|---------|--------|------|------------|
| FP16    |   8.03 | 16.0 |    16.0600 |
| Q4_K_M  |   8.03 |  4.5 |     4.5169 |
| Q8_0    |   8.03 |  8.5 |     8.5319 |

GOLD (for VRAM_ESTIMATOR.html):
  Llama-3-8B Q4_K_M weights = 8.03 * 4.5 / 8 = 4.516875 GB  (~4.52 GB)
[check] Q4_K_M weights ~= 4.52 GB :  OK  (got 4.516875)

The quant type is the single biggest lever on the weights term (and
hence on the whole budget at short context). Cutting 16->4.5 bpw shrinks
the resident weights ~3.5x with modest quality loss (see QUANT_TYPES.md).

==========================================================================
  SECTION B: KV CACHE term - the part that scales with context
==========================================================================
The KV cache stores, for every token decoded so far, the K and V vectors
of every KV head in every layer. It grows LINEARLY with n_ctx, which is
why long-context models eat VRAM that the rule-of-thumb never predicted.

Formula:
  bytes = n_layers * 2 * n_ctx * n_kv_heads * head_dim * bytes_per_element
           ^^^^^^^^  ^        ^^^^^^^^^^   ^^^^^^^^   ^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^
           per layer K+V      GQA heads    head width   FP16=2, Q8=1
  GB    = bytes / 1e9

Worked: Llama-3-8B (32 layers, 8 KV heads, head_dim=128), FP16 KV (2 B/E):
  @   4096 ctx: 32*2*4096*8*128*2   =   536,870,912 bytes = 0.536871 GB
  @  32768 ctx: 32*2*32768*8*128*2  = 4,294,967,296 bytes = 4.294967 GB
  -> context x8 (4k->32k) => KV x8.0 (strictly linear in n_ctx)

GQA is the multiplier that decides HOW FAST the cache grows. Compare the
KV head ratio (n_heads / n_kv_heads) for each model:
| model        | n_heads | n_kv_heads | GQA ratio | KV@4K (FP16) | KV@32K (FP16) |
|--------------|---------|------------|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Llama-2-13B  |      40 |         40 |    1.0x   |     3.3554 GB |     26.8435 GB |
| Llama-3-70B  |      64 |          8 |    8.0x   |     1.3422 GB |     10.7374 GB |
| Llama-3-8B   |      32 |          8 |    4.0x   |     0.5369 GB |      4.2950 GB |
| Qwen2.5-7B   |      28 |          4 |    7.0x   |     0.2349 GB |      1.8790 GB |

Llama-2-13B uses plain multi-head attention (ratio 1x) so its KV cache is
HUGE relative to its size - 13B MHA caches more than 70B GQA at the same ctx.
  Llama-2-13B @32K FP16 KV = 26.844 GB   vs   Llama-3-70B @32K FP16 KV = 10.737 GB
[check] 70B GQA KV < 13B MHA KV at 32K :  OK  (70B=10.737 vs 13B=26.844)
[check] KV is linear in context (32K/4K == 8x) :  OK

KV-cache quantization (Q8, 1 B/E) HALVES this term. Cross-ref kv_cache_quant.
  Llama-3-8B @32K: FP16 KV = 4.295 GB, Q8 KV = 2.147 GB (-50%)

==========================================================================
  SECTION C: OVERHEAD term - context buffers + compute workspace
==========================================================================
The third term is everything that is neither weights nor KV: activations,
the compute workspace (scratch buffers for the matmuls / attention), the
CUDA/Metal context, and OS/display reservation on consumer cards.

Formula (the least-exact term - it is a rule of thumb):
  overhead_GB = max(0.5, 0.10 * weights_GB)
  (a ~0.5 GB floor for small models, ~10% of weights for large ones)

| model        | quant   | weights_GB | 10%    | overhead_GB |
|--------------|---------|------------|--------|-------------|
| Llama-2-13B  | FP16    |    26.0400 |  2.604 |    2.6040  (10%) |
| Llama-2-13B  | Q4_K_M  |     7.3237 |  0.732 |    0.7324  (10%) |
| Llama-2-13B  | Q8_0    |    13.8338 |  1.383 |    1.3834  (10%) |
| Llama-3-70B  | FP16    |   140.0000 | 14.000 |   14.0000  (10%) |
| Llama-3-70B  | Q4_K_M  |    39.3750 |  3.938 |    3.9375  (10%) |
| Llama-3-70B  | Q8_0    |    74.3750 |  7.438 |    7.4375  (10%) |
| Llama-3-8B   | FP16    |    16.0600 |  1.606 |    1.6060  (10%) |
| Llama-3-8B   | Q4_K_M  |     4.5169 |  0.452 |    0.5000  (floor) |
| Llama-3-8B   | Q8_0    |     8.5319 |  0.853 |    0.8532  (10%) |
| Qwen2.5-7B   | FP16    |    15.2400 |  1.524 |    1.5240  (10%) |
| Qwen2.5-7B   | Q4_K_M  |     4.2862 |  0.429 |    0.5000  (floor) |
| Qwen2.5-7B   | Q8_0    |     8.0962 |  0.810 |    0.8096  (10%) |

Why a FLOOR: even a tiny model needs context buffers + a CUDA/Metal
context (~0.3-0.5 GB). Why a PERCENTAGE: activation + workspace tensors
scale with the matmul sizes, which scale with the weights.

If you OOM right at the edge, the overhead term is the first place to
blame: quantize the KV cache, drop --parallel-context / batch size, or add
a few hundred MB of slack to this estimate.
