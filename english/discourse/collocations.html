6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
One verb "làm" maps to both make and do → "make homework", "do a decision"Store the noun welded to its verb: learn make-a-decision, do-homework as single units.
Synonym substitution — "mạnh" → strong attached to any noun → "strong rain"Learn the adjective+noun pair whole: heavy-rain, strong-wind. The noun owns one adjective.
Word-by-word translation → "make a meeting", "give attention", "make a photo"Memorize the fixed chunk (have a meeting, pay attention, take a photo).
Dictionary-synonym reflex — assumes interchangeability ("strong = heavy")Use a collocation dictionary (Oxford Collocations, BBI), not a thesaurus, when gluing a word to a noun.
No "sounds right" instinct — cannot self-monitor because the chunk was never stored wholeRe-read your writing asking only: "does this pairing sound right?" If unsure, you don't know the chunk.
Over-generalizes one chunk ("make a decision" → "make a photo")Each noun is a separate chunk. make a choice ✓ but take a photo ✓. Learn each on its own merits.
Translates the verb literally ("chú ý" → "give attention")Accept the verb is idiomatic: pay attention has nothing to do with money — it's the chunk.
