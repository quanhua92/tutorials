📖 Read the full guide — architecture diagrams, decision tables, killer gotchas, cheat sheet. This page is the interactive companion.
A deadlock is a cycle in the wait-for graph. Step through the scenario and watch the ring close. Then pick a victim-selection heuristic and see which txn gets aborted. The DFS cycle detector and the heuristics are recomputed in JS with the identical logic as deadlock_detection.py (GOLD section).  |  isolation levels that cause the waits →

① Wait-for graph — step through the deadlock; the cycle lights up red

What to do: pick a scenario, then drag the step slider. Each step adds one lock/wait event. Edges point waiter → holder. The instant a cycle closes, the ring turns red and a DEADLOCK banner appears. detect_cycle runs live on every step.

② Lock table — who holds what, who waits for what

What this shows: the lock table that produces the graph above. For each resource: the holder (exclusive) and the waiters queued behind it. One waiter → holder edge per row is exactly the wait-for graph.

③ Victim selection — 3 heuristics pick 3 different victims (cycle T1→T2→T3→T1)

What to do: the 3-txn ring is deadlocked. Pick a heuristic; the cheapest txn to roll back is the victim (aborted). Different heuristics pick different txns — a policy trade-off, not a fact.
