Music Generation — raw audio → RVQ codec tokens → full song 📖 MUSIC_GENERATION.md 📄 music_generation.py

A 3-minute 44.1 kHz stereo song is 15,876,000 samples — hopeless to predict one-by-one. A neural audio codec (EnCodec) strides the waveform 320× to 75 frames/s, then quantizes each frame with 8 residual codebooks in series → 600 tokens/s (108,000 tokens for a whole song, 147× fewer than raw). Slide the codebook count to watch the reconstruction converge and residual energy collapse. The gold-check pins 15,876,000 samples, 108,000 tokens, and ACE-Step's 2.20 s/min to the exact .py output.

② Token budget — why the codec makes music tractable

What this shows: a 3-minute song as raw samples vs EnCodec frames vs tokens. The codec cuts the things-to-predict from 15.88M to 108K — a 147× reduction. That is the entire reason music generation is feasible.
75 frames/s × 8 codebooks = 600 tokens/s. Frames are the time axis (75 Hz, stride 320); each frame carries 8 parallel codebook indices (its tokens).

③ Model comparison — ACE-Step vs YuE vs the rest

What this shows: the two SOTA open-source paths to a full song (vocals + accompaniment). ACE-Step generates the whole song latent in parallel (flow-matching, ~27 steps); YuE emits tokens sequentially (two-stage autoregressive cascade). Same end product, very different speed.
Licenses verified from each GitHub README: ACE-Step & YuE are Apache 2.0 (commercial-OK); Stable Audio Open & MusicGen are CC-BY-NC (non-commercial).

④ Speed — time to render 1 minute of audio

What this shows: ACE-Step real-time factor (RTF) across hardware (27 steps), plus YuE for contrast. Lower bar = faster. A100 renders a minute in 2.20 s; a MacBook M2 Max takes ~26 s; YuE's token-by-token cascade takes minutes.
RTF 27.27× = 1 minute of audio in 60/27.27 = 2.20 s. A full 4-minute song on A100 ≈ 8.8 s (parallel — not 4× one-minute autoregression).

What this shows: typical VRAM footprint (peak, with codec + text encoder). ACE-Step's 8 GB is the minimum with --cpu_offload true --overlapped_decode true --bf16 true; budget headroom above it.

⑥ Pipeline — text + lyrics → full song

What this shows: both models share stages 1–3 and 5–6; they differ only in stage 4 (how the latent/tokens are produced). ACE-Step = parallel flow-matching (~27 steps over the whole song); YuE = autoregressive semantic→acoustic cascade.
Stage 4 is the whole speed story: ACE-Step visits a fixed ~27 ODE steps (whole-song latent in parallel); YuE must emit ~108,000 tokens sequentially — ~4000× more steps.
