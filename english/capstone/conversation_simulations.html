Setting: a networking event

Model — 6 turns, 4 functions, each turn's management move labelled:

Turn 1 (A · OPEN) — So, what brings you here? I don't think we've met. /soʊ wɑːt ˈbrɪŋz juː ˈhɪr/ — OPEN

Turn 2 (B · small talk / ELICIT) — So, tell me about your work — you said you're in design? /soʊ ˈtel miː əˈbaʊt/ — ELICIT

Turn 3 (A · opinion / LINK) — Speaking of design, I'd say remote work makes teams more creative. /ˈspiːkɪŋ əv/ — LINK + opinion

Turn 4 (B · diplomatic disagree) — I see your point, but I'd say in-person sparks better ideas. diplomatic disagree (🔗 DIPLOMATIC_DISAGREEMENT)

Turn 5 (A · redirect / RETURN) — Anyway, let me ask you this — do you miss the office at all? /ˈeniweɪ/ + /ˌlet mi ˈæsk juː ˈðɪs/ — RETURN + REDIRECT

Turn 6 (B · CLOSE) — It was great talking to you, but I should get going. /ɪt wəz ˌɡreɪt ˈtɑːkɪŋ tə juː/ — CLOSE

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Knows functions in isolation but freezes between them — can open, or small-talk, or close, but cannot chain across minutesDrill the management moves as the glue. After every function, load the next move: open → "tell me about…" → "speaking of…" → "anyway" → close.
Opens and then stops — waits passively for the partner to driveAlways pair the open with an eliciting question: "So, what brings you here? … So, tell me about your work." The elicitation is the engine.
Jumps topics with no bridge — "The weather is nice. I bought a new phone."Deploy a marker before the new topic. Related → Speaking of… / That reminds me…; unrelated → By the way…
Waits passively or interrupts bluntly — no polite way to take the wheelUse Let me ask you this… as the self-authorizing redirect. It flags a weighted question is coming.
Starts a fresh topic after a digression instead of returning — loses the threadDrill Anyway + the parked topic as your return ticket. After every side trip, plan the "Anyway" that brings you home.
Ghosts or traps themselves at the end — wants to leave but has no exit lineLoad the pre-close: "It was great talking to you, but I should get going."
Translates word-by-word in real time — slow, halting, freezes when the reply isn't memorisedRetrieve chunks, not words. "So, what brings you here?" is one unit. Drill the 8 chunks until they fire as blocks under load.
Pro-drop / omitted subject → "Bring you here?" instead of "What brings you here?"Supply the subject + auxiliary. English demands "What brings…" / "It was great talking…" — the subject is load-bearing.
Drops the final consonant in brings /brɪŋz/ → "bring", tell /tel/ → "te", remind /rɪˈmaɪnd/ → "remin"Release every final: /ŋz/ on brings, /l/ on tell, /d/ on remind. A dropped final kills the management move.
Wrong stress on anyway → "any-WAY", mapping VN even-syllable rhythm onto EnglishStress the first syllable: ANYway /ˈeniweɪ/. The marker only works with native stress.
No turn-management awareness — treats conversation as monologue; never hands the floor or signals a topic boundaryConversations are managed turns + topics + repair, not solo speech. Name every move out loud until it is automatic.
