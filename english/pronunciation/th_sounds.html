6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
/θ/ → /t/ — think → "tink", three → "tree", bath → "bat"Put the tongue tip between the teeth (you will see it). Hiss air out, no buzz. Drill think/tink, three/tree.
/θ/ → /s/ — thick → "sick", thin → "sin"Pull the tongue forward to the teeth; /s/ stays on the ridge. Minimal pair: thick/sick, thin/sin.
/ð/ → /z/ — this → "zis", they → "zey", with → "wiz"Same posture as /θ/, then add voice (throat buzz). Touch your throat — it must vibrate on /ð/.
/ð/ → /d/ (often implosive /ɗ/) — this → "dis", brother → "broder"Tongue between teeth + hum. Vietnamese /ɗ/ uses the ridge; /ð/ uses the teeth.
No dental fricatives in L1 → attach one sound (/t/) to every "th"Learn the position-rule: initial content word = /θ/; function word = /ð/; medial native word = /ð/; final noun = /θ/.
Tongue never protrudes in Vietnamese stops → posture feels wrongPractise in a mirror until you see the tongue tip. Exaggerate first, then relax to a light contact.
Drops final /θ/ — bath → "ba", with → "wi"Hold the tongue on the teeth and release the hiss audibly before the next word.
De-voices /ð/ → /θ/ — the /ðə/ said as /θə/ (no buzz)Keep the throat buzzing the whole time. The without buzz sounds whispered; buzz makes it natural.
Confuses /θ/ and /ð/ as the "same th" — think buzzed, this notHand test (/θ/ = cool air only) vs throat test (/ð/ = air + vibration). Alternate think/this.
