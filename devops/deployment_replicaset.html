A Deployment is a foreman (desired count + pod template). It hires a ReplicaSet per revision (the crew chief that self-heals), which owns the Pods (workers). Drag maxSurge/maxUnavailable and watch the interleaved rollout keep two guardrails: total ≤ replicas+maxSurge, available ≥ replicas−maxUnavailable. ① The hierarchy — Deployment owns ReplicaSets owns Pods

What this shows: the Deployment never creates Pods directly. A new image tag → a new pod-template-hash → a new ReplicaSet. Each RS enforces "actual == desired" and self-heals lost pods.
Ownership chain: Deployment web ──owns──▶ ReplicaSet web-7b9c ──owns──▶ N Pods. The pod-template-hash ties each Pod to its ReplicaSet.

② Rolling update — interleaved scale up/down, two guardrails

What to do: drag the knobs. maxSurge bounds scale-UP (extra pods); maxUnavailable bounds scale-DOWN (capacity loss). With surge=1, unavail=0 you get zero downtime (avail never below replicas) at the cost of 1 extra pod. The bars show v1 shrinking as v2 grows.

③ RollingUpdate vs Recreate — downtime trade-off

What to do: toggle the strategy. RollingUpdate interleaves (avail never below replicas−maxUnavailable). Recreate kills ALL old first, then creates new — there is a downtime step (avail=0). Use Recreate only when two versions cannot coexist.

④ ReplicaSet self-healing — actual always returns to desired

What to do: click a Pod to kill it. The ReplicaSet reconcile loop notices desired != actual and creates a replacement on the next step. The Deployment is not involved — self-healing is the RS's job.
