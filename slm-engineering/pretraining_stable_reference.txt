================================================================================
[1] https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.06395
    Hu et al. 2024 "MiniCPM: Unveiling the Potential of End-side Large Language
    Models with Scalable Training Algorithms" (official).
    Verifies: the Warmup-Stable-Decay (WSD) learning rate scheduler -- "For data
    scaling, we introduce a Warmup-Stable-Decay (WSD) learning rate scheduler
    (LRS), conducive to continuous training and domain adaptation." WSD = linear
    warmup to lr_max, STABLE plateau at lr_max for the main phase, then a DECAY
    phase (the "cooldown") over the final ~10% of steps. The stable-phase
    checkpoint is reusable; the cooldown is where the loss sharply drops.

[2] https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.05192
    Wen et al. 2024 "Understanding Warmup-Stable-Decay Learning Rates" (official).
    Verifies: the WSD three-phase decomposition (warmup, stable, decay); the
    decay shape S(x) family (linear, square, sqrt, cosine); that the decay phase
    is typically 8-20% of total steps; the "river valley" loss-landscape
    explanation (stable phase explores, cooldown suppresses variance -> the
    observed sharp loss drop). Independent theoretical confirmation of MiniCPM's
    empirical schedule.

[3] https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.05101
    Loshchilov & Hutter 2019 "Decoupled Weight Decay Regularization" (ICLR 2019,
    official; old title "Fixing Weight Decay Regularization in Adam").
    Verifies: the AdamW update rule with DECOUPLED weight decay --
      m = beta1*m + (1-beta1)*g ; v = beta2*v + (1-beta2)*g^2
      m_hat = m/(1-beta1^t)    ; v_hat = v/(1-beta2^t)   (bias correction)
      w <- w - lr*( m_hat/(sqrt(v_hat)+eps) + wd*w )    (decay DIRECT on w)
    -- versus Adam+L2 which folds lambda*w INTO the gradient g before the
    moments, so the decay is averaged by the momentum (the bug AdamW fixes).
    "Adam generalizes substantially better with decoupled weight decay than with
    L2 regularization."

[4] https://arxiv.org/abs/1608.03983
    Loshchilov & Hutter 2017 "SGDR: Stochastic Gradient Descent with Warm
    Restarts" (ICLR 2017, official).
    Verifies: the cosine annealing learning rate schedule --
      eta_t = eta_min + 0.5*(eta_max - eta_min)*(1 + cos(pi*t/T)),  t in [0,T]
    -- the half-cosine decay from eta_max down to eta_min over T steps. This is
    the schedule GPT-3, Llama 1/2, and most modern LLMs adopt (with a linear
    warmup prepended).

[5] https://arxiv.org/abs/1211.5063
    Pascanu, Mikolov & Bengio 2013 "On the difficulty of training Recurrent
    Neural Networks" (ICML 2013, official; proceedings.mlr.press/v28/pascanu13).
    Verifies: gradient-norm clipping to counter exploding gradients --
      if ||g|| > max_norm:  g <- g * (max_norm / ||g||)   (else: unchanged)
    -- the global-norm clipping strategy. Direction is preserved (all gradients
    scaled by the same factor); only the magnitude is capped. This is the
    technique GPT-3 / Llama 2 apply with max_norm=1.0 every step.

[6] https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165
    Brown et al. 2020 "Language Models are Few-Shot Learners" (GPT-3, official).
    Verifies: GPT-3 training hyperparameters -- Adam optimizer, gradient norm
    clipped at 1.0, weight decay 0.1 ("All models use weight decay of 0.1 to
    provide a small amount of regularization"), cosine decay with linear warmup
    on the learning rate. The canonical reference for clip=1.0 + wd=0.1 +
    cosine+warmup as the de-facto LLM pretraining recipe.

[7] https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.09288
    Touvron et al. 2023 "Llama 2: Open Foundation and Fine-Tuned Chat Models"
    (official, Training A.1).
    Verifies: Llama 2 uses AdamW with beta1=0.9, beta2=0.95, eps=1e-5; cosine
    learning rate schedule with peak LR 3e-4, 2000 warmup steps, decay to 10%
    of the peak (final LR ratio 0.1); weight decay 0.1; gradient clipping 1.0.
    The modern standard AdamW + cosine + clip=1.0 + wd=0.1 hyperparameter set.

[8] https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.13971
    Touvron et al. 2023 "LLaMA: Open and Efficient Foundation Language Models"
    (Llama 1, official).
    Verifies: Llama 1 training used AdamW (beta1=0.9, beta2=0.95), cosine
    schedule with 2000 warmup steps and peak LR 3e-4, weight decay 0.1,
    gradient clipping 1.0. Confirms the Llama 1/2 hyperparameter set is shared.

[9] https://docs.pytorch.org/docs/stable/generated/torch.nn.utils.clip_grad_norm_.html
    PyTorch 2.x documentation, torch.nn.utils.clip_grad_norm_ (authoritative).
    Verifies: the global-norm clipping IMPLEMENTATION -- "The norm is computed
    over the norms of the individual gradients of all parameters"; total_norm =
    sqrt(sum of per-tensor norm^2); clip_coefficient = max_norm / (total_norm + 1e-6);
    g <- g * min(clip_coefficient, 1). This is the exact math the .py implements
    by hand (Section B) and asserts against.

[10] https://mbrenndoerfer.com/writing/adamw-optimizer-decoupled-weight-decay
    Michael Brenndoerfer, "AdamW: Decoupled Weight Decay for Neural Networks"
    (Language AI Handbook, 2025) -- authoritative secondary analysis.
    Verifies: the AdamW vs Adam+L2 update equations; that weight decay in AdamW
    is applied DIRECTLY to the parameters (decoupled), while Adam+L2 adds it to
    the gradient (coupled); the "AdamW's flat line" intuition (the regularization
    coefficient has consistent meaning across parameters). Second-source
    confirmation of arXiv:1711.05101.

[11] https://www.emergentmind.com/topics/warmup-stable-decay-wsd-scheduling
    EmergentMind, "Warmup-Stable-Decay Scheduling" (topic survey, 2024-2025).
    Verifies: the WSD parameterization in closed form -- warmup
    eta(t)=eta_max*t/T_w; stable eta(t)=eta_max; decay eta(t)=eta_max*S(x) with
    x in [0,1] over T_d steps and S_cos(x)=(1+cos(pi*x))/2; the decay phase is
    8-20% of total steps; sqrt and cosine decay shapes are best. Independent
    confirmation of the WSD formula from arXiv:2404.06395 / 2410.05192.

[12] https://apxml.com/courses/optimization-techniques-ml/chapter-3-adaptive-learning-rate-algorithms/learning-rate-schedules-advanced
    APXML, "Advanced Learning Rate Scheduling Techniques" (2024) -- secondary
    educational source.
    Verifies: the cosine annealing schedule (arXiv:1608.03983) "introduces the
    cosine annealing learning rate schedule with periodic warm restarts";
    warmup + cosine decay as the standard for transformer/LLM pretraining.
    Second-source confirmation of the cosine formula.

NOTABLE VERIFICATION NOTES:
  * The cosine formula lr(t) = lr_min + 0.5*(lr_max - lr_min)*(1 + cos(pi*t/T))
    is confirmed verbatim by SGDR (arXiv:1608.03983), the APXML survey, and is
    the schedule Llama 1/2 cite (arXiv:2307.09288 Training A.1).
  * The WSD three-phase shape and the cosine decay shape S(x)=(1+cos(pi*x))/2
    are confirmed by BOTH MiniCPM (arXiv:2404.06395) and "Understanding WSD"
    (arXiv:2410.05192), plus the EmergentMind survey.
  * Llama 2 clip=1.0, wd=0.1, peak 3e-4, 2000 warmup, beta=(0.9,0.95): confirmed
    in the Llama 2 paper Training A.1 and second-sourced by the gilesthomas /
    MosaicML DecoupledAdamW docs that quote the Llama hyperparameter set.
  * GPT-3 clip=1.0, wd=0.1, cosine+warmup: confirmed in the GPT-3 paper (sec 4)
    AND the NeurIPS "Why Do We Need Weight Decay" paper which quotes GPT-3's
    "weight decay of 0.1" verbatim.
