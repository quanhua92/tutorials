What to do: drag the slider to apply unions one at a time. Each green node is a root; arrows point from a node to its parent. Union by rank links the lower-rank root under the higher one (equal ranks bump rank by 1).

② Kruskal's MST — union-find as a cycle detector (step through edges)

What to do: step through edges cheapest-first. An edge is added if its endpoints are in different sets; skipped if they're already connected (would form a cycle).
9 edges sorted by weight; MST needs |V|-1 = 5

③ Path compression — a deep chain flattened in one find

Naive unions build a degenerate chain where find(0) walks all 5 hops. With path compression, one find(0) repoints every node on the path straight at the root. Toggle to compare.
