English splits them by weight. These are the **low-weight** end — a bump, a slip,

## C. "Sorry to interrupt" vs "Sorry to apologize" — *excuse me*

The most common Vietnamese→English confusion: a single phrase *xin lỗi* maps to
**two different English functions**. *Excuse me* **gets attention / clears a
path / interrupts** — it is **not** an apology for harm. *Sorry* (or *I'm
sorry*) is the apology. Picking the wrong one marks you instantly as
non-native.

| English chunk | meaning | IPA | source URL | frequency rank | accent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Excuse me. | to get attention / pass / interrupt (NOT an apology) | /ɪkˈskjuːz miː/ | https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/excuse_2 | ≈#400 (of *excuse*) | US/UK |
| Sorry, can I just…? | polite interrupt using *sorry* | /ˈsɒr.i kæn aɪ dʒʌst/ UK · /ˈsɔːr.i kæn aɪ dʒʌst/ US | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/pronunciation/english/sorry | ≈#200 (of *sorry*) | US/UK |
| Excuse me, I didn't see you there. | apology for a bump (polite) | /ɪkˈskjuːz miː aɪ ˈdɪdnt siː jʊ ðeə/ | https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/excuse_2 | — | US/UK |

> **Verification note:** *excuse me* /ɪkˈskjuːz miː/ is the Oxford Advanced
> Learner's Dictionary transcription (web-fetched, 2026-06-23); the entry lists
> all six functions — get attention, ask to pass, apologise for interrupting,
> disagree politely, announce you're leaving, and apologise for a bump — and
> flags the North-American "apology for pushing someone" use explicitly. The
> Vietnamese-mapping contrast (*xin lỗi* covers both *excuse me* and *sorry*) is
> documented in Le, *Transnational Variation in Linguistic Politeness in
> Vietnamese* (VUIR) and Vu (1997, via core.ac.uk).

## D. Responding to an apology (acceptance chunks)

The missing half for many Vietnamese learners: English **expects a verbal
acceptance**. Vietnamese culture often accepts an apology with silence or a small
gesture — which reads as cold or unforgiving in English. These chunks close the
loop.

> **Verification note:** *no problem*, *that's OK*, *don't worry about it*,
