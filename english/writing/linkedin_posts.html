5Writing task — primary drill

Write a LinkedIn post using the hook → value → CTA skeleton. Then reveal the model answer.

Prompt: You just shipped a small project at work (pick anything real). Write a post where line 1 = hook, lines 2–5 = value (one specific lesson), last line = CTA (a question). No "humbled and honored."

Model (hook → value → CTA; no humble-brag):

[HOOK] We shipped the new checkout in 6 weeks. Here's the one thing that saved us.

[VALUE] Here's what I learned: we kept the scope frozen for the first 2 weeks. Every "small addition" got parked in a backlog. The key insight — saying "not now" 14 times is how we hit the date. Three things that helped: a daily 10-min sync, a public scope doc, and a no-blame retro each Friday.

[CTA] What would you add? Curious how your team protects a deadline.

Structure does the work: the hook promises, the value delivers, the CTA invites. The voice stays modest — no bragging needed.

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Modesty / "face" culture → discomfort with self-promotion; you undersell or bury the newsLet the structure carry the confidence, not the adjectives. Sharp hook + useful value + clean CTA = confident without bragging.
Overcompensate into the boastful cliché → "Humbled and honored to announce…" (feels modest in VN, reads as a flex in EN)Cut the qualifier pile-up. Lead with the fact + lesson: "Last month I shipped X. Here's what it taught me."
Pro-drop / missing subject → "Is good news. Want to share."Supply the subject + copula every time: "I'm excited to share…"
Drops finals when read aloud → "Three thing I learn" (the -s and -ed vanish)When you narrate the post, enforce Three things I learned — /θriː θɪŋz/, /ˈlɜːrnd/.
Drops the final cluster /θɔːts/ → "Thought?" (the /-ts/ collapses)Hold the cluster: Thoughts? /θɔːts/ with rising intonation. Drill the /-ts/ final.
"Vinh dự" → literal "honored" overuse in every sentenceReserve honored for genuine external recognition (award, speaking slot). For your own work, use the fact + lesson pattern.
Oversharing personal detail — a 400-word life story with no takeawayKeep value > story. The story is the setup; Here's what I learned is the point.
Email-register formality in a feed post → "I would like to respectfully inform that…"LinkedIn is professional-but-not-stiff. Short sentences, line breaks, contractions (I'm, here's, I'll).
Question-word translation → missing auxiliary → "You think?"Build the CTA from a verified chunk: What would you add? /wɒt wʊd juː æd/. Keep the would.
Hashtag stuffing — 12 hashtags to "get reach"3–5 relevant hashtags max. More reads as SEO grasping. Name who the post is for.
