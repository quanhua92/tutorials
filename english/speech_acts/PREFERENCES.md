## Why this bundle matters

Choosing between two options вАФ a restaurant, a meeting time, a tool, a plan вАФ is
one of the highest-frequency social moves in English. Yet most Vietnamese
learners reach for exactly the wrong chunk: **"I wantвА¶"**. "I want the Italian
place" sounds blunt, even childish, in a negotiation between adults. The fluent
move is a **preference**, not a demand вАФ `I'd ratherвА¶`, `I prefer X to Y`, or a
soft `I'm more of a вА¶ person`.

The second reason this bundle is load-bearing is **grammar**. The two anchor
patterns have traps that Vietnamese L1 walks straight into:

- `prefer` takes **`to`** between the two options вАФ never `*than*` (*"I prefer
  coffee **to** tea"*, not *"I prefer coffee than tea"*).
- `would rather` takes a **bare infinitive** вАФ never `*to*` (*"I'd rather
  **go**"*, not *"I'd rather to go"*).

Both traps come from the Vietnamese **"th√≠chвА¶h∆°n"** frame, which has no
preposition at all, so the learner either drops the linking word or calques the
wrong one. This bundle's whole payoff is fixing those two slots.

## 1. The two anchor patterns (drill these first)

### 1a. `I'd rather + bare infinitive`

`'d rather` = `would rather`. It means "would prefer to." The verb after it is
**bare** вАФ no `to`, no `-ing`, no `-s`.

> From `preferences_corpus.md`:
>
> | chunk | IPA |
> |---|---|
> | I'd rather + bare inf | /a…™d ЋИr…СЋР√∞…Щr/ UK ¬Ј /a…™d ЋИr√¶√∞…Щr/ US |
>
> Real attestations:
> - *"I'd rather have a beer."* вАФ Cambridge, `rather` вЖТ **would rather**.
> - *"I'd rather go to the concert than the play."* вАФ Oxford, *Express Yourself*.

The comparative frame is **`rather вА¶ than`** (parallel bare infinitives on both
sides):

> - *"I think I'd rather stay in than go out tonight."* вАФ Oxford, *Express
>   Yourself*.
> - *"I think I'd like to stay at home this evening rather than go out."* вАФ
>   Cambridge, `rather` вЖТ **rather than**.

**The Vietnamese trap:** learners insert `to` вЖТ *"I'd rather **to** go"*. There
is no `to` вАФ `rather` already carries the "prefer" meaning. Drill the chunk as
one unit: *I'd-rather-go*, *I'd-rather-stay*, *I'd-rather-not*.

### 1b. `I prefer X to Y`

`prefer` links the two options with **`to`** (standard) or **`over`** (esp. US,
informal). Never `*than*`.

> From `preferences_corpus.md`:
>
> | chunk | IPA |
> |---|---|
> | I prefer X to Y. | /a…™ pr…™ЋИf…ЬЋРr/ UK ¬Ј /a…™ pr…™ЋИf…ЭЋРr/ US |
>
> Real attestations:
> - *"I prefer red wine to white."* вАФ Cambridge, `prefer`.
> - *"I prefer jazz to rock music."* вАФ Oxford, `prefer` вЖТ *prefer something to
>   something*.
> - *"Many people prefer streaming over other forms of consuming media."* вАФ
>   Oxford, `prefer` вЖТ *prefer something over something*.

For an action (not a noun), use `would prefer to + infinitive`:

> - *"I'd prefer not to discuss this issue."* вАФ Cambridge, `prefer`.
> - *"I'd prefer to wait here."* вАФ Oxford, *Express Yourself*.

> **Why `to` and not `than`?** Cambridge's grammar panel states it directly: "We
> can use a prepositional phrase with **to** when we compare two things."
> `prefer than` is not a pattern any major dictionary lists вАФ it is an L1 calque.

## 2. The register ladder вАФ from blunt to soft

The same preference climbs a register ladder. Direct `I prefer` is fine among
friends but can sound categorical in a meeting; natives soften it. Knowing where
to land on the ladder is what separates "fluent" from "correct-but-rude."

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    BLUNT["<b>Blunt вАФ avoid</b><br/>I wantвА¶<br/>I like X more."]
    DIRECT["<b>Direct</b><br/>I prefer X to Y.<br/>I'd rather + bare inf."]
    SOFT["<b>Soft / hedged</b><br/>I'm more of a вА¶ person.<br/>I tend to preferвА¶<br/>I lean towardвА¶"]
    NEUT["<b>Neutral / no preference</b><br/>Either is fine.<br/>I don't mind.<br/>Happy either way."]
    BLUNT --> DIRECT --> SOFT --> NEUT
    style BLUNT fill:#fdecea,stroke:#c0392b,stroke-width:2px
    style DIRECT fill:#eafaf1,stroke:#27ae60,stroke-width:2px
    style SOFT fill:#eaf2f8,stroke:#2980b9
    style NEUT fill:#fef9e7,stroke:#f59e0b
```

> From `preferences_corpus.md` (the soft + neutral tiers, verbatim):
>
> - **Soft:** *"Industries still **tend to prefer** virgin raw materials to
>   recycled ones."* (Oxford). *"I'm **more of a** morning **person**."*
>   (Cambridge `person`).
> - **Neutral:** *"I'm **happy either way**."* (Oxford, *Express Yourself*, N.
>   American). *"I **don't really mind** whether we talk now or later."*
>   (Oxford, *Express Yourself*, British).

The neutral tier is the **social lubricant** вАФ when you genuinely don't care,
saying so fluently ("Either is fine") lets the other person decide without
awkwardness. Blank *"anything"* or *"I don't know"* reads as disengaged; *"Either
is fine"* reads as easy-going and confident.

## 3. The "I'd rather you didn't" subjunctive (advanced)

When the preference is about **someone else's** action, English uses the **past
subjunctive** after `would rather`:

> From `preferences_corpus.md`:
> - *"Well, actually, **I'd rather you didn't**."* вАФ Cambridge Advanced
>   Learner's Dictionary.

This is *not* past tense вАФ it's a polite, distanced way to say "please don't."
Vietnamese has no subjunctive mood, so learners default to *"I'd rather you
**don't**"*, which sounds abrupt. The fix: drill the fixed chunk `I'd rather you
didn't` as one unbreakable unit. (рЯФЧ See
[REQUESTING OFFERING](./REQUESTING_OFFERING.md) for the wider polite-request
system this belongs to.)

## 4. Pronunciation notes

- **`rather`** вАФ the /√∞/ is the sound Vietnamese replaces with /z/ or /d/. Keep
  the tongue between the teeth. UK /ЋИr…СЋР√∞…Щr/ vs US /ЋИr√¶√∞…Щr/ вАФ the **vowel**
  differs more than the consonant. рЯФЧ [TH SOUNDS](../pronunciation/TH_SOUNDS.md).
- **`either`** вАФ the famous transatlantic split: UK /ЋИa…™√∞…Щr/ (rhymes with
  *kite*), US /ЋИiЋР√∞…Щr/ (rhymes with *feet*). Pick one variety and stay
  consistent within a turn. The /√∞/ is the same trap as `rather`.
- **`prefer`** вАФ stress on the second syllable: **pre-FER** /pr…™ЋИf…ЬЋРr/. Learners
  often stress the first syllable (*PRE-fer*), which sounds off. The final /r/
  is released in US English, silent or light in UK.
- **`tend to`** вАФ reduces to /tend t…Щ/ (not /tend tuЋР/). The /d/ of *tend*
  links to the /t/ of *to* вАФ a consonant cluster Vietnamese tends to break with
  a schwa (*"ten-duh-to"*). рЯФЧ [LINKING](../pronunciation/LINKING.md),
  [FINAL CONSONANTS](../pronunciation/FINAL_CONSONANTS.md).

## 6. Vietnamese вЖТ English L1 pitfalls table

The "expert payoff." These are the specific interference traps a Vietnamese
speaker hits on expressing preference вАФ extend, don't replace, the seed rows
from the spec.

| Vietnamese trap (what you do) | English fix (what to do instead) |
|---|---|
| **"th√≠chвА¶h∆°n" has no preposition** вЖТ *"I prefer coffee tea"* or *"I prefer coffee than tea"* | Enforce **`prefer X to Y`** (standard) or **`over`** (US). Drill the slot: *coffee-**to**-tea*, *jazz-**to**-rock*. Never `than`. |
| **Adds `to` after `rather`** вЖТ *"I'd rather **to** go"* (expecting an infinitive marker) | `would rather` takes a **bare infinitive**. Drill *"I'd-rather-go"* as one chunk вАФ no `to`, ever. |
| **"th√≠ch h∆°n" calque with `like`** вЖТ *"I like coffee more tea"* (missing `than`) | The comparative is **`I like X better than Y`** (with `than`). Or skip it вАФ use `I prefer X to Y` instead. |
| **Overuses "I wantвА¶"** вЖТ *"I want Italian food"* (translating *t√іi muбїСn*) | Swap to a preference chunk: **`I'd ratherвА¶` / `I'd preferвА¶` / `I prefer X to Y`**. "I want" is a demand, not a preference вАФ it sounds blunt in a negotiation. |
| **Uses `than` after `rather`** вЖТ *"I'd rather go **to** the concert"* (parallel structure breaks) | The frame is **`rather вА¶ than`**: *"I'd rather go to the concert **than** the play."* Keep both sides parallel. |
| **No subjunctive** вЖТ *"I'd rather you **don't**"* (sounds abrupt) | Use the past subjunctive: **`I'd rather you didn't.`** Drill it as one fixed, unbreakable chunk. |
| **/√∞/ вЖТ /z/ or /d/** in `rather` / `either` вЖТ "razer" / "ee-zer" | Tongue **between the teeth** for /√∞/. рЯФЧ [TH SOUNDS](../pronunciation/TH_SOUNDS.md). Minimal pairs: *rather/laser*, *either/easer*. |
| **`either` vowel guess** вЖТ mixing UK /a…™/ and US /iЋР/ mid-turn | Pick **one variety** per turn. UK /ЋИa…™√∞…Щr/, US /ЋИiЋР√∞…Щr/. Don't blend into *"ay-ther"*. |
| **Breaks the `tend to` cluster** вЖТ *"ten-duh-to prefer"* (schwa insertion) | Keep the cluster tight: **/tend t…Щ/**. Link /d/вЖТ/t/ without a vowel. рЯФЧ [LINKING](../pronunciation/LINKING.md). |
| **Blank *"anything"*/"I don't know"* for no-preference** вЖТ reads as disengaged | Use the neutral tier: **`Either is fine.` / `I don't mind.` / `Happy either way.`** вАФ signals easy-going, not checked-out. |

## How to practise this bundle (the daily 20 min)

1. **READ** (5 min) вАФ this guide, ¬І1вАУ¬І3.
2. **SHADOW** (7 min) вАФ open `preferences.html`, drill the 8 flip cards + the
   two-option role-play **aloud**, exaggerating the `to`/bare-infinitive slot,
   then relaxing.
3. **PRODUCE** (8 min) вАФ the writing task: write **2 preference sentences** (one
   `I'd ratherвА¶`, one `I prefer X to Y`). Read them aloud, recording yourself;
   check the grammar slot is right (`to` after prefer, bare inf after rather).
