## A. Subject-line chunks (clear + concise)

The subject line is the first thing a reader sees. Oxford University Press's
email guide states: "keep the subject line meaningful and concise so they don't
hit the delete button." The military BLUF convention (HBR, Sehgal 2016) goes
further — use keyword tags: **ACTION**, **INFO**, **DECISION**, **SIGN**.

## D. BLUF — the Bottom Line Up Front statement

The BLUF convention: state the key information — the ask, the decision, the
action required — in the **first sentence** of the body, then give context.

| English chunk | meaning | IPA | source URL | frequency rank | accent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

> **Verification note:** The BLUF example sentence ("BLUF: I need you to
> approve both the design and content of the attached flyer by noon on August
> 10.") is attested verbatim on the Wikipedia "BLUF (communication)" article
