## A. Acknowledge — name the fault (the apology opener)

The first move of a professional apology email is **explicit acknowledgement**:
name what went wrong, in your own words, without "if", "may", or passive voice.
Three registers of the same move, from most to least formal.

## B. Reason — give a *brief*, blame-free cause

The reason is one or two sentences — never longer than the apology. It answers
"why?" without pointing outward. Two openers carry almost the whole load.

| English chunk | meaning | IPA | source URL | frequency rank | accent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

> **Verification note:** `unfortunately` UK /ʌnˈfɔː.tʃən.ət.li/, US

## C. Responsibility + fix — own it, then repair

The move that separates a real apology from a non-apology: name the fix and who
delivers it. The Vietnamese instinct is to stop at the "sorry"; English demands
the **forward-looking repair**.
