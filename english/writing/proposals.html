★ primaryWriting task — mini-proposal

Write a mini-proposal: one sentence per move — problem + solution + benefits + ask. Put at least one number in the benefits. Then reveal the model answer.

1 problem 2 solution 3 benefits (number!) 4 ask + next step

Model — four moves, one number in the benefits, one clear ask + dated next step:

Problem: The challenge we're facing is that turnover hit 22% last quarter — well above the industry average.

Solution: I propose that we pilot a hybrid-work policy for the engineering team.

Benefits: The benefits of this approach would be a projected 20% reduction in churn and higher retention of senior staff.

Ask: I'm seeking approval to run a 90-day pilot. Next steps: I draft the policy by Friday for your sign-off.

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Inductive structure buries the recommendation — context for 3 paragraphs, ask at the end, softlyDeductive: recommendation in the first or second sentence. BLUF — "I propose that we pilot remote work" — then justify.
Hedges the ask away — "Nếu được thì…" → "If possible, maybe you could consider…"Make one explicit ask: "I'm seeking approval to…" / "Could we pilot this…". One softener max; let the verb carry it.
Modesty → under-quantifies benefits — "nó giúp một chút" / lists traits not numbersQuantify the payoff: "This would result in a 20% reduction". No number = no credibility.
No concrete next step — ends "mong nhận được phản hồi" (please reply)End with one dated next step: "Next steps: I draft the spec by Friday." The ask is what makes it a proposal.
Face-saving softens the problem — won't name the difficulty plainlyName it in sentence one: "The challenge we're facing is…". Framing it as a shared challenge (not blame) keeps face while staying direct.
Generic trait-list, no evidence — "hardworking, responsible, careful"Replace traits with one quantified result: "cut churn by 18%". Evidence > adjectives.
Translates L1 formal filler — "Kính gửi… Trận trọng…" word-for-word into stiff EnglishUse the proposal register, not ceremonial filler: "I propose… The benefits… I'm seeking approval…". Formal ≠ ornate.
Over-hedges with "I think / maybe" — "I think maybe it could possibly help"Calibrate to one hedge: "This would likely result in…". Three hedges in one clause reads as no confidence.
