Write a delegation message (task + deadline + support). Use Could you… by… and Let me know if you hit any roadblocks. Then reveal the model answer.

Model (all three moves: assign → scope + deadline → support):

Hi Linh, Could you take the lead on the Q3 client report? (move 1: assign — modal question, not imperative)

You'll be responsible for the data section, and the deadline is end of day Friday. (move 2: scope + deadline — explicit)

Let me know if you hit any roadblocks — let's check in on this on Thursday. (move 3: support + follow-up)

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Top-down imperative — "You must do this" / "Do this report" (VN hierarchy, power-distance ≈70)Swap the imperative for a modal question (Could you…?) or the I'd like you to… frame.
Too vague to save face — "Please help with the report" (no scope, no deadline)Name the deliverable + deadline explicitly: "You'll be responsible for the Q3 report, by Friday."
Omits the deadline — assumes it is "understood" from contextAlways attach a time: "…by end of day Friday," "…before our Monday standup." Low-context norms: if you don't say it, it isn't there.
Omits ownership language — uses "help with" instead of own / take the lead onUse ownership verbs: "I'd like you to own…", "Could you take the lead on…?"
No support offer — dumps the task and disappearsAlways close with a support line: "Let me know if you hit any roadblocks." Silence reads as indifference.
Struggles delegating to seniors/peers (age/status hierarchy)Use hedged ownership: "I was wondering if you could…" / "Would you be able to…?"
Drops the final /k/ in roadblock → "roadblo" (VN has no /bl/+/k/ coda cluster)Hold the /bl/ cluster tight then release the /k/ audibly: /ˈrəʊd.blɒk/.
/θ/ → /t/ in the / Thursday → "te lead"Tongue-between-teeth for /θ ð/: the /ðə/, Thursday /ˈθɜːz.deɪ/.
Flat intonation on Could you…? (VN is tonal; word-tone, not pragmatic-intonation)Give Could you…? a polite rise-then-fall: rise on Could, slight rise at the end to mark it as a request.
Uses must / have to for polite delegation (sounds like a legal contract)Reserve must for hard rules. For delegation use Could you…? / I'd like you to….
