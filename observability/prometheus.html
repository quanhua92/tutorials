Prometheus — pull-based metrics, TSDB & PromQL 📖 PROMETHEUS.md 📄 prometheus.py

A pull-based metrics warehouse: scrape /metrics on a clock, file numbers into a TSDB (Head → WAL → 2h blocks), query with PromQL. The two expert primitives are rate() (counter slope + reset correction) and histogram_quantile() (interpolate a quantile out of cumulative buckets). The killer trap is cardinality explosion. ① PromQL playground — what each query produces

What this shows: five canonical PromQL queries and exactly what each evaluates to. All numbers are recomputed in JS from the same inputs as prometheus.py Section D. Click a query to see its inputs + result.
rate() = (last - first + reset_correction) / window. Reset correction = sum of pre-reset values whenever the counter decreases.

② histogram_quantile — how buckets become a quantile

What this shows: a cumulative histogram (50 observations). Drag the quantile slider. The bucket containing rank = φ·total highlights; Prometheus linearly interpolates inside it. +Inf bucket is the safety net (unbounded).
Default buckets: .005 .01 .025 .05 .1 .25 .5 1 2.5 5 10 (+Inf). One histogram = 14 series per label combo.

③ Cardinality calculator — the label-set is the budget

What this shows: active series = the PRODUCT of each label's cardinality. Each series costs ~1.5 KB of RAM (index + head chunks + labels). Add one toxic label and watch the series — and RAM — explode.
~1.5 KB/series. 1M series ≈ 1.5 GB just for the index. A histogram multiplies by 14.

④ Two clocks — scrape_interval vs evaluation_interval

What this shows: Prometheus runs two independent tickers. scrape_interval pulls targets; evaluation_interval runs recording + alert rules. They are decoupled. A rule evaluated at t=30s sees whatever samples exist then.
