LTX-Video — the ~1500× VAE compression 📖 LTX_VIDEO.md 📄 ltx_video.py

LTX-Video is fast because its Video VAE compresses the pixel buffer ~1500× before the Diffusion Transformer runs — so each diffusion step works on a tiny latent grid (81K positions, not 41.8M). Drag resolution & frames below and watch the compression, the VRAM budget, and the real-time factor recompute live. The gold-check pins the canonical 720×480×121 compression to the exact .py output.

① The compression pipeline — pixel → VAE → latent → DiT → decode

What this shows: the pixel buffer a U-Net would denoise directly, vs the tiny latent grid the LTX DiT actually works in. 8× on EACH spatial axis (64×) + 8× temporal = 512× pure grid reduction; the headline ~1500× also folds in the RGB channels. Same formulas as ltx_video.py Section A.
The size bars are log-scaled (each step ×10) so the pixel bar and the latent bar are both visible — linearly, the latent sliver is invisible. The latent tensor is not 81K scalars: each position carries C≈128 channels, so the true byte compression is ~12×, not 1500×.

Generation time scales with latent positions × steps ÷ GPU speed. “Faster than real-time” (RTF ≥ 1) means gen ≤ playback — the H100 claim. On an RTX 4060 it is “<60 s” (RTF ≈ 0.1), still ~7–50× faster than Wan/Hunyuan.

③ The video-model landscape — LTX vs Wan vs HunyuanVideo

What this shows: same target clip (720×480×121, ~5 s video). The spread is ~2 orders of magnitude in VRAM and speed. Same numbers as ltx_video.py Section E. RTF = duration ÷ gen time (≥1 = faster than real-time).
LTX is the only one that fits a consumer 8 GB card and finishes in under a minute. Wan 2.2 (higher quality, heavier) closes the gap with Lightning/TeaCache/GGUF — see the wan_video bundle.
