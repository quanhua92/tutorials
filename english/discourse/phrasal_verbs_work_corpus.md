## A. The 8 core workplace phrasal verbs (the survival set)

These are the high-frequency verbs that make **workplace English sound native**.
Vietnamese has no phrasal-verb structure at all (every verb is one morpheme), so
learners either reach for the stiff Latinate single verb (*investigate*,
*postpone*, *contact*) or split the object wrong. The result sounds formal and
stilted in a fast office exchange. Each verb below is **figurative/idiomatic**:
its meaning is NOT the literal sum of verb + particle — *follow up* is not
"pursue upward," *roll out* is not "unroll a mat."

## B. Literal vs figurative + separable/inseparable

A phrasal verb is a **single semantic unit**. The particle does not keep its
literal spatial meaning. This is the core trap: a learner who translates
word-by-word gets nonsense. The **grammar** (where the object goes) is equally
critical — Vietnamese has no verb+particle structure, so learners split the wrong
verbs or fail to split the separable ones.

| Phrasal verb | ❌ Literal reading (the trap) | ✅ Real workplace meaning | Grammar | Example with object |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **follow up** | to go behind, pursuing upward | to pursue/check on a previous action | **separable** | *follow **it** up* / *follow up **on it*** |
| **reach out** | to extend one's arm outward | to make contact / communicate | **inseparable** | *reach out **to them*** |
| **roll out** | to unroll a mat/carpet | to launch / introduce a product | **separable** | *roll **it** out* |
| **push back** | to shove something backward | to resist / delay / oppose | **separable** | *push **it** back* / *push back **against it*** |
| **wrap up** | to enclose in wrapping paper | to finish / complete | **separable** | *wrap **it** up* |
| **bring up** | to carry upward | to mention / raise a topic | **separable** | *bring **it** up* |
| **look into** | to direct one's eyes inside | to investigate / examine | **inseparable** | *look into **it*** (NOT ~~look it into~~) |
| **come up with** | to move upward with | to invent / produce an idea | **inseparable** | *come up with **something*** |

> **The separable/inseparable rule:** for **separable** verbs, a noun object can
> go **between** the verb and the particle (*"bring **the issue** up"*) or
> **after** (*"bring up **the issue**"*). A **pronoun** object must go
> **between**: *"bring **it** up"* — never ~~"bring up it"~~. For
> **inseparable** verbs, the object **always follows the whole phrase**: *"look
> into **it**"* — never ~~"look it into"~~.

> **Vietnamese L1 note:** Vietnamese has **no verb+particle structure** — every
> verb is a single morpheme (*theo dõi* = follow up, *liên hệ* = contact,
*điều tra* = investigate, *trì hoãn* = postpone). So there is no mental model for
> "two words = one verb" AND no mental model for "where does the object go?"
> Learners either (a) avoid the phrasal verb entirely and use the stiff Latinate
> synonym, or (b) split the wrong verbs. Both sound broken in a fast office.
