Hi team,

As discussed in today's Q4 planning meeting, here's a quick recap and our action items. recap opener → signals "this is the record"

Decisions:

— We launch the new pricing tier on Nov 1.

— We agreed that the beta stays invite-only. records what we agreed

Action items: action · OWNER · by DATE — all three present

— Draft the pricing proposal (Mai) by Thu, Oct 10.

— Share last quarter's numbers (Quan) by Fri, Oct 11.

— Confirm beta customer list (Linh) by Wed, Oct 9.

Next stand-up: Mon, Oct 7, 9:00. Thanks all!
Quan

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Sends no follow-up — a verbal "em sẽ làm" felt bindingSend a recap within 24 hours. Open with As discussed / Following our meeting, then label the rest.
Writes a narrative wall instead of scannable bulletsBuild a skeleton: Decisions: / Action items: / Next steps: — one-line bullets the reader scans.
Action with no owner — "we'll prepare the report"Assign a name, never "we": "Draft the report (Mai)". An ownerless action is nobody's job.
Action with no deadline — "we'll send it soon"Attach a specific date: "by Thu, Oct 10". "Soon" is not a deadline.
No accountability bullet — recap lists what was discussed, never who does whatConvert every point into [task] (owner) by [date]. Three components per bullet: action · owner · date.
Drops the /t/ in "discussed" → "discuss" (loses past tense)Drill discuss /dɪˈskʌs/ → discussed /dɪˈskʌst/. The -ed is /t/, not a syllable.
De-voices /ʒ/ → /ʃ/ in "decision" → "de-ci-shun"Keep vocal cords buzzing through /ʒ/: /dɪˈsɪʒ.ən/. It vibrates, unlike /ʃ/.
Pro-drop in writing → "Will send report Friday."Supply full subject + verb: "I will send the report on Friday."
"Please find attached the meeting minute" (dated, singular)Use modern phrasing: "Here's a quick recap" + "Action items:" list. Drop pursuant to / minutes.
