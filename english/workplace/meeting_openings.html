6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Over-defers / waits to be called — freezes when the turn reaches youTake the turn within ~1–2 seconds. Lead with the chunk, not an apology. Silence reads as unprepared, not respectful.
Overly elaborate formal openings — "Respectfully, I would like to humbly begin…"English openings are one clause each: welcome → start → agenda → handover. Cut the ceremony.
Hedges the start with apology — "Sorry, can I start?"Don't ask permission to chair. "Let's get started" is a polite assertion, not a command.
Drops final consonants → "Let's get start-it", "thank", "kic-off"Release every final: /ˈstɑːtɪd/, /θæŋks/, /kɪk ɒf/. Drill Final Consonants.
Mis-stresses agenda → /ˈædʒendə/ or /əˈdʒenədə/Stress the second syllable: a-GEN-da /əˈdʒendə/. The /dʒ/ is one sound — no inserted vowel.
/θ/ → /t/ → "tanks everyone" for thanksTongue between the teeth for /θ/: thanks /θæŋks/.
Translates "xin phép / cho phép" literally → "With your permission, I would like to open…"No English equivalent at meeting-open. Permission-asking belongs to interrupting, not opening. Use "Let's get started."
Names the agenda but never hands over — waits for someone to volunteerAlways end the opening with an explicit handover by name: "Mai, would you like to kick us off?"
Stresses grammar words — "Thanks for making the time"Keep for/to/the weak: /fə(r)/, /tə/, /ðə/. Stress only content words.
