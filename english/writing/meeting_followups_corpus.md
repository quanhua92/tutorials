## A. Recap openers (the opening line of a meeting follow-up)

The first half of the bundle: the line that **signals "this is the recap"**.
English meeting follow-ups open with a fixed formula that *refers back* to the
meeting — *As discussed*, *Following our meeting*, *To summarize* — so the reader
knows instantly this is the written record, not a new ask. Oxford lists **as
discussed** as a phrase under *discuss*, and Cambridge Business English lists
**To recap** / **Here's a recap of** as the meeting-closing formula.

## B. Action items & accountability structure (the owner + deadline format)

The second half — and the heart of an English meeting follow-up: a **scannable
list where every action has an owner and a deadline**. The genre convention is
mechanical: Cambridge defines an **action item** as *"a job for a particular
person or group to do following a meeting"*, and meeting-management sources
(Otter.ai, Superhuman) formalize the format as **action · owner · date**. This
is the structure Vietnamese L1 learners most often omit.

## C. Decisions & meeting-structure labels (the section headers)

English meeting recaps are **skeleton documents** — the reader scans labelled
sections, not paragraphs. The standard headers — *Decisions:*, *We agreed that*,
*Parking lot:* — are borrowed from facilitation practice. Cambridge lists
**decision** with the collocations *make / reach / come to a decision*; the
**parking lot** is the facilitation method for deferring off-topic items
(Facilitator School, Lucid Meetings).
