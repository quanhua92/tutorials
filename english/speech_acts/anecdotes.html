5Writing task

Write a 4-sentence anecdote using past simple + past continuous + past perfect (one of each). Then reveal the model answer.

Model (past continuous = background, past simple = action, past perfect = earlier event):

So, this one time, I was walking home from work. past continuous — the background

It all started when I realized I had left my phone at the office. past simple + past perfect — the earlier event

Suddenly, out of nowhere, it started to pour. past simple — the turning point

Long story short, I ran back and got totally soaked. past simple — the payoff

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
No past-tense morphology → "Yesterday I go to the park."Enforce -ed/irregular past on every narrative verb. Pair a time anchor ("Last week…") with audible past forms.
No past-perfect-vs-past-simple contrast → one flat past, flashback/background lostPast continuous for the background ("I was walking…"), past perfect for the earlier event ("…I had forgotten my keys").
Tells the story in the present: "So I walk home, then I see a dog…"Default to past tenses for anecdotes. Only use the dramatic present once your past forms are flawless.
Tense drift — starts past, slips into present, slips backPick one frame and hold it. Re-read and circle every verb; they should all sit in the past family.
No "story glue" — beats separated by silence or "and… and…"Insert a sequencer between beats: So then…, And then…, Suddenly…
Tells it flatly — no Suddenly, no You'll never guess, no The funny thing is…Add one drama marker per turning point and one punchline marker at the payoff.
Drops the -ed → started → "star", happened → "happen" — past tense vanishesExaggerate the -ed release: started, happened. 🔗 Final Consonants §3.
No punchline — trails off with "…yeah, that's all"Land with The funny thing is… or Long story short… so the listener gets the payoff.
