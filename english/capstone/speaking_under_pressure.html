5Writing task

Write a composed deferral to a tough question you can't answer (buy time + honest deferral). Then reveal the model answer.

Tough question: "Can you give me the exact revenue figure for your last project's first quarter?"

Model (buy time → honest deferral → pivot to what you know):

"That's a fair question." — buy ~2 sec, look composed

"I don't have the exact figure in front of me, but…" — name the gap honestly

"…the project grew the user base by roughly 40% that quarter." — give the shape you do know

"I'd need to verify the precise number, so let me come back to you with it." — confident deferral, no apology

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Face-saving (thể diện) → panic or freeze under a tough question (fear of looking incompetent)Run the arc: a 2-second pause + "That's a fair question" looks composed, not ignorant.
Bluffs a guessed answer to avoid "I don't know" (caught out = credibility gone)Honest deferral: "I don't have the exact figure, but…" / "I'd need to verify that." Composed honesty beats a panicked bluff.
"Tôi không biết" translated bluntly → bare "I don't know" (abrupt / dismissive)Soften into a deferral — never the bare phrase: "I don't have the exact figure, but…" or "Let me come back to that."
Fills silence with "uhm… uhm…" or a rushed answer (silence feels like failure)Fill it deliberately: "Let me take a moment" / "Give me a second to think."
Over-apologises → "I'm so sorry, I don't know" (leaks weakness)Defer confidently, no apology: "I'd need to verify that." Not knowing is normal.
Answers in one rushed, unstructured sentence under loadUse a structure: buy-time → give what you know → defer the rest.
Pronunciation collapses under stress — finals dropped (nerves undo Phase 0 drills)Slow down the first sentence. Breathe. Release every final consonant. 🔗 Final Consonants
Avoids the boundary statement ("That's outside my area") — naming a limit feels like failureName the limit, then pivot: "That's outside my area, but I'm a fast learner."
