RoPE Context Extension — stretch the frequency ladder 📖 CONTEXT_EXTENSION.md 📄 context_extension.py

Four methods to extend a model's trained context window, newest strictly better: linear (divide all positions → blurs local dims) → NTK-aware (adjust the base θ, high-freq preserved) → YaRN (band-wise piecewise + attention factor) → dynamic NTK (per-token, streaming). Drag the θ slider and toggle the method to watch the frequency grid reshape. The gold-check pins NTK to the exact .py output.

① Frequency ladder — θ + method → reshaped grid

What this shows: the RoPE frequency ladder freq_d = 1/θ^(2d/D) for the selected head_dim, reshaped by the chosen method. The left bar in each rung is the ORIGINAL frequency; the overlaid bar is the SCALED frequency. High-frequency dims (left, short wavelength, local structure) should stay tall under NTK/YaRN; linear squashes them all equally.
Bars are log-scaled (frequencies span orders of magnitude). Dim d=0 (left) = highest frequency = local structure. Dim d=D/2-1 (right) = lowest frequency = long-range structure.

② Side-by-side — original 4K ceiling vs extended grid

What this shows: the angle each dim reaches at the trained ceiling (L_orig·freq) vs at the extended target (L_target·scaled_freq). A good method keeps the extended long-range angle near the trained ceiling (interpolation) while leaving the high-freq angle free to grow (extrapolation).
"angle match %" = how close the extended angle is to the original trained ceiling (100% = exact interpolation back into the trained range). NTK/YaRN hit ~100% on the low-freq dims while leaving high-freq untouched; linear forces every dim to exactly 100% (which is exactly why it blurs the local dims).

③ Method comparison — high-freq preservation

What this shows: for the current θ/D/scale, how much each method preserves the highest-frequency dim d=0 (local structure). 100% = untouched; 25% (= 1/scale) = fully blurred. This single number is why NTK/YaRN beat linear.
The NTK row is the gold-checked method: θ_new = θ·scale^(D/(D-2)). YaRN blends band-by-band; linear divides everything uniformly.
