6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Fakes understanding to save face — smiles, nods, says vâng / dạ instead of admitting they didn't followAsk immediately. The face-saving move in English is the early clarification (Sorry, I didn't catch that), not the late reveal.
Uses "What?" (transfer of L1 cái gì?) — sounds rude / impatientAlways front a politeness particle: Sorry? or a hedged modal Could you…?. Treat bare What? as a warning buzzer.
Says "Yes" to look competent when checked — the vâng reflexThe competent move is the opposite: Not quite — could you elaborate on…? Asking shows engagement; fake-yes shows the gap.
Skips the paraphrase-check — goes to "I don't understand" (too broad) or "Yes"Try a targeted paraphrase-check first: So you mean…? It narrows the gap and lets the speaker repair exactly the broken part.
Mixes repeat and explain — asks "Could you repeat that?" when the problem was the meaningDiagnose first: hearing → Sorry, I didn't catch that (same words again); meaning → What do you mean by…? (different words / example).
Reaches for "Pardon?" in US English (from UK textbooks) — sounds stiff / datedPardon? is chiefly UK. In US, default to Sorry? or Could you repeat that?. Save I beg your pardon? for formal/UK.
Drops finals on the request itself → "Sorry, I didn't ca' that" (no /tʃ/), "Could you repea' that?" (no /t/), "you've lo' me" (no /st/)Release every final. The /tʃ/ in catch, the /t/ in repeat, the /st/ in lost carry the request's meaning.
Vietnamese /θ/ → /t/, /ð/ → /z/ in catch /kætʃ/ and that /ðæt/The /tʃ/ in catch is an affricate (t + sh), not a plain /t/. The /ð/ in that is TH — tongue-between-teeth.
Says "What do you mean?" with no by… — blunter, can sound like a challengePin a specific word after by: What do you mean by 'scalable'? The by softens it and shows exactly what to rephrase.
Uses "Could you elaborate?" in casual chat → sounds overly formal among friendsReserve elaborate for meetings / emails / client. In casual chat, use What do you mean by…? / I'm not sure I follow.
