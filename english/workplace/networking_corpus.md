## A. Openers ‚ÄĒ breaking the ice at an event (the "why are you here?" move)

English networking small talk is **transactional-first**: the opener establishes
*why each person is at the event* within the first 30 seconds. The single most
attested networking opener is **"What brings you here?"** ‚ÄĒ the OED glosses it
"for what reason have you come? / why are you here? ‚Ä¶ now often used as a
greeting or rhetorical question".

## B. The "what do you do?" exchange (the occupation move)

Once the opener lands, the conversation pivots to occupation. **"What do you
do?"** (short for *what do you do for a living?*) is the standard English
networking question ‚ÄĒ it is *not* "what is your job?" (that sounds blunt) and
*not* "what is your work?". The three reply frames below cover the whole
register range.
