AVL Tree (self-balancing BST) — interactive AVL_TREE.md avl_tree.py

An AVL tree keeps every node's balance factor (height(left) − height(right)) in {-1, 0, +1}. The instant an insert pushes a node to ±2, a rotation pulls it level. Step through inserting [10,20,30,40,50,25] below — three rotations fire (RR, RR, RL). All numbers are recomputed live in JS from the identical logic as avl_tree.py.

③ Height: AVL (≤1.44·log₂N) vs unbalanced BST chain

Inserting sorted data into a plain BST makes a degenerate chain of height N (O(N) search). The AVL tree's rotations cap the height at ~1.44·log₂N. Drag N and watch the gap blow up.

④ AVL vs Red-Black — the trade-off

Read it twice: AVL's rigidity buys the shortest tree (fastest lookup) at the cost of more rotations on update. That is why read-heavy workloads pick AVL, while most stdlibs default to Red-Black.
