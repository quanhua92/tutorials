Model (right opener for the day + a follow-up each):

(Mon) How was your weekend? → Oh yeah? What did you get up to?

(Tue/Wed) So, how's your week been? Been busy? → No way!

(Thu/Fri) Any plans for the weekend? → That sounds nice.

(any day) Weird weather lately, huh? → I know, right?

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Skips small talk entirely — jumps straight to businessInsert a 30–60 sec small-talk buffer first: "How was your weekend?" / "Been busy?"
"Ăn cơm chưa?" ("have you eaten?") as a greeting ≈ "how are you?"Don't translate it — in EN "Have you eaten?" reads as a literal lunch invitation. Use "How's it going?" instead.
"Đi đâu đấy?" / "Where are you going?" as a passing greetingAvoid — sounds like an interrogation. Use "How's your day going?" for a passing hello.
Asks age / marital status / income / children early (normal & caring in VN)Park these — intrusive-in-EN until you know someone well. Lead with weather / weekend / plans / travel.
Stops at "Fine" / "Good" with no return question → exchange diesMemorize reply-and-return: short positive reply → one detail → return question. "Pretty good, thanks. You?"
Drops the -s on "weekend" / "plans" / "sounds"Enforce every -s & final cluster. plans /plænz/, sounds /saʊndz/. 🔗 Final Consonants bundle.
Pro-drop / missing subject — "Is good." / "Been to Hanoi?"Supply the subject: "It's good." / "Have you been to Hanoi?" (the opener "Been busy?" is the one accepted ellipsis.)
Gives a 3-minute monologue to a phatic "How was your weekend?"The phatic question wants a 15-second answer: one positive word + one detail + return question.
Misreads the tag question "…, huh?" / "…, right?" as a real questionTag questions are invitations to agree, not data requests. Reply "I know, right?" / "Yeah, isn't it?"
