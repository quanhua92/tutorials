## A. Dates ‚ÄĒ the OOO core (when you're out + when you return)

The spine of every auto-reply: **state the absence, then state the return.**
English business writing treats explicit dates as professional, not blunt ‚ÄĒ the
sender needs to know *exactly* how long you are unreachable. Cambridge defines
the whole genre in one line: *OOO = "a reply that can be automatically sent to
an email that you have received when you are not in the office and cannot reply
to it."* The two templated openers below are the highest-frequency phrasings in
real OOO corpora (Indeed, LanguageTool, TextExpander, NetHunt).

## B. Access level (how reachable you are)

The second mandatory slot: **tell the sender whether to expect a reply while you
are away.** Three calibrated levels, from "do not wait" to "I may answer":
*no access* (strictest), *limited access* (the default vacation line), and
*checking periodically* (you will triage). Omitting this line is the most common
reason a sender escalates prematurely. The phrasings below are attested across
Indeed, Exclaimer, NetHunt, TextExpander, and Chaffey College auto-reply
guidance.

## C. Coverage / urgent matters (who handles things while you're gone)

The third mandatory slot: **route anything urgent to a named colleague.** This is
the line that protects the sender (they get help now) *and* you (you are not the
bottleneck). The polite frame is *For urgent matters, please contact‚Ä¶* ‚ÄĒ the
modal *please* + the hedge *urgent matters* keeps it from reading as "don't
bother me." The *in my absence* variant is the formal, slightly more deferential
twin. All phrasings below are attested across Grammarly, Indeed, LanguageTool,
LiveAgent, Exclaimer, and Nimblework OOO templates.

## D. Return + thanks (closing the auto-reply)

The final two lines every OOO message needs: **set the reply expectation**
(when / whether you will answer) and **acknowledge the sender** (the courtesy
"thanks for your email"). The *respond when I return* line is the polite way to
say "do not expect a reply before [date]"; the *thanks* opener is the standard
face-saving acknowledgement that softens the auto-reply's inherent brusqueness.
All phrasings attested across Indeed, LanguageTool, Nimblework, Making Business
Matter, and IU auto-reply politeness research.
