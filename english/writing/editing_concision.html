5Writing task

Take 3 wordy / passive sentences and edit them lean + active. Then reveal the model answers.

Model — cut filler, de-nominalize, go active:

before: We are writing in order to inform you, due to the fact that the server is down.

after: We're writing to tell you the server is down because… (in order to → to; due to the fact that → because)

before: A decision was made to make a decision to postpone the launch.

after: I decided to postpone the launch. (passive → active; make a decision → decide)

before: It is recommended that consideration be given to a large number of options.

after: I recommend we consider many options. (it is recommended → I recommend; give consideration to → consider; a large number of → many)

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Translation inflation — calques ornate L1 register: "due to the fact that", "in order to"Cut to the lean word. Ask "can one word do this?" — because, to, now.
Ornate = respectful (Vietnamese formal writing rewards elaboration)Re-tune: in English, brevity = respect for the reader's time. Elaboration reads as clutter.
Passive-as-polite — "it is recommended", "a decision was made" to sound "professional"Default to active + first person: "I recommend", "I decided". Active is accountable, not rude.
Piles on nominalizations — "make a decision", "have a discussion"De-nominalize: unwrap the noun back into its verb — decide, discuss, consider.
Wordiness from L1 norms — "in the event that" for "nếu", "a large number of" for "nhiều"Reach for the one-word equivalent: if, many. The plain word is the professional word.
Fear that concise = rude / unfinished; pads length to "sound complete"Trust the lean version. A short, clear English sentence reads as confident and finished. Cut, then stop.
Hides the actor to be humble — omits "I/we" ("was decided") like Vietnamese often drops the subjectName the actor: "We decided", "I suggest". Dropping the subject sounds evasive, not humble.
Over-uses make/have/give + noun ("make an improvement") as a one-size frameUse the specific verb: improve, consider. The weak-verb + noun frame is the #1 clutter source.
