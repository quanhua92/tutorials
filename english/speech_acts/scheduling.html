6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Drops the proposing chunk — "Tuesday OK?" / "We meet Tuesday?" (VN marks politeness by tone)Use the full chunk: Does Tuesday work? / How about Monday?. The auxiliary + verb is the politeness.
Blunt rescheduling — "Can change time?" (VN "đổi giờ" has no softening auxiliary)Use the soft set: Can we push it to 3? / Can we reschedule? Can we…? frames it as negotiable.
Over-apologises on a routine reschedule — "I am so sorry, please forgive me"One light sorry + the relocation chunk: "Sorry, can we push it to 3?" A routine change is not a crisis.
Preposition errors — "in Tuesday", "on 3 o'clock", or no preposition (VN has no time-preposition morphology)Drill the rule: clock → at (at 3), day → on (on Tuesday). Cambridge Grammar At, on and in (time).
12h/24h ambiguity — "3 o'clock" with no AM/PM (VN uses sáng/chiều/tối)Always add AM/PM or in the afternoon when ambiguous: 3pm, 3 in the afternoon.
free vs available — uses only available (formal) or "free time" (noun, wrong)Use the adjective free for spoken scheduling: I'm free then. Reserve available for written/formal.
"Wednesday" → "Wed-nes-day" (3 syllables) or drops /d/ → "Wennessay"Standard form is two syllables: /ˈwenzdeɪ/ ("WENZ-day"), first d silent. 🔗 FINAL CONSONANTS.
"push back" vs "bring forward" confusion — assumes push back = earlierpush back = later (delay); bring forward = earlier. To avoid ambiguity, name the time: Can we move it to 3?
Pro-drop confirmation — "Is good for me?" (VN-style, no subject)Supply the full structure: That works for me. / That's good for me. Never drop the subject.
