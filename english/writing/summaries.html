5Writing task — the executive brief

primary mode

Compress a paragraph into an executive brief: one BLUF line + ≤3 "Key takeaways" bullets + one "Recommendation:". Then reveal the model.

Source paragraph (compress this):

Over the last quarter the team ran four pilot workshops in Hanoi and Da Nang with 60 enterprise customers. Attendance was strong and feedback forms were mostly positive, though several managers said the sessions ran long. The Hanoi cohort asked for a shorter half-day version, while Da Nang preferred hands-on labs. Costs came in 12% under budget. We are now deciding whether to roll the programme out company-wide next quarter or refine the format first.

Model — BLUF + ≤3 bullets + one recommendation:

BLUF: The bottom line is the pilot succeeded and we should refine the format before a company-wide rollout.

Key takeaways:

  • 4 pilots, 60 customers, feedback mostly positive; ran long.
  • Cities split on format (Hanoi: half-day; Da Nang: hands-on labs).
  • Costs 12% under budget — room to invest in a v2.

Recommendation: Build a shorter half-day format with optional labs, then roll out company-wide next quarter.

Notice: the decision is line 1, not the last line. ≤3 bullets, not five. One recommendation, not a list of options.

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Narrative wind-up — retells the story in order, saves the conclusion for the last sentenceBLUF first. Put the decision in sentence 1: "The bottom line is…" The build-up goes below, if at all.
Buries the point — reader reaches line 8 before learning why they're readingLead with "The bottom line is…" / "The key takeaway is…". Line 1 alone should carry the answer.
No recommendation — ends on "those are the facts" (VN summary = describe, don't advise)End on one recommendation: "Recommendation:" / "Proposed action:". A brief without one is unfinished.
Over-detail / can't prioritize — lists 5–10 bullets equally because cutting feels incompleteCut to ≤3 bullets under "Key takeaways:". Isolate the major points; don't re-list.
Prose paragraphs over bullets — writes the summary as flowing text (school-essay default)Use scannable bullets. Executives skim; a wall of prose hides the points.
Translation inflation in the opener — "In order to summarize the aforementioned…"Use the lean opener: "In summary," / "To summarize,". Cut filler.
Hedge instead of decide — "It might perhaps be suggested that we consider…" to sound humbleState it directly: "Recommendation: we proceed with Option A." A hedge weakens a brief.
Repeats background the reader already knows — re-narrates context out of politenessCarry the decision and the delta, not the history. Assume the reader knows the context.
