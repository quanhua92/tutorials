## A. The openers (high-frequency conversation-starters)

attested Monday-morning opener in English-speaking workplaces is **"How was your
weekend?"** ‚ÄĒ confirmed across the British Council small-talk guide, the EF
EnglishLive "Topics to Avoid" article (which lists it as the model *safe*
opener), and 81 YouGlish US clips of the exact phrase. The weekday lookahead
opener **"Any plans for the weekend?"** is the Thursday/Friday mirror.

## B. Safe-topic anchor words (the four universal safe zones)

Small talk works because a small set of **neutral** topics is pre-approved as
safe across English-speaking cultures: **the weather, the weekend, plans, and
travel/food/sports**. These are confirmed as the canonical safe set by the
British Council guide ("The weather‚Ä¶ Weekend plans‚Ä¶") and Verywell Mind's "10
Best and Worst Small Talk Topics" (neutral topics: weather, sports, travel). The
words below are the lexical anchors the openers are built on.

## C. Follow-ups & backchannels (keep the exchange alive)

An opener with no follow-up dies. These short reactions show you are listening
and invite the other speaker to continue. They are the lubricant that turns a
one-line opener into a two-minute exchange.
