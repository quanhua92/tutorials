② VRAM — GGUF Q4 is the enabler (28 GB → 8.5 GB)

What this shows: toggle GGUF Q4 (above) to quantize the 14B weights. FP16 = 28 GB fits NO consumer GPU; GGUF Q4 = 8.5 GB fits a 12 GB card. The red line is the GPU limit. Without GGUF the model won't load — the speed optimizations are moot. Same budget as wan_video.py Section C.

③ Quality tradeoff & the video-model landscape

What this shows (left): each layer's fidelity cost — TeaCache & compile are free; GGUF Q4 is ~2%; Lightning is the one real trade (~5–10%). Right: same target clip as ltx_video.py — the full stack makes 14B Wan FASTER than LTX 2B on the reference numbers, while keeping the 14B quality edge.
LTX wins on the smallest card (8 GB); full-stack Wan wins on quality at similar speed but needs ≥12 GB. GGUF Q4 alone (row 3) gives NO speed — it only makes the model fit; you still pay 30 steps. Q4 = fit, Lightning/TeaCache/compile = speed.
