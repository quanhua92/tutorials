③ The key schedule — 128-bit key expands into 11 round keys

Each round key is derived from the previous words; every 4th word uses RotWord → SubWord → ^Rcon. K0 is the master key; K1 is highlighted (verified against FIPS-197).

④ Full AES-128 + avalanche — 1 bit in, ~half the block changes

Ten rounds scramble the block into unrecognizable ciphertext (verified vs FIPS-197). Flip one bit of the plaintext and watch the avalanche: ~half of all 128 ciphertext bits change — that is confusion + diffusion compounding.
