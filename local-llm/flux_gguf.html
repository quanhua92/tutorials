Flux.1 [dev] is a 12-billion-parameter Diffusion Transformer — 23 GB at FP16, far too big for most GPUs. GGUF block quantization (the same format LLMs use) shrinks it to 7 GB (Q4_K_M) with <2% quality loss. Pick a GPU tier below and watch the VRAM budget recalculate. The gold-check pins the VAE compression ratio to the exact .py output.

④ fp8 vs GGUF — speed vs compatibility

What this shows: two ways to shrink Flux below FP16. fp8 is faster but needs RTX 40+ hardware. GGUF is slower but runs on ANY GPU and lets you pick the size.

fp8 native float8

• VRAM: ~12 GB (one size)
• Speed: faster (native fp8 matmul)
• Needs: Ada/Hopper only (RTX 40+)
• Quality: ~1% loss

GGUF block quant Q4-Q8

• VRAM: 6.5–12 GB (you pick)
• Speed: slower (dequant on-the-fly)
• Needs: ANY GPU (even old Maxwell)
• Quality: <1–4% (depends on level)
Rule of thumb: RTX 4090/4080/5090 → fp8 (faster). Everything else → GGUF (only option). Want the smallest? GGUF Q3_K_S (5.5 GB DiT).
