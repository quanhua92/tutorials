Hi there,

Thanks for your email. [slot 4 · courtesy opener — softens the auto-reply]

I'm out of the office until Tuesday 22 July, returning on Wednesday 23 July. [slot 1 · dates — exact day, stated twice]

I'll have limited access to email during this time. [slot 2 · access level — replies will be slow]

For urgent matters, please contact Mai Nguyen at mai.nguyen@example.com. [slot 3 · coverage — named contact, pre-warned]

I'll respond to your email when I return. [slot 4 · return — sets the reply expectation]

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Vague about dates — "on leave, back soon" (literal "nghỉ phép, về sớm")Name the exact day, twice: out until Mon 14 July, returning Tue 15 July. Explicit dates are professional.
Omits the coverage line (VN relies on the caller "knowing" to ask)Always add slot 3: For urgent matters, please contact [Name + contact]. No coverage = a dead inbox.
Names a colleague who was not warned (face-saving culture assumes the team "just covers")Confirm with the colleague before naming them. The coverage line is a promise that [X] has agreed.
Too casual — "Hi, I busy now, reply later" (translating "bận, rep sau")Match the four-slot form. Open with Thanks for your email; close with a return line. An OOO is a small formal document.
Pro-drop — "On leave. Back Monday. Contact Mai." (subjects omitted)Supply full subject + verb: I am on leave; I will be back Monday.
No tense marking — "I out of office" / "I return on Monday"Mark tense + future: I am out of the office; I will return on Monday.
"If urgent, contact X" (no softener — reads as a command)Soften with please + hedge matters: If this is urgent, please contact [X].
"Kindly contact" / "do the needful" (dated calque)Drop kindly. Use please once, or let the If urgent frame carry the politeness.
Expects sender to "wait patiently" (VN norm: silence = patience)English senders expect an explicit timeline: I'll respond to your email when I return. Silence ≠ patience; it reads as ignoring.
One OOO for all audiences (client, vendor, peer get the same casual line)Calibrate by recipient. Client = formal; internal peer = brief (away from my desk, back today).
Forgets the timezone on a global team — "back Monday" = Monday where?Add the timezone or window: returning Monday, July 14 (ICT).
