5Writing task

Model (match the register; answer the thank-you if you're the helper):

Casual: Thanks so much for the help today — I owe you one! /ˈθæŋks soʊ mʌtʃ/ — warm informal

Formal: I'm very grateful for your time and support. /aɪm ˈveri ˈɡreɪt.fəl/ — written/professional register

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
“Cảm ơn” needs a pronoun — cảm ơn anh / chị — so you freeze searching for the English “title”English thanking is pronoun-free: Thanks, Thank you, I appreciate it. Drop the anh/chị entirely.
Silence after “thank you” — L1 answers cảm ơn with a smile or nothing; in English that reads cold/rudeAlways answer a thank-you verbally: You're welcome (safe default), No worries (casual), My pleasure (warm). The response turn is mandatory.
Under-thanking — one quiet “thank you” for a big favour (L1 shows gratitude by deed, not volume)Match the favour's weight: small → Thanks; medium → Thank you so much / I really appreciate it; large → I'm very grateful.
Over-thanking — stacking “thank you, thank you” (the cảm ơn, cảm ơn nhiều habit)Say it once, with the right chunk: Thank you so much — I really appreciate it beats thank you thank you thank you.
“No worries” / “Anytime” feel too casual or dismissive, so you avoid themThey are standard friendly, not rude. No worries = “no trouble”; Anytime = “I'd help any time.” Use freely with peers.
“Thanks a lot” said flatly — risks sounding sarcastic without you noticingUse Thanks so much for guaranteed-sincere casual thanks. Save Thanks a lot for a warm, falling intonation.
Drops finals on the formal words — “gray-te-fu” (grateful), “a-pree-see-ate” (appreciate)Release every final: grateful /ˈɡreɪt.fəl/, appreciate /əˈpriː.ʃi.eɪt/. 🔗 Drill with Final Consonants.
/ʒ/ → /dʒ/ or /z/ on pleasure — “ple-jer” (VN has no /ʒ/)Hold the voiced “sh”: throat buzzes on /ʒ/. pleasure /ˈpleʒ.ər/. Practise measure / pleasure / treasure.
Translates không có gì literally → “no have nothing” or freezesMap không có gì to the response set: You're welcome, No worries, Don't mention it.
Confuses thanking & apologising responses — No worries answering both feels wrongNo worries / No problem are genuinely dual-purpose — they answer a thank and a small apology. One chunk, two speech acts.
