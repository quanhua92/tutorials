The primary ships WAL to standby(s) over TCP; each standby write → flush → replays it into a hot copy. Async acks at the local fsync (data-loss window); sync waits for a standby's flush ack. synchronous_standby_names picks FIRST n (priority) or ANY n (quorum). ① Async vs sync — when does the client get told "committed"?

What to do: toggle the mode. In async the client is acked at the primary's fsync (t=5) — a 6 ms data-loss window opens until the standby flushes. In sync the primary holds the ack until s1 confirms flush (t=13) — zero RPO, but the client pays the round-trip.

② Quorum — FIRST n (priority) vs ANY n (any n ack first)

What to do: pick the rule and n. The standbys that satisfy the commit light up green. ANY n acks at the n-th fastest flush (tolerates a straggler); FIRST n acks at max of the first n named (bound to the slowest named). Watch ANY 2 beat FIRST 2.
