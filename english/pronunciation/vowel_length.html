6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
No tense/lax contrast → collapses /iː/ and /ɪ/ to one vowel; sheep and ship identical; "I live in a sheep"Drill the pair as two words. Hold /iː/ long + spread-lipped; make /ɪ/ short + relaxed. The length must differ.
Ignores the length mark /ː/ → reads /iː/ and /ɪ/ as the same; leave = liveTreat /ː/ as a real letter. Long vowels are ~1.5× a short vowel. Time it: sheeeep vs ship.
No /ʊ/-/uː/ contrast → pool and pull collapse to one vowelRound lips forward for /uː/ (long, pushed out); keep /ʊ/ short with loose lips. Drill: pool/pull, fool/full.
No /æ/ phoneme → substitutes /e/; bad → "bed"Open the jaw wider for /æ/ than for /e/. Drill: bad/bed, man/men, sat/set.
All vowels equal-length monophthongs (Vietnamese is syllable-timed)Switch to stress-timed length: long vowels stretch, short + schwas shrink. Content words get the long vowel.
Never reduces to schwa /ə/ — stresses every grammar word; "I want to go" all full vowelsLet unstressed grammar words shrink to /ə/: "I want tə go". 🔗 See Sentence Stress + Linking.
/ɜː/ has no Vietnamese equivalent → replaced by /e/ or /ə/; bird → "bed"Centre the tongue (mid, neutral, unrounded), lips relaxed, hold it long. Pair: bird /bɜːd/ vs bed /bed/.
Copies spelling instead of length → sees "live"/"leave" both with i/e and gives same vowelTrust the dictionary IPA, not the spelling. Look up the length mark until the contrast is automatic.
Diphthongizes a long vowel → sheep → "shee-yip"Keep /iː/ a steady monophthong, held long, no off-glide. Hold the smile, hold the vowel, release the /p/.
