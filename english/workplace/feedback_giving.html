5Writing task

Write a 3-line SBI feedback (Situation-Behavior-Impact). Use both "When you…, the impact was…" and "Next time, you might want to…". Then reveal the model answer.

Model (Situation → Behavior → Impact → Forward suggestion):

In yesterday's presentation, one thing that worked well was your opening — it grabbed the room. [specific positive]

When you skipped the testing slide, the impact was the client looked confused. [SBI: behavior + impact; pause between]

Next time, you might want to slow down on that section. [forward-looking hedged suggestion]

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Avoids critical feedback entirely to preserve harmony (giữ thể diện)Use the SBI frame: "When you…, the impact was…" — be honest without being harsh.
Delivers criticism bluntly: "Your presentation was bad" / "You are not clear"Describe the behavior + impact, not the person: "When you skipped the slide, the impact was the client looked confused."
Gives vague praise — "Good job" / "Very good" with no specificsName the specific thing: "One thing that worked well was your opening."
Uses the feedback sandwich (praise → criticize → praise) to softenEnglish workplaces read it as insincere. Give positive and constructive feedback as separate, specific statements.
Goes straight to "You should…" for the suggestion → sounds like an orderHedge it: "Next time, you might want to…" / "Have you considered…?"
Wraps critique in vague hedges — "Maybe somehow it could be a bit better"Be specific about what behavior and what impact. Vagueness defeats the purpose.
Fear of offending seniors → stays silent in upward feedbackSBI works upward — it's factual, not personal: "When the deadline moved, the impact was the team had to redo the work."
Runs behavior + impact together → reads as a rehearsed complaintInsert a real pause after the behavior: "When you… ↑" (beat) "…the impact was…"
Drops past-tense -ed on feedback verbs → "When you skip…, the impact is…"SBI is past-tense by design — it names a specific past moment. Drill skipped, noticed, liked, worked with audible finals.
