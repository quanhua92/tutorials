GPU Offload (-ngl) — interactive 📖 GPU_OFFLOAD.md 📄 gpu_offload.py

A Transformer is a stack of layers. -ngl N puts the first N layers on the GPU (green, VRAM) and leaves the rest on the CPU (orange, RAM/SIMD). Drag the slider to move the seam — watch the VRAM budget, the activation-transfer size, and the decode-speed curve update live. All values recomputed in JS — identical to gpu_offload.py.

① Layer split — drag -ngl

What to do: drag -ngl. The first N layers turn green (GPU), the rest stay orange (CPU). The yellow line is the seam where one activation tensor crosses CPU↔GPU per token. At -ngl 32 every layer is on the GPU — no seam, no CPU bottleneck.

③ Activation transfer — what actually crosses the seam

The seam copies an activation (= batch × seq × dim × bytes), not the weights. Decode is tiny (8 KiB); prefill is larger (8 MiB @1024). The weights, by contrast, are ~145 MiB/layer but move once at load — 18514× bigger than a decode activation.

④ Decode-speed curve — ~linear, then flat

Each offloaded layer removes one CPU-bound layer from the hot path, so decode tok/s rises ~linearly with -ngl — then flattens at full offload (decode becomes VRAM-bandwidth-bound). The dashed line marks the current -ngl from panel 1. Illustrative shape, not a benchmark.
