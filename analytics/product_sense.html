A product-sense interview is problem → users → metrics → tradeoffs, in that order, every time. Below: the 8-step framework, the 18-24 mobile journey, a decision simulator (pick a feature → see its DAU/Revenue/Satisfaction impact), a tradeoff matrix with live weight sliders (drag the guardrail weight up — watch Reels fall below onboarding), RICE vs ICE scoring, and a branching A/B-test walkthrough. Everything is recomputed live in JS from the same dataset as product_sense.py.  |  how this bundle was built →

① The 8-step framework — problem first, solution second, metrics third

9/10 vs 6/10 separator: the 9/10 names data signals at step 4 (NPS, session recordings, support tickets) and names the suppression trap at step 8 unprompted. Everything else is structure a 6/10 also has. Worked example: "Facebook engagement declining in 18-24 (mobile)".

② The user journey — drop-off at each stage (where to invest)

Per 100,000 reached, 18-24 mobile. The step with the largest drop is the highest-leverage place to spend.

④ Tradeoff matrix — the suppression trap (drag the weights, watch the ranking flip)

NET = wprimary·ΔDAU + wguardrail·ΔRev + wcounter·ΔSat. A primary lift with a guardrail DROP = the suppression trap; a primary lift with a counter DROP = the gaming signal. Drag guardrail toward 0.6 — Reels (DAU +8.5%, Rev −3.2%) drops below onboarding.

⑤ Scoring model — RICE vs ICE for feature prioritization

RICE = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort — favors cheap, broad wins.
Both models agree onboarding is the quick win and collab is the dud. But scoring is an input to judgment, not a substitute: the 9/10 answer sequences onboarding first (de-risk activation) then Reels as the strategic bet for the format-mismatch root cause. See PRODUCT_SENSE.md §5.

⑥ Scenario walkthrough — Reels A/B test, branching decisions

Week-2 readout tree. Click a branch to see its DAU/Rev/Sat, computed NET, and the recommendation — decided by a rule that encodes the suppression trap and the gaming test. A "good" primary is NOT enough; the guardrail and counter gate the ship call.
Decision rule: seg-split → segmented rollout · DAU≤2% → kill · DAU>2% & Rev≤−1% → iterate (suppression) unless Sat<0 → kill · DAU>2% & Sat<0 → hold (gaming) · else ship.
