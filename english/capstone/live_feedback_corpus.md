## A. Ask permission / open (the real-time "can I?" anchor)

The single move that makes English live feedback feel kind, not attacking: **ask
before you give it.** This is the "feedback permission slip" — a documented
politeness strategy (Viktor Cessan: *"One way to get permission is to simply ask
'May I offer you some feedback?'"*; *From Insult to Respect*: ask permission
*"before providing criticism"*). It is the opposite of the Vietnamese default
(see L1 pitfalls): in face-saving cultures, feedback is either avoided entirely
or delivered bluntly with no preamble. The English fix is a one-line ask that
hands the listener control of the moment.

## B. Positive / what worked (the real-time "you nailed it" anchor)

Real-time feedback opens with **one specific thing that worked** — never a vague
"good job." Cambridge records `nail` (verb, SUCCEED sense) as informal *"to do
something successfully: She nailed her audition… You totally nailed it!"*
(/neɪl/, past `nailed` /neɪld/). `What worked well was…` is the canonical
debrief/retro opener (attested in meeting-facilitation guides, sibling to #36's
`One thing that worked well was…`, shortened for the spoken real-time register).
`I loved how you…` is the specific manner-compliment (Cambridge `love`, past
`loved` /lʌvd/ + `how` = the way it was done). These are the **specificity
anchors** that stop a Vietnamese learner defaulting to the vague *"good job."*

| You nailed… | you did (a specific part) perfectly — informal | /juː neɪld/ UK · /ju neɪld/ US | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/us/dictionary/english/nailed | (phrase; `nail` SUCCEED sense) | US/UK |

## C. Constructive / the one tweak (the real-time forward move)

After the specific praise, deliver **one** forward-looking tweak — specific,
actionable, and bounded. `Next time, try…` is the imperative forward-move (sibling
to #36's hedged `Next time, you might want to…`, but faster for live use).
`One thing to tweak…` names the adjustment without judgment (Cambridge `tweak`
/twiːk/ = "to change something slightly, usually to improve it"). `Watch this
part…` and `Be careful with…` are the in-the-moment pointers used *during* a
dry-run or rehearsal (pointing at the specific moment in the recording/performance).
These keep the criticism **behavioral and small**, not personal.
