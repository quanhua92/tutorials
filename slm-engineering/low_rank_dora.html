Drag d, k, r for the param bars, then steer the magnitude & direction sliders to feel the decoupling. ① Trainable params: full-FT vs LoRA vs DoRA — drag d, k, r

How to read: full-FT = d·k (all weights), LoRA = r(d+k) (only A, B), DoRA = r(d+k)+k (A, B, plus the magnitude vector m — the +k). At d=k=4, r=2 all three are 16/16/20 — the GOLD pin. Real scale (d=k=4096, r=8): full = 16.8M, LoRA = 65K, DoRA = 69K (~256× fewer; DoRA adds only +4096).
full-FT = d·k LoRA = r(d+k) DoRA = r(d+k)+k (+k magnitude)

② The decoupling: steer magnitude (m) and direction independently

One weight column, shown as a 2-D arrow. Grey = frozen W (fixed). DoRA rewrites it as m ⊙ (V/‖V‖): a magnitude m (length) times a unit direction (angle). Drag m — only the length changes, direction holds; drag direction — only the angle changes, then m re-scales it. LoRA = W + BA is shown for contrast: its single additive patch moves length AND angle together (you cannot tune them apart).

③ Param table — recomputed live (mirrors low_rank_dora.py Section D)

How to read: full-FT grows as d·k (quadratic); LoRA/DoRA grow as r(d+k) (linear). DoRA is always exactly k more than LoRA. The highlighted row is the GOLD pin (d=k=4, r=2). When r ≥ min(d,k) LoRA stops paying (it can exceed full-FT) — real use keeps r ≪ min(d,k).
