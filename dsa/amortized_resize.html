Amortized Analysis of Dynamic Array Resizing — interactive AMORTIZED_RESIZE.md amortized_resize.py

A dynamic array doubles when full — one O(N) resize day, then many cheap O(1) days. Spread the pain and the average per append is a constant. Step through appends, slide the growth factor, and toggle the shrink threshold to see thrashing cured by a 25% hysteresis gap. Every number is recomputed in JS from the identical formulas as amortized_resize.py, gold-checked below.

① Doubling in action — capacity doubles, the average stays flat

What to do: drag appends up to 16 and watch the orange resize bars (the O(N) days) get rarer while the running amortized cost settles near 2. Each column = capacity after one append; the blue fill = how full it is.
capacity (fill = size) resize day (O(N) copy) current step
Resizes fire at append# 2, 3, 5, 9 copying 1, 2, 4, 8. Total copies for 16 appends = 15, total ops = 31 = 2·16−1, amortized ≈ 2. The worst single step costs 8, yet the average is a constant — that is amortized O(1).  © amortized_resize.py Section A

② Growth factor dial — bigger g = fewer resizes but more waste (all still O(1))

What to do: slide g from 2.0 down to 1.25. Smaller g wastes less space but resizes more often — yet the amortized constant g/(g−1) stays bounded, so it is O(1) for any g > 1.
N = 1024 appends — matches amortized_resize.py Section D
Copy work is a geometric series: copies ≈ N/(g−1), so amortized ops/append = 1 + 1/(g−1) = g/(g−1). Fill ratio ranges [1/g, 1.0], so max wasted space = 1 − 1/g. g=2 wastes 50%, g=1.5 wastes 33%, g=1.25 wastes 20%.  © amortized_resize.py Section D

③ The two proofs — accounting (bank ≥ 0) and potential (Φ ≥ 0)

What this shows: charge $3/append (accounting) or track Φ=2·num−cap (potential). Both quantities never go negative, which is the proof that amortized cost ≤ 3 ⇒ O(1). Step through appends; resize steps drain the bank/Φ, cheap steps refill it.
bank balance $0
Φ (potential) 0
Accounting: 1 pays the write, 2 is banked; the m/2 newest elements' $2 credit = $m funds each resize. Potential: on a resize of k copies, actual spikes to k+1 but ΔΦ = 2−k is negative, cancelling it → amortized ≡ 3.  © Sections B & C

④ Shrink threshold — 25% cures the 50% thrash

What to do: build to 16, then hammer with 8 (add, del) pairs. At 50% every op resizes (thrash → O(N²)). At 25% the hysteresis gap stops the ping-pong → O(1).

50%: add→grow(16), del→shrink(16) every pair = 32 copies/pair. 25%: after one grow, add/del oscillates at cap=32 with 0 further shrinks — a single op cannot cross both the 25% and 100% thresholds.  © amortized_resize.py Section E
