## A. Initial clusters — s + stop (sp / st / sk)

The highest-frequency English onset type, and the single biggest cluster trap
for a Vietnamese learner. Vietnamese syllables cannot begin with two
consonants, so the mouth either **inserts a schwa** before the cluster
(*stop* → "sə-top") or **deletes the first consonant** (*stop* → "top"). The
fix: glide the /s/ straight into the second consonant with **no vowel
between**.

## B. Initial clusters — s + sonorant (sm / sn / sl) + 3-consonant s-clusters (spr / str / skr)

These are where schwa-epenthesis is most audible: Vietnamese learners turn
*small* into "sə-mall" and *street* into "sə-treet". The three-consonant
onsets /spr str skr/ are the ceiling of the difficulty — and the words are
among the most frequent in English (*street*, *spring*, *strong*).

## D. Final clusters (-st / -ld / -mp / -nd / -kt / -kst)

Vietnamese has **no final clusters** (coda inventory = /p t k m n ŋ/ only, no
two-consonant codas). So *desk* → "de", *world* → "wo", *next* → "ne". This
section is the mirror image of bundle #01 (`final_consonants`) — there a single
final was the issue; here it is two or three consonants stacked at the end.

## F. The epenthesis error — correct vs inserted-schwa

The textbook L1 error: a vowel is **inserted inside** a cluster to "open" it
into CV syllables the L1 mouth can produce. The most-cited example is
*groserry* for *grocery*; the same repair produces *sə-tree* for *street* and
*sə-mall* for *small*. This row pair is the bundle's pinned sanity-check.

| English chunk | meaning | IPA | source URL | frequency rank | accent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| grocery | a food shop / groceries (US) | /ˈɡrəʊsəri/ UK · /ˈɡroʊsəri/ US | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/grocery | ≈#1500 | US/UK |
| groserry ❌ | (learner error — schwa inserted inside /gr-/) | /ɡrəˈseri/ ❌ — not a dictionary form | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/pronunciation/english/grocery | — | error |

> **Verification note:** the correct form `grocery` /ˈɡrəʊsəri/ (UK) /
> /ˈɡroʊsəri/ (US) is the Cambridge pronunciation-page form; the two-syllable
> learner form /ɡrəʊsriː/ is noted on the UsingEnglish ESL forum; the
> schwa-inserted "groserry" /ɡrəˈseri/ is the documented L1 epenthesis repair
> (Can Tho University Journal: Vietnamese learners "add schwa" to clusters;
> Diva-Portal: "adding schwa" is a primary Vietnamese strategy for English
> finals/clusters). The ❌ row is **labelled as an error**, not a citation of a
> real word.
