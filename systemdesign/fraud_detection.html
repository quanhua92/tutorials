A multi-stage cascade — cheap rules first, an expensive ML scorer last on the few that survive. Dial a transaction's features and watch a hand-trained logistic-regression scorer produce P(fraud), flip switches in the rule engine, sweep the cost-based threshold τ, scrub the feature-aggregation windows, then expose a fraud ring through 2-hop graph connectivity — every number matches fraud_detection.py exactly.

① Fraud score calculator — 8 features → logistic-regression P(fraud)

What to do: drag the 8 raw features. Each is normalized by a fixed divisor (capped at 1.0), then fed to a logistic-regression scorer trained by batch gradient descent (lr=0.5, 2000 epochs) — the same model in fraud_detection.py Section 3. The gauge marks the cost-optimal threshold τ; the bar turns red past it. Try the presets.

② Rule engine playground — hard rules fire BEFORE the ML scorer

What to do: set a transaction's rule-relevant fields and toggle each rule. A fired rule BLOCKs (or REVIEWs) instantly in ~3ms and the ML scorer never runs. Edit the thresholds to see the boundary move (fraud_detection.py Section 1).
—

③ Feature aggregation visualizer — sliding windows over history

What to do: the current transaction arrives at t=0. Drag the current amount and watch Flink-maintained Redis counters recompute: how many txs fall in the 1h window, the 24h sum, and the 30d behavioral average (fraud_detection.py Section 2).

④ Threshold & cost explorer — τ = C_FP / (C_FN + C_FP)

What to do: never use 0.5. Fraud has asymmetric costs: a missed fraud (C_FN) costs ~5× a wrongful block (C_FP). Drag the costs and watch the optimal block threshold τ move, plus the REVIEW band around it (fraud_detection.py Section 4).

⑤ Fraud ring — 2-hop graph connectivity (GraphSAGE aggregation)

What to do: click any account. A1 is clean in isolation (risk 0.10), but it shares a device/card with A2/A3/A6, who in turn link to confirmed-fraud A4 / A5. Two fraud accounts within 2 hops is the ring signal that boosts every A1 transaction's score (fraud_detection.py Section 6).

⑥ Cascade latency — cheap filters first, expensive scorer last

What to do: tune each stage's latency and the fraction of traffic that reaches it. The avg latency/tx is the traffic-weighted sum; p99 is a worst-case tx hitting every stage. Both must stay under the 100ms budget (fraud_detection.py Section 5).
