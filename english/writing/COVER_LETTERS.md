## Why this bundle exists (read this first)

A Vietnamese learner writing an English cover letter almost always makes the
same two mistakes, and both are **genre** mistakes, not grammar ones.

**Mistake 1 ‚ÄĒ the template restatement.** Vietnamese *ńĎ∆°n xin viŠĽác* (application
letters) are **formal, role-generic, and CV-restating** ‚ÄĒ a fixed template of
*"T√īi t√™n l√†‚Ä¶ T√īi ńĎ√£ tŠĽĎt nghiŠĽáp‚Ä¶ T√īi c√≥ kinh nghiŠĽám‚Ä¶ T√īi mong ńĎ∆įŠĽ£c trao c∆° hŠĽôi"*
that politely restates what the CV already says. English cover-letter culture is
the **opposite**: the letter must be **targeted to THIS role** at THIS company
and must **add evidence the CV does not make obvious** (McGill: *"A cover letter
should add nuance to your C.V."*). A letter that summarises the CV is read as
redundant ‚ÄĒ the recruiter already has the CV.

**Mistake 2 ‚ÄĒ modesty ‚Üí under-selling.** Vietnamese culture reads self-praise as
*ki√™u ngŠļ°o* (arrogance), so the writer **under-states achievements** (*"I think
I can do the job"*, *"I have some experience"*) or pads with **generic traits**
(*"I am hardworking, careful, and responsible"*) with no evidence. English
cover-letter culture reads this as **no confidence + no proof** ‚ÄĒ the letter
must **name a specific result** (*"I led X, resulting in Y%"*). The fix is not
to brag; it is to **let the evidence do the talking**.

This bundle teaches the four-move English skeleton that fixes both: **open** by
naming the role, **why** (fit + motivation), **evidence** (one concrete
achievement), **close** (a confident ask + thanks).

Notice what is **not** a move: the long self-introduction (*"My name is‚Ä¶"*), the
CV summary, the generic *"I am hardworking"* trait list, the apology (*"I hope
you will give me a chance"*). The open is one sentence. The why is targeted.
The evidence is one STAR point. The close is confident. That is the whole genre.

## 1. The open ‚ÄĒ name the role (one sentence)

The first line does one job: **name the role and say you are writing to apply**.
No preamble, no *"My name is‚Ä¶"*, no life story. Two registers, standard and
slightly softer:

| Register | Chunk | When |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | **I'm writing to apply for‚Ä¶** | the default ‚ÄĒ direct, professional |
| Softer | **I'd like to be considered for‚Ä¶** | more formal / internal / committee |

> From `cover_letters_corpus.md`:
>
> | I'm writing to apply for‚Ä¶ | I'd like to be considered for‚Ä¶ |
> |---|---|
> | /a…™m ňąra…™t…™Ňč t…ô …ôňąpla…™ f…ô(r)/ | /a…™d la…™k t…ô bi k…ônňąs…™d…ôd f…ô(r)/ |
>
> The Harvard OCS model letter opens verbatim: *"I am writing to apply for the
> Marketing and Communications position‚Ä¶"*. *"I'd like to be considered for‚Ä¶"*
> is the Cambridge B2 passive *"be considered for a job/role"*. `apply`
> /…ôňąpla…™/ ‚ÄĒ identical US/UK (Cambridge, Britannica).

**The Vietnamese trap here:** Vietnamese letters open with a long
self-introduction (*"T√īi t√™n l√† NguyŠĽÖn VńÉn A, sinh nńÉm‚Ä¶"*) and reach the role
only in paragraph two. English recruiters open hundreds of letters ‚ÄĒ a missing
*"apply for [role]"* in line 1 is read as a copy-paste template. The fix is
mechanical: **line 1 always names the role.**

## 2. The why ‚ÄĒ fit + motivation (the "because")

The second move is **why THIS role, why THIS company**. This is the move a
Vietnamese L1 writer most often skips ‚ÄĒ Vietnamese letters assume motivation
(*of course I want the job*), so the letter never states it. English recruiters
read a letter with **no "why" as a form letter**. A specific *"I'm excited about
this role because‚Ä¶"* or *"What draws me to [company] is‚Ä¶"* is what separates a
real application from spam.

> From `cover_letters_corpus.md`:
>
> | I'm excited about this role because‚Ä¶ | What draws me to [company] is‚Ä¶ |
> |---|---|
> | /a…™m …™kňąsa…™t…™d …ôňąba ät √į…™s r…ô äl b…™ňąk…íz/ UK | /w…ít dr…Ēňźz miňź tuňź [ňąk Ćmp…ôni] …™z/ UK |
>
> The Harvard OCS model attests *"I am excited about the field‚Ä¶"*; Indeed Canada
> attests *"What draws me to Wavewood is its commitment to giving back."*.
> `drawn` /dr…Ēňźn/ UK ¬∑ /dr…Ďňźn/ US is the Cambridge adjective *"attracted to"*;
> `particularly` /p…ôňąt…™kj…ôl…ôli/ UK ¬∑ /p…ôrňąt…™kj…ôl…ôrli/ US.

**The pinned one-liner for this bundle:**

> From `cover_letters_corpus.md`:
>
> > **I'm excited to apply because‚Ä¶** /a…™m …™kňąsa…™t…™d t…ô …ôňąpla…™ b…™ňąk…íz/ UK ¬∑
> > /‚Ä¶b…™ňąk…Ďňźz/ US ‚ÄĒ Dice cover-letter example: *"I'm excited to apply these
> > skills professionally."* Source: dice.com/career-advice.

The discipline here is the word **"because"**: it forces a *specific* reason.
*"I'm excited to apply"* alone is a feeling; *"‚Ä¶because [specific thing about
the role/company]"* is an argument. Write the because-clause first, then attach
the open.

## 3. The evidence ‚ÄĒ one concrete achievement (the proof)

The third move is **evidence the CV does not already make obvious** ‚ÄĒ one
specific achievement with a result. This is where the letter earns its page.
Three lead-ins carry the whole move:

> From `cover_letters_corpus.md`:
>
> | In my current role, I‚Ä¶ | One achievement I'm proud of is‚Ä¶ | Most recently, I‚Ä¶ |
> |---|---|---|
> | /…™n ma…™ ňąk Ćr…ônt r…ô äl a…™/ | /w Ćn …ôňąt Éiňźvm…ônt a…™m pra äd …ív …™z/ UK | /m…ô äst ňąriňźs…ôntli a…™/ UK |
>
> *"In my current role, I‚Ä¶"* is the verbatim body opener in the Indeed
> cover-letter example. `achievement` /…ôňąt Éiňźvm…ônt/, `proud` /pra äd/,
> `current` /ňąk Ćr…ônt/ ‚ÄĒ Cambridge headwords.

**The evidence shape is STAR** (Situation ‚Üí Task ‚Üí Action ‚Üí Result) ‚ÄĒ the same
shape used in a behavioral interview. ūüĒó See
[BEHAVIORAL INTERVIEW Q&A](../workplace/INTERVIEWS_BEHAVIORAL.md). The written
version compresses it into one or two sentences with a **metric**:

- ‚ĚĆ *CV restatement:* "I have 3 years of experience in marketing." (the CV says
  this already ‚ÄĒ redundant)
- ‚ĚĆ *Generic trait:* "I am a hardworking and responsible person." (no evidence)
- ‚úÖ *Targeted evidence:* "In my current role, I led a campaign that grew sign-ups
  by 34% in one quarter." (Situation + Action + **Result with a number**)

ūüĒó Pair every evidence point with the action-verb + metric pattern from
[CV / R√ČSUM√Č BULLETS](./CV_BULLETS.md) ‚ÄĒ *"Led X, resulting in Y%"*.

**The Vietnamese trap here:** modesty (*khi√™m tŠĽĎn*) is a virtue in Vietnamese, so
the writer **under-sells** achievements (*"I helped a little with‚Ä¶"*) or drops
the metric (*"I improved sales"* ‚ÄĒ by how much?). English cover-letter culture
reads the **absence of a specific result as no result**. The fix: let the number
carry the confidence ‚ÄĒ you are not bragging, the metric is just a fact.

## 4. The close ‚ÄĒ confident ask + thanks

The final move is **a confident ask + thanks**, one or two sentences. No
begging, no apologising. Three chunks carry the whole move:

| Move | Chunk |
|---|---|
| Confident ask | **I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss‚Ä¶** |
| Thanks (pinned closer) | **Thank you for your consideration.** |
| Expectation | **I look forward to hearing from you.** |

> From `cover_letters_corpus.md`:
>
> > **Thank you for your consideration.** /őł√¶Ňčk juňź f…ô(r) j…Ēňź(r)
> > k…ônňĆs…™d.…ôňąre…™. É…ôn/ ‚ÄĒ University of Maryland cover-letter sign-off; cited as
> > a standard close by Adobe Acrobat, LinkedIn, and Ladders.
> > Source: dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/consideration.
>
> *"I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss how my skills can contribute‚Ä¶"* is the
> verbatim University of Cincinnati close. `consideration`
> /k…ônňĆs…™d.…ôňąre…™. É…ôn/ UK / US (Cambridge, fetched directly); `opportunity`
> /ňĆ…íp…ôňątjuňźn…ôti/ UK ¬∑ /ňĆ…Ďňźp…ôrňątuňźn…ôti/ US (Oxford, Collins).

**The Vietnamese trap here:** Vietnamese letters close by **begging for a
chance** (*"T√īi mong ńĎ∆įŠĽ£c anh/chŠĽč trao c∆° hŠĽôi ńĎŠĽÉ ńĎ∆įŠĽ£c thŠĽÉ hiŠĽán"* ‚Üí "I hope you
will give me a chance to show myself"). English reads *"hope you give me a
chance"* as **desperate, not polite**. The confident close is *"I'd welcome the
opportunity to discuss‚Ä¶"* ‚ÄĒ it assumes the conversation will happen. The shift
is psychological: **you are offering value, not asking a favour.**

## 6. Vietnamese ‚Üí English L1 pitfalls table

The "expert payoff." These are the specific interference traps a Vietnamese
speaker hits on a cover letter ‚ÄĒ extend, don't replace, the seed rows from the
spec.

| Vietnamese trap (what you do) | English fix (what to do instead) |
|---|---|
| **Long self-introduction open** ‚ÄĒ "My name is‚Ä¶, I graduated from‚Ä¶, I was born in‚Ä¶" (the *ńĎ∆°n* template) | Open with **"I'm writing to apply for [role]"** in line 1. The CV has your bio; the letter names the role. |
| **Restates the CV** ‚ÄĒ "I have 3 years of experience in marketing" (the CV already says this) | **Add evidence the CV does not make obvious.** Pick one achievement and give the *result*. McGill: *"add nuance to your C.V."* |
| **Skips the "why"** ‚ÄĒ assumes motivation; no *"I'm excited about this role because‚Ä¶"* | State **why THIS role / THIS company** specifically. A letter with no "why" reads as a copy-paste template. |
| **Modesty ‚Üí under-sells** ‚ÄĒ "I helped a little", "I have some experience", drops the metric | Let the **number** carry the confidence: *"grew sign-ups by 34%"*. You are stating a fact, not bragging. |
| **Generic traits, no evidence** ‚ÄĒ "I am hardworking, careful, responsible" | Replace every trait with a **proof point**. "Hardworking" ‚Üí *"I led X, resulting in Y%."* ūüĒó See [CV BULLETS](./CV_BULLETS.md). |
| **Begging close** ‚ÄĒ "I hope you will give me a chance" (*mong ńĎ∆įŠĽ£c trao c∆° hŠĽôi*) | Close **confidently**: *"I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss‚Ä¶"*. You are offering value, not asking a favour. |
| **Apologising for gaps/inexperience** ‚ÄĒ "I am sorry I do not have‚Ä¶" | **Pivot, don't apologise.** Frame what you *bring*: *"Most recently, I‚Ä¶"*. Never put a weakness in the close. |
| **Over-formal / stiff register** ‚ÄĒ "I have the honour to submit herewith my humble application" | Use **clean professional register**, not ceremonial. *"I'm writing to apply"* is already formal enough; no "herewith" or "humble". ūüĒó See [FORMAL VS CASUAL REGISTER](./FORMAL_CASUAL_REGISTER.md). |
| **One generic letter for every job** (role-template) | **Re-target each letter:** name the company and the specific reason (*"What draws me to [company] is‚Ä¶"*). A reusable template = spam. |
| **No article / missing plural in evidence** ‚ÄĒ "I led team of five people" | Enforce **a/the** and **-s** plurals in the evidence sentence ‚ÄĒ a dropped *"the"* or *"five people ‚Üí five people"* reads careless to a recruiter. ūüĒó See [FINAL CONSONANTS](../pronunciation/FINAL_CONSONANTS.md). |

## How to practise this bundle (the daily 20 min)

1. **READ** (5 min) ‚ÄĒ this guide, ¬ß1‚Äď¬ß4.
2. **SHADOW** (7 min) ‚ÄĒ open `cover_letters.html`, drill the 8 flip cards + read
   the model body **aloud**. The close (*"Thank you for your consideration"*)
   and the pinned open (*"I'm excited to apply because‚Ä¶"*) are the two to
   over-learn ‚ÄĒ they work in almost any letter.
3. **PRODUCE** (8 min) ‚ÄĒ the **writing task** (this bundle's primary mode): write
   a cover-letter body ‚ÄĒ **one "why this role" sentence + one evidence point +
   one close.** Target one specific (real) job. Reveal the model answer to
   compare structure, then copy yours to refine.
