The 4-move status-report skeleton (project-reporting consensus: Asana · Atlassian · ProjectManager)

1. Status
RAG traffic light — On track / At risk / Blocked
2. Progress
bulleted, scannable — completed + milestones
3. Risks + Mitigation
every risk paired with its fix + contingency
4. Next steps
forward-looking — owner + date + forecast

Model — the 4-move skeleton, each move labelled:

[status] Status: 🟢 On track

[progress] Progress to date:
• Completed onboarding the payments vendor (milestone 3 of 5 — ahead of schedule).
• Fixed the login bug reported in last week's report.
Milestones: M3 ✓ done · M4 (beta launch) on track for July 12.

[risks + mitigation] Risks:
1. The API rate limit may block bulk import at launch.
Mitigation: Requested a limit increase from the vendor; contingency = batch import if denied.

[next steps] Next steps:
• Submit vendor contract to legal — owner: Linh, by July 10.
• Run the bulk-import load test — owner: Marco, by July 14.
Forecast: Green through M4 → Amber if rate-limit request denied by July 8.

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Writes narrative prose, not bullets — a flowing "đầu tuần em đã làm…" paragraphSplit progress into bullets, one fact per line. English status reports are scanned, not narrated.
Omits the RAG status line — starts with "This week I did…", no colourFirst line is always a status + colour. 🟢 On track / 🟡 At risk / 🔴 Blocked. No colour = reader must read everything to know if there's a fire.
Uses vague status — "going well", "fine" with no RAG and no ETAReplace vague with the exact RAG phrase + a date: "On track for July 12" / "At risk — ETA slips to July 20 if X isn't resolved."
Under-states / hides risks (the face trap) — no Risks section, "không có vấn đề gì"Name the risk and pair it with the mitigation. Surfacing a risk early is competence, not weakness.
Over-states progress — "almost done", "nearly finished" when it isn'tState the milestone status factually: "M3 done (ahead of schedule); M4 on track for July 12." Let dates + RAG carry honesty.
Drops the mitigation / contingency — names a risk but no fixEvery risk gets a Mitigation (what you're doing) + a Contingency (plan-B). A risk with no fix is complaining.
No owners / dates on next steps — "we will do X" with no name, no deadlineEvery next step needs an owner + a date: "Vendor contract to legal — Linh, July 10."
Noun/verb stress on progress — says /prəˈɡres/ (verb) for the noun /ˈprəʊɡres/Drill the shift: noun PRO-gress (/ˈprəʊɡres/) vs verb pro-GRESS (/prəˈɡres/). Both appear in one report.
US/UK schedule / forecast mixed mid-reportPick one accent per report. schedule UK /ˈʃedjuːl/ · US /ˈskedʒuːl/; forecast UK /ˈfɔːkɑːst/ · US /ˈfɔːrkæst/.
Final clusters in risks / next / steps / blocked dropped or schwa-openedRelease every cluster: risks not "risk", next not "nex", blocked not "block". These headings are read aloud in standups.
