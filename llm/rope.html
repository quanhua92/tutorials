The compass idea: every token holds a compass needle. Its seat number m tells it how far to spin. Drag m below and watch the 4 needles turn at different speeds. Every number is recomputed in JS from the identical formula in rope.py.  |  compare with absolute PE (barcode, not needle) →
D = 8 (4 pairs), base = 10000 — same as rope.py

① The four compass needles — each spins at its own speed θj

red arrow = seat 0 (no spin, the reference) green arrow = seat m (spun by m·θj) FAST clock (tracks local position) SLOW clock (tracks global position)
What to do: drag m and watch the green needles spin. Notice the left clock (pair 0, θ=1.0) spins fastest — it's the twitchy stopwatch. The right clock (pair 3, θ=0.001) barely moves — it's the slow hour-hand that only matters over thousands of seats. That speed spread is what lets RoPE record both fine and coarse position at once.

② Spinning one token — the 8 numbers before vs after RoPE

Fixed input token (from rope.py Section D): x1 = [1.0, 0.5, −0.3, 0.8] (first 4),   x2 = [0.2, −0.1, 0.4, 0.6] (last 4)
grey = original x (seat 0) green = real half after spin (d0..d3) orange = imag half after spin (d4..d7)
What to do: drag m and watch the green/orange bars shift. At m=0 they exactly match the grey bars (no spin). As m grows, the green/orange bars for the fast clocks (d0, d4) swing the most; the slow clocks (d3, d7) barely change. The numbers above each bar are live — check them against ROPE.md §5.

③ The spin recipe: cos / sin lookup tables (rows = seats)

cos_freqs[m, j] — how much of the old arrow to keep
sin_freqs[m, j] — how much to tilt sideways
What to do: the highlighted row follows seat m. A token at seat m simply grabs that row to spin its 4 clocks. Notice row 0 is always cos=1, sin=0 (= no spin, the identity). Column j=0 swings hard; column j=3 stays near 1.000 — that's the fast/slow spread in table form.
