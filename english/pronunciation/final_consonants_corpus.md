## A. Final-consonant minimal pairs (dropped final → meaning changes)

The #1 Vietnamese intelligibility failure: a released/clustered final consonant
that Vietnamese has no syllable slot for, so it is omitted ("wen" for *went*),
given a schwa ("finishe" for *finished*), or replaced ("dock"→"dog"). One
dropped final flips the word.

## B. The `-s` plural allomorphs — /s/ vs /z/ vs /ɪz/

the sound (not the letter) right before it. Confirmed in *The Cambridge
Dictionary of Linguistics* (Brown & Miller, CUP) and corroborated in the Longman
Pronunciation Dictionary references.

## D. High-frequency finals Vietnamese learners drop (function + content words)

These are the words whose dropped finals cause the most "pardon?" / "what did
you say?" in real conversation. Vietnamese has **no consonant clusters** and a
coda inventory of only /p t k m n ŋ/ (+ glides), so any English word ending in
/θ ð f v s z tʃ dʒ l/ or any cluster is at risk.
