5Writing task

Take the blunt sentence below and hedge it 3 ways — one with kind of, one with around, one with -ish. Then reveal the model answer.

Blunt (unhedged) version:

"It starts at 3:00 and costs $50."

Model (one hedge each — don't over-hedge):

kind of: "It kind of starts at three — I'm not totally sure." softener → claim strength

around: "It starts around three and costs around $50." approximator → number precision

-ish: "It starts at three-ish — fifty-ish bucks." suffix approximator → informal spoken

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Too precise / too direct — "The meeting is at 3:00. It costs $50." (exact, unsoftened)Soften the claim: "It's around three." / "Fifty-ish bucks." Hedge by default in casual speech.
Never softens a claim — "I am tired." / "It is expensive." (no kind of / a bit; VN marks degree with hơi)Insert a softener before adjectives/states: "I'm kind of tired." / "It's a bit expensive." Drill hơi → a bit / kind of.
The -ish suffix is unfamiliar (VN has no bound morpheme for "approximately"; misreads "six-ish" as the adjective suffix childish)Learn -ish as its own chunk: number/time + /ɪʃ/ = "around ___". Practise six-ish, seven-ish, forty-ish. Remember the standalone "Ish."
Translates literally → "more or less" becomes "nhiều hoặc ít" (a quantity, not a hedge)Treat more or less as one unit = "almost / approximately": "I've more or less finished." Don't decompose it.
Over-formal in casual chat — reaches for approximately / somewhat in speechDownshift in speech: approximately → around/about, somewhat → kind of / a bit. Save the formal ones for writing.
Confuses a bit (small degree) with a bit of a (rather a — often negative)Learn the frame: a bit + adjective = slightly ("a bit tight"); a bit of a + noun = rather a ("a bit of a mess / a bit of a pain").
Mispronounces the weak of in kind of / sort of — says /ɒv/ fullyWeak-form the of: kind of /ˌkaɪnd əv/ → often /ˌkaɪndə/. 🔗 See LINKING & REDUCTIONS.
