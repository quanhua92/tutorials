6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Skips the pre-closing — stops and says "bye" abruptlyAlways run signal → farewell. Lead with "Well, I should let you go" before the goodbye.
Over-loops the farewell — "ok… ok… bye… bye… bye"One signal + one farewell. Don't stack goodbyes; a single "Take care" then go.
Defaults to "goodbye" everywhere (word-for-word "tạm biệt")In casual chat use "see you", "take care", "talk soon". Reserve "goodbye" for formal.
Misses the appreciation turn — never says "It was great talking to you"Slot appreciation in after the signal: "Well, it was great talking to you. Take care."
Drops final consonants → "I should let you go" sounds like "leh you go"Release the /t/ in let and the /d/ in should.
Flat intonation on "take care" → reads as coldAdd slight rise-warmth (↗↘); "Take care." is warm, not robotic.
Translates "gặp lại sau" as "see you again" (stiff)Native default is "see you" / "see ya". Cambridge glosses see you (later) = "goodbye".
Says "I will go now" (word-for-word "tôi đi đây")Say "I'd better get going" — the get going idiom is the native way.
Forgets the future-plan closer with friends — ends flatAdd "Let's catch up soon" / "Talk soon" to signal warmth + continuity.
