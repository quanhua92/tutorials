✍️ 5Writing task — the cover-letter body

primary mode

Write a cover-letter body: one "why this role" sentence + one evidence point + one close. Target one real job. Then reveal the model answer to compare structure.

① why "I'm excited to apply because…"
② evidence "In my current role, I… [metric]"
③ close "I'd welcome the opportunity… Thank you."
0 words

Model (open → why → evidence → close, every chunk is a corpus row):

[open] I'm writing to apply for the Marketing Specialist role at Northwind. /ˈraɪtɪŋ tə əˈplaɪ/

[why] I'm excited to apply because of Northwind's focus on data-driven growth. /ɪkˈsaɪtɪd tə əˈplaɪ bɪˈkɒz/ UK

[evidence] In my current role, I led a campaign that grew sign-ups by 34% in one quarter. evidence adds a metric the CV does not make obvious — McGill: "add nuance to your C.V."

[close] I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss how I can contribute. Thank you for your consideration. /kənˌsɪd.əˈreɪ.ʃən/

Notice: line 1 names the role · the "because" forces a specific reason · the evidence has a number · the close is confident (no "hope you give me a chance").

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Long self-introduction open — "My name is…, I graduated from…" (the đơn template)Open with "I'm writing to apply for [role]" in line 1. The CV has your bio; the letter names the role.
Restates the CV — "I have 3 years of experience" (the CV already says this)Add evidence the CV does not make obvious. Pick one achievement and give the result. McGill: "add nuance to your C.V."
Skips the "why" — assumes motivation; no "I'm excited about this role because…"State why THIS role / THIS company specifically. No "why" = reads as a copy-paste template.
Modesty → under-sells — "I helped a little", drops the metricLet the number carry the confidence: "grew sign-ups by 34%". You state a fact, not brag.
Generic traits, no evidence — "I am hardworking, careful, responsible"Replace every trait with a proof point. "Hardworking" → "I led X, resulting in Y%."
Begging close — "I hope you will give me a chance" (mong được trao cơ hội)Close confidently: "I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss…". You offer value, not ask a favour.
Apologising for gaps — "I am sorry I do not have…"Pivot, don't apologise. Frame what you bring: "Most recently, I…". Never put a weakness in the close.
Over-formal / stiff — "I have the honour to submit herewith my humble application"Use clean professional register. "I'm writing to apply" is already formal; no "herewith" or "humble".
One generic letter for every job (role-template)Re-target each letter: name the company + the specific reason ("What draws me to [company] is…").
Missing article / plural in evidence — "I led team of five people"Enforce a/the + -s plurals in the evidence sentence — a dropped the reads careless to a recruiter.
