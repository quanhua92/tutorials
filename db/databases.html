① Database families — match the workload, not the hype

CAP theorem: across multiple nodes you keep at most two of Consistency · Availability · Partition-tolerance. Partitions are unavoidable, so the real choice is C-vs-A. PostgreSQL = CA (single shard), Cassandra = AP, MongoDB (strong) = CP.

③ Query planner — seq scan vs index scan, picked live

defaults: seq_page_cost=1.0  random_page_cost=4.0  cpu_tuple=0.01
Selectivity = fraction of rows the predicate matches = 1/distinct. Lower = fewer rows fetched = index wins. The planner switches to seq scan once selectivity crosses ~1.5% (the break-even point).
