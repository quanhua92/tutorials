① The quantization staircase — 32 fp16 weights vs their Q4_0 reconstruction

How to read: the teal bars are the original fp16 weights (a seeded 32-element block, the same one in gguf_quant.py Section A). The orange bars are the Q4_0 reconstruction ŵ = d·(q−8) — notice how many weights share the same orange level (the staircase): 16 levels cannot distinguish them. The thin yellow ticks are the per-element error. Scale d = amax/7 = 0.1511.

② The size/error ladder — drag N (params) and watch what fits edge RAM

How to read: bytes/param × N = total size. fp16 (2.0) is the baseline; Q4_0/Q4_K (0.5625) drop a 7B model from 14 GB to 3.94 GB — that is THE lever that fits it in a 6 GB phone budget. Q4_K_M (0.605) is the default (mixed Q6_K + Q4_K, best quality/size). The bytes/param figures are the verified GGML block-struct sizes (18 B/32 w, 34 B/32 w, 144 B/256 w).

③ The byte math — where 0.5625 bytes/param comes from

How to read: each block stores scale_bytes + quant_bytes. Q4_0 = 2 (fp16 scale) + 16 (32 nibbles packed 2/byte) = 18 B per 32 weights. The GOLD pin: 18/32 = 0.5625 bytes/param. The Q4_K super-block is 144 B per 256 weights — same bytes/param, but the 144 bytes buy a per-sub-block min.
