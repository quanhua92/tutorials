## A. Past simple — completed events (the backbone of any narrative)

The past simple narrates actions that **started and finished** in the past — the
main events of a story, in sequence. Formed with `-ed` (regular) or an irregular
stem (`go→went`, `ring→rang`, `see→saw`, `leave→left`). Confirmed in the British
Council "Past simple" reference and Oxford verb-form tables.

## B. Past continuous — background & interrupted actions

The past continuous paints what **was in progress** — the backdrop against which
a sudden (past simple) event lands. Form: *was/were* + `-ing`. Confirmed in the
British Council "Past continuous" reference.

| English chunk | meaning | IPA | source URL | frequency rank | accent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| was walking | ongoing movement on foot (background) | /wəz ˈwɔːkɪŋ/ UK · /wəz ˈwɑːkɪŋ/ US | https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/walk_1 | phrase | US/UK |
| was raining | weather in progress (background) | /wəz ˈreɪnɪŋ/ | https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/rain_1 | phrase | US/UK |
| was driving | ongoing act of driving (background) | /wəz ˈdraɪvɪŋ/ | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/drive | phrase | US/UK |
| was waiting | ongoing act of waiting (background) | /wəz ˈweɪtɪŋ/ | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/wait | phrase | US/UK |

> **Verification note:** the *was + -ing* pattern and the background/interrupting
> function ("The children **were doing** their homework when I **got** home";
> "The other day I **was waiting** for a bus when…"; "Last week, as I **was
> driving** to work,…") are attested verbatim on the British Council "Past
> continuous" page. `walk` -ing form /ˈwɔːkɪŋ/ confirmed in the Oxford verb-form
> table; `rain` /reɪn/ confirmed in Oxford (rain_1).

## C. Past perfect — earlier-than-past

The past perfect marks what had **already happened before** another past moment
— the "earlier past" layer that gives a narrative depth. Form: *had* + past
participle. Confirmed in the British Council "Past perfect" reference.

| English chunk | meaning | IPA | source URL | frequency rank | accent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| had finished | completed before another past moment | /həd ˈfɪnɪʃt/ | https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/finish_1 | phrase | US/UK |
| had left | gone away before another past moment | /həd left/ | https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/leave_1 | phrase | US/UK |
| had lost | misplaced before another past moment | /həd lɒst/ UK · /həd lɑːst/ US | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/lose | phrase | US/UK |
| had gone | departed before another past moment | /həd ɡɒn/ UK · /həd ɡɑːn/ US | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/go | phrase | US/UK |

> **Verification note:** the *had + past participle* form and the
> "earlier-than-past" function are attested verbatim on the British Council "Past
> perfect" page: "I **had finished** the work."; "She **had gone**."; "I couldn't
> get into the house. I **had lost** my keys."; "Teresa wasn't at home. She **had
> gone** shopping." `finished` /ˈfɪnɪʃt/ confirmed in the Oxford verb-form table
> (finish_1); `left` /left/ confirmed in Oxford (leave_1).

## D. The interplay — three tenses in one breath

This is the payoff of the bundle: the three tenses **interact** to build a
layered story. Each row below is the canonical grammar-book pattern, attested in
a real reference (British Council grammar pages + Oxford/Cambridge dictionary
example sentences).

### D1. Background + interrupting event (past continuous `when` past simple)

| English chunk | meaning | IPA | source URL | frequency rank | accent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| I was walking when I saw… | a background action cut by a sudden event | /aɪ wəz ˈwɔːkɪŋ wen aɪ sɔː/ | https://learnenglish.britishcouncil.org/grammar/english-grammar-reference/past-continuous | phrase (pinned) | US/UK |

> **Pinned sanity-check.** The exact pattern "I was walking … when I suddenly
> felt ill" is attested in the Oxford `walk_1` entry example list, and the "was
> …-ing when …" structure is illustrated verbatim on the British Council "Past
> continuous" page ("The other day I was waiting for a bus when…"). The chunk
> above is the canonical grammar-teaching compression of that attested pattern.

### D2. "By the time" + past perfect (an earlier past overtaken)

| English chunk | meaning | IPA | source URL | frequency rank | accent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| By the time we arrived, she had already left. | a later past arrives and finds an earlier one done | /baɪ ðə taɪm wiː əˈraɪvd ʃiː həd ɔːlˈredi left/ | https://learnenglish.britishcouncil.org/grammar/english-grammar-reference/past-perfect | phrase (pinned) | US/UK |

> **Pinned sanity-check.** The "by the time … had …" structure is the standard
> grammar reference illustration of past perfect (British Council "Past perfect"
> page documents the "started in the past and continued up to a given time in the
> past" function with *had*-examples; Cambridge Grammar of English treats "by the
> time + past perfect" as the canonical trigger). The chunk is the canonical
> compression: *arrived* (past simple) meets *had … left* (past perfect).

### D3. Sequence: "After … had … , … (past simple)"

| English chunk | meaning | IPA | source URL | frequency rank | accent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| After I had eaten, I left. | an earlier completed action, then the next event | /ˈɑːftər aɪ həd ˈiːtn̩ aɪ left/ | https://learnenglish.britishcouncil.org/grammar/english-grammar-reference/past-perfect | phrase | US/UK |

> **Verification note:** the "After + past perfect, past simple" sequence pattern
> is attested in British Council tutor responses ("After the plane **had
> landed**, we **collected** our luggage." / "After the plane **landed**, we
> **collected** our luggage." — both accepted) on the "Past simple" comments, and
> is the canonical sequencing use in the Cambridge Grammar reference.
