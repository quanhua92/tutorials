context_extension.py - RoPE context-extension scaling reference.
Pure Python stdlib. Numbers below feed CONTEXT_EXTENSION.md.
Sources: NTK-aware (reddit r/LocalLLaMA), YaRN paper arXiv:2309.00071.

Four methods: linear -> NTK-aware -> YaRN -> dynamic NTK

==========================================================================
  SECTION A: the RoPE frequency ladder (what we are scaling)
==========================================================================
RoPE base theta = 10000 (default), head_dim D = 4
freq_d = 1 / theta^(2d/D), for d in 0..D/2-1. angle_i_d = i * freq_d

| d | exponent 2d/D | theta^(2d/D) |   freq_d   | wavelength 2pi/freq |
|---|---------------|--------------|------------|----------------------|
| 0 |        0.0000 |         1.00 |   1.000000 |               6.2832 |
| 1 |        0.5000 |       100.00 |   0.010000 |             628.3185 |

freqs = [+1.0000, +0.0100]
d=0 is the HIGHEST frequency (wavelength ~6.3 positions): it encodes
LOCAL token-to-token structure. d=1 is lower (wavelength ~628): it
encodes LONG-RANGE structure. Every scaling method trades these off.

The extrapolation problem: at position i the model saw angle i*freq
only up to i = L_orig = 4096. Beyond that the angle is UNSEEN. The
angle at i = 4096 is the trained CEILING for each dim:

| d | freq_d   | angle at L_orig=4096 |
|---|----------|----------------------|
| 0 |   1.0000 |            4096.0000 |
| 1 |   0.0100 |              40.9600 |
[check] freq_0 = 1.0 for any theta :  OK
[check] freq_1 = 0.01 for theta=10000, D=4 :  OK  (got 0.01)

==========================================================================
  SECTION B: LINEAR scaling (Position Interpolation) - divide positions
==========================================================================
scale = L_target / L_orig = 4  (4096 -> 16384)
Method:  position_eff = position / scale   (apply to ALL positions)
Effect:  effective freq_d becomes freq_d / scale for EVERY dim.

| d | original freq | linear freq  | ratio |
|---|---------------|--------------|-------|
| 0 |        1.0000 |       0.2500 |  0.25 |
| 1 |        0.0100 |       0.0025 |  0.25 |

original = [+1.0000, +0.0100]
linear   = [+0.2500, +0.0025]

The HIGH-freq dim d=0 is ALSO divided by 4 (1.0 -> 0.25). That blurs
local structure: two adjacent tokens that used to differ by a full
rotation now differ by only a quarter. Quality degrades hard >4x.

At L_target=16384, linear angle_d = 16384*freq/4 = 4096*freq (the ORIGINAL ceiling):
| d | linear freq | angle at L_target | original ceiling | match? |
|---|-------------|-------------------|------------------|--------|
| 0 |      0.2500 |         4096.0000 |        4096.0000 |    YES |
| 1 |      0.0025 |           40.9600 |          40.9600 |    YES |
[check] linear divides every freq by scale :  OK
[check] linear maps L_target angle back to original ceiling :  OK

==========================================================================
  SECTION C: NTK-AWARE - adjust the base frequency  *** GOLD ***
==========================================================================
theta_new = theta * scale^(D/(D-2))
         = 10000 * 4^(4/(4-2))
         = 10000 * 4^2
         = 10000 * 16
         = 160000

| d | original freq | NTK freq     | ratio freq_new/freq |
|---|---------------|--------------|---------------------|
| 0 |        1.0000 |     1.000000 |            1.000000 |
| 1 |        0.0100 |     0.002500 |            0.250000 |

original = [+1.0000, +0.0100]
NTK      = [+1.000000, +0.002500]

KEY: d=0 (highest freq) is UNCHANGED (ratio 1.0) -> local structure
preserved. Only d=1 (low freq) is stretched (0.01 -> 0.0025). This is
why NTK beats linear: it never blurs the local dims.

  freq_1: 0.0100 -> 0.002500

==========================================================================
  SECTION D: YaRN - band-wise scaling (piecewise, with a ramp)
==========================================================================
YaRN needs several dim pairs to show its bands, so D=8 here ((4 freq pairs). Same theta=10000, scale=4).

Each dim is classified by how many full rotations its wavelength
completes over L_orig=4096:
  beta_fast=32 rotations  -> high-freq boundary (above = extrapolation)
  beta_slow=1  rotation   -> low-freq boundary  (below = interpolation)

| d | original | w_d (interp weight) | band        | YaRN freq  | linear | NTK freq |
|---|----------|---------------------|-------------|------------|--------|----------|
| 0 |   1.0000 |              0.0000 | extrapolation |   1.000000 | 0.2500 | 1.000000 |
| 1 |   0.1000 |              0.0000 | extrapolation |   0.100000 | 0.0250 | 0.062996 |
| 2 |   0.0100 |              0.4591 | ramp (blend) |   0.006557 | 0.0025 | 0.003969 |
| 3 |   0.0010 |              1.0000 | interpolation |   0.000250 | 0.0003 | 0.000250 |

original = [+1.0000, +0.1000, +0.0100, +0.0010]
YaRN     = [+1.000000, +0.100000, +0.006557, +0.000250]
linear   = [+0.2500, +0.0250, +0.0025, +0.0003]  (reference: uniform /scale)
NTK      = [+1.000000, +0.062996, +0.003969, +0.000250]  (reference: smooth base stretch)

YaRN preserves BOTH high-freq dims (d=0,1 unchanged, like NTK but a
SHARPER boundary), fully interpolates the lowest dim (d=3 = linear),
and blends the middle (d=2). YaRN also multiplies the attention logits
by an attention_factor ~ sqrt(1 + ln(scale)/ln(L_orig)) to compensate
for the distribution shift. Best quality at 8x-32x extension.

==========================================================================
  SECTION E: DYNAMIC NTK - scale grows per-token (streaming)
==========================================================================
For streaming (unknown final length) recompute theta each token:
  scale_t = max(1, current_position / L_orig), capped at target scale
  theta_t = theta * scale_t ^ (D/(D-2))

Within L_orig: scale=1 -> original theta (zero degradation). Past
L_orig theta grows gradually until it reaches the full NTK value.

| position | scale_t | theta_t  | freq_1 (low-freq dim) |
|----------|---------|----------|-----------------------|
|     2048 |  1.0000 |    10000 |              0.010000 |
|     4096 |  1.0000 |    10000 |              0.010000 |
|     8192 |  2.0000 |    40000 |              0.005000 |
|    12288 |  3.0000 |    90000 |              0.003333 |
|    16384 |  4.0000 |   160000 |              0.002500 |

At p=4096 (the trained boundary) theta is still 10000 (unchanged). The
model only starts stretching once it actually exceeds L_orig. This gives
gradual degradation instead of a fixed scale applied from token 0.

==========================================================================
  SECTION F: comparison - which method, which scale
==========================================================================
| method      | what it scales      | high-freq (local) | quality @4x | quality @8x+ | use case              |
|-------------|---------------------|-------------------|-------------|--------------|-----------------------|
| linear/PI   | positions (/scale)  | blurred (/scale)  | OK          | degrades     | simplest, old models  |
| NTK-aware   | base theta          | preserved (~1.0)  | good        | soft drop    | one-shot, no finetune |
| YaRN        | bands + attention factor | preserved         | best        | best         | extreme 8x-32x, finetuned |
| dynamic NTK | base theta per-token | preserved early   | good        | gradual      | streaming, unknown len |
