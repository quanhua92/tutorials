① The 12·H² law — depth vs width for a fixed budget

depth L (layers you can afford) per-layer 12·H² (cost of ONE layer)
What to do: drag H up and watch the depth bar collapse — halving H quadruples L (the 1/H² law). At H=512, N=125M you get L≈39.74 (deep-narrow); at H=1024 only L≈9.93 (shallow-wide). See DEPTH_VS_WIDTH.md §2.

② Layer sharing — ONE block applied K times (MobileLLM-LS)

shared block (reused, ONE set of weights) distinct block (its own weights) arrow = recurrent application x → Block(x)
What to do: toggle shared vs separate and slide K. Shared keeps unique params = 12·H² no matter how many times you reuse the block; separate pays K·12·H². Effective depth (= K) is identical either way — only the unique param bill differs. See DEPTH_VS_WIDTH.md §4.

③ Decode traffic — the edge win (memory-bandwidth bound)

What to do: raise K and watch the weight-read/token column drop while effective depth stays fixed. Decode latency scales with UNIQUE params (read once per token), not with depth — so sharing cuts latency without cutting quality. See DEPTH_VS_WIDTH.md §3 & §5.
