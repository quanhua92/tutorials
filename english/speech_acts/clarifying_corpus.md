## A. Ask to repeat ‚ÄĒ the listener didn't HEAR (the hearing problem)

The listener's side of the act when the **sound** didn't arrive clearly (noise,
speed, a bad phone line). This is the half Vietnamese learners fake most often:
rather than admit they missed it, they **smile and nod** ‚ÄĒ which in Vietnamese
culture preserves face but in English compounds the misunderstanding. The fix is
to ask for a repeat **immediately**, with one of these chunks. All four are
backchannellable into a single rising *Sorry?* once the learner owns the habit.

## B. Ask to explain ‚ÄĒ the listener didn't UNDERSTAND (the meaning problem)

The listener's side when they **heard** the words but the **meaning** didn't
land. This is the harder half for a Vietnamese learner, because admitting *"I
don't understand"* feels like a face loss ‚ÄĒ so the learner either fakes it
(*"Ah, yes"* + smile) or reaches for the blunter *"What?"* which English hears
as rude. The native move is a **hedged request to explain**: *What do you mean
by‚Ä¶?* / *I'm not sure I follow* / *Could you elaborate?* ‚ÄĒ polite on the
surface, precise underneath.
