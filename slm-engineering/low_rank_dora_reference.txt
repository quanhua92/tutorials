low_rank_dora_reference.txt
============================
Web provenance log for the LOW_RANK_DORA bundle (LoRA + DoRA, built from scratch).

Every formula, signature, year, author, init convention, and behavioural claim
is traced below to >=2 sources where possible. Distinct URLs: >= 2.
The reader-facing summary lives in LOW_RANK_DORA.md "## Sources".

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[1] https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.09353
    Liu et al 2024 "DoRA: Weight-Decomposed Low-Rank Adaptation" (ICML 2024;
    official). Project page: https://nbasyl.github.io/DoRA-project-page/
    PDF: https://raw.githubusercontent.com/mlresearch/v235/main/assets/liu24bn/liu24bn.pdf
    Verifies: the central claim of this bundle -- "DoRA decomposes the
    pre-trained weight into two components, magnitude and direction, for
    fine-tuning, specifically employing LoRA for directional updates." This is
    the canonical reference for the DoRA update
        W'_dora = (m / ||V||_col) odot V,   V = W + (alpha/r) B A,   m in R^k
    and for the empirical result that DoRA "matches or outperforms LoRA" and
    can match LoRA "at half the rank" (Section E / lineage recap). The
    decomposition reuses Salimans & Kingma's weight normalization per column
    (see [3]). Used in Sections B, E.

[2] https://magazine.sebastianraschka.com/p/lora-and-dora-from-scratch
    Sebastian Raschka, "Improving LoRA: Implementing Weight-Decomposed Low-Rank
    Adaptation (DoRA) from Scratch" (Ahead of AI, Feb 2024; authoritative
    from-scratch implementation). Companion code:
    https://github.com/rasbt/dora-from-scratch
    Verifies: the exact DoRA forward in PyTorch:
        numerator   = linear.weight + alpha * (A @ B).T      # = V = W + delta
        denominator = numerator.norm(p=2, dim=0, keepdim=True)  # ||V||_col
        new_weight  = m * (numerator / denominator)          # (m/||V||) odot V
        m = nn.Parameter(linear.weight.norm(p=2, dim=0, keepdim=True))  # init = ||W||_col
    This is the per-column normalisation the bundle implements in DoRALinear.
    Also confirms the magnitude vector "adds 0.01% more parameters" over LoRA
    (Section D: DoRA = LoRA + k, the +k being the magnitude vector), that the
    decomposition is "inspired by weight normalisation" (Salimans & Kingma
    2016), and that DoRA decouples magnitude/direction updates which LoRA
    "seems to lack the capability" to do (Section E's core claim). Used in
    Sections B, E.

[3] https://arxiv.org/abs/1602.07868
    Salimans & Kingma 2016 "Weight Normalization: A Simple Reparameterization
    to Accelerate Training of Deep Neural Networks" (NeurIPS 2016; official).
    Verifies: the magnitude/direction decomposition DoRA reuses. Weight
    normalisation reparameterises a weight vector w as w = (g / ||v||) * v,
    with a magnitude scalar g and a direction v. DoRA applies this PER COLUMN
    of the merged weight V (so the magnitude becomes a length-k vector m and
    the direction is V/||V||_col), and lets LoRA update V while m is trained
    separately. Section E cites this as the source of the factorisation
    m odot (W/||W||_col) == W.

[4] https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.09685
    Hu et al 2022 "LoRA: Low-Rank Adaptation of Large Language Models" (ICLR
    2022; official). The LoRA foundation this bundle builds on.
    Verifies: the LoRA forward h = W0 x + DeltaW x = W0 x + B A x (Eq. 3,
    Section 4.1); the init "a random Gaussian initialization for A and zero
    for B, so DeltaW = BA is zero at the beginning of training"; the alpha/r
    scaling ("We then scale DeltaW x by alpha/r"); and that "we let r be small
    ... DeltaW ... rank <= r". GPT-3 175B: ~10,000x fewer trainable params.
    Used in Sections A (LoRA from scratch, B=0 init, alpha/r, rank<=r), C, D.

[5] https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.14314
    Dettmers et al 2023 "QLoRA: Efficient Finetuning of Quantized LLMs"
    (NeurIPS 2023; official).
    Verifies: the QLoRA context cited in the lineage -- the frozen base W0 can
    be stored 4-bit NF4 while only the fp16 LoRA adapters A,B are trained
    (gradient flow is through the adapters only). DoRA is fully compatible
    with the QLoRA recipe (same adapter structure A,B plus the magnitude m).
    Context for the "mergeable / 4-bit base" discussion in the pitfalls table.

[6] ../llm/PEFT_LORA.md  (in-repo LoRA reference guide)
    Local path: /Volumes/data/workspace/tutorials/llm/PEFT_LORA.md
    The sibling-section LoRA foundations this bundle extends. Quoted directly
    in LOW_RANK_DORA.md as a cross-reference.
    Verifies: (in-repo, derived from [4]) the LoRA forward
    "y = W0 x + (alpha/r) B (A x)", the init "A ~ N(0,sig^2), B = 0 so
    DeltaW = BA = 0 at step 0", the param count "r(d+k)" vs full-FT "d*k",
    the ratio "r(d+k)/(dk) ~ r/min(d,k)", and the merge "W = W0 + (alpha/r)BA
    -> plain linear, ZERO added latency". The LoRALinear class here mirrors
    PEFT_LORA's peft_lora.py Step 0 / Step 1 structure.

[7] https://arxiv.org/html/2406.08447v1
    "The Impact of Initialization on LoRA Finetuning Dynamics" (Malladi et al,
    NeurIPS 2024 analysis).
    Verifies: "In standard LoRA training, one of the two LoRA matrices is
    initialized with random values and the other is initialized to zero" --
    confirming the A-random / B-zero init convention the bundle implements
    (Section A). Also confirms that B=0 at init makes the adapter a no-op on
    step 0 (DeltaW = BA = 0). Independent second source for [4]'s init claim.

[8] https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2024/file/d4387c37b3b06e55f86eccdb8cd1f829-Paper-Conference.pdf
    "The Impact of Initialization on LoRA Finetuning Dynamics" (NeurIPS 2024
    proceedings PDF). Mirror of [7].
    Verifies: the random/zero init split for A,B (independent mirror of [7]), and that the
    standard LoRA init yields DeltaW = 0 at step 0. Second authoritative
    source for the init convention, cross-checked against the original LoRA
    paper [4] and the Raschka from-scratch code [2].

[9] https://nbasyl.github.io/DoRA-project-page/
    DoRA official project page (Shih-Yang Liu, ICML 2024).
    Verifies: "DoRA decomposes the pre-trained weight into two components,
    magnitude and direction, for fine-tuning, specifically employing LoRA for
    directional updates" -- the verbatim abstract sentence that defines the
    method. Independent (non-arXiv) source for [1]'s central claim, and the
    primary public face of the DoRA authors' work.

[10] https://huggingface.co/docs/peft/main/en/package_reference/lora_variant_dora
     Hugging Face PEFT -- DoRA (Weight-Decomposed Low-Rank Adaptation) docs
     (official).
     Verifies: DoRA is shipped in the standard PEFT library as a LoRA variant
     (`use_dora=True`), confirming the production relevance and that DoRA
     "decomposes the updates of the weights into two parts, magnitude and
     direction. Direction is handled by normal LoRA, whereas the magnitude is
     [a separate trainable vector]." Documents the same forward as [1]/[2]:
     magnitude * (direction / ||direction||). Turning on use_dora=True "adds
     magnitude parameters and extra normalization math on top of LoRA's
     low-rank" adapters (HF forum corroboration).

[11] https://github.com/NVlabs/DoRA
     NVlabs/DoRA -- the OFFICIAL PyTorch implementation of DoRA
     ([ICML2024 Oral], by the paper authors).
     Verifies: the authors' own code applies the per-column weight-normalised
     update on top of the LoRA adapter pair (A, B) plus a magnitude vector m,
     matching the DoRALinear forward in this bundle:
         V = W + (alpha/r) * B @ A
         W'_dora = m * (V / V.norm(dim=0))      # broadcast over rows
     with m initialised to ||W||_col. Confirms the init (m = ||W||, B = 0) and
     the merge path used at deploy time. Independent second source for [1]'s
     method details, alongside the from-scratch reference [2].

UNVERIFIED / APPROXIMATE FACTS (flagged for honesty):
- The exact init std for A: the LoRA paper [4] uses Kaiming-uniform for A; the
  Raschka from-scratch code [2] uses sigma = 1/sqrt(r); the bundle uses
  sigma = 1/sqrt(r) for reproducibility. This choice does NOT affect any pinned
  number (at init B=0 so BA=0 regardless of A), and it is a standard choice.
- alpha = r (so alpha/r = 1, W' = W + BA) is the bundle's choice to match the
  paper's CORE formula verbatim; real recipes often set alpha = 2r or alpha = r
  independently. The gold param counts (16 / 20) do not depend on alpha.
- The "DoRA matches LoRA at half the rank" claim (Section E / lineage recap) is
  the empirical finding of [1] on LLM + ViT benchmarks; it is a trend, not a
  guarantee for every task. Stated as the paper's reported result.
- The 0.2% overhead figure for the magnitude vector is the DoRA paper's [1]
  report at typical scales (here recomputed at toy scale as +k over r(d+k), e.g.
  +4 over 16 = +25% at d=k=4,r=2; negligible at real scale d=k=4096,r=8).
