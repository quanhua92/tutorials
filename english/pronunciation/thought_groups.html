6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Run-on, breathless delivery — no pauses (Vietnamese is syllable-timed; pausing was never a meaning-tool in L1)Insert // at every grammatical boundary. A comma is a pause.
Pausing feels like "not fluent" — so you race through to sound smoothReframe: pausing is fluency. Native speakers pause constantly. A 0.3-second silence signals control, not weakness.
Pauses in the wrong place — "the // store", "to // go" (no determiner/infinitive grouping instinct)Apply the 3 rules: never inside the+noun, to+verb, or before a conjunction. Pause only at boundaries.
No breath plan — runs out of air mid-clause, gasps inside a wordPlan breaths at clause boundaries. The pause doubles as a breath: "Before you leave ↑(breathe) // could you send me the file?"
Equal stress on every word → no focus word, so groups don't close audiblyStress the last content word of each group + let the voice fall/rise there. 🔗 SENTENCE STRESS.
Monotone across a long turn — listener can't tell where one idea endsDrop pitch at the end of a final group (falling = "done"); rise mid-sentence (rising = "more coming"). 🔗 INTONATION.
Over-pauses at every word — choppy, robotic, one-word groupsGroup bigger: aim for 3–7 words per group. One-word groups are for emphasis, not the default.
