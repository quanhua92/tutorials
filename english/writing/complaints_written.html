5Writing task ★ primary

Write a full written complaint (issue + impact + ask + close). Be firm and factual — no emotion-leak (no CAPS, no !!!, no sarcasm). Then reveal the model.

Scenario: You ordered a laptop battery online; it arrived dead and would not charge. You tried it for two days. Write the company a one-paragraph complaint email asking for a replacement.

Model — 4 moves, firm + factual, zero emotion-leak:

[Issue] I am writing to express my concern about a laptop battery (model XB-200) I purchased from your store on 10 June. — date + item + stance, in facts

[Impact] The battery arrived dead and would not charge after two days of testing; as a result, I was unable to use my laptop for work. — the cost, factual past tense

[Ask] To resolve the problem, I would like a replacement, or a full refund within 14 days. — specific remedy + timeframe

[Close] Please look into this matter. I look forward to your prompt response. — firm + polite

Yours faithfully,
(your name)

✗ What NOT to write: "This is ABSOLUTELY UNACCEPTABLE!!! I want my money back NOW!!!" — emotion-leak weakens the case.

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Vent emotion — "This is ABSOLUTELY UNACCEPTABLE!!!", CAPS, exclamation overloadDelete every ! and CAP. Restate as a fact: "This does not match the advertised specification."
Under-asks / never complains — "if possible, maybe a small refund…"State the ask directly: "I would like a full refund." A vague ask gets no action; you also give up your rights.
No tense marking — "Yesterday I buy / I receive the item broken"Enforce past tense on every fact: "On 14 June, I purchased…" Past tense makes the timeline checkable.
Omitted articles — "I bought product from your shop"Supply a/an/the: "I bought a [model] from your shop."
No plural marking — "I received two item"Mark plurals: "I received two items."
Pro-drop / no subject — "Is unacceptable" / "Want refund"Supply subject + verb: "It is unacceptable." / "I would like a refund."
Indirectness as politeness — circles the point, never states the issueLead with the BLUF: "I am writing to express my concern about [X]." Indirectness reads as weak in English.
"Hope" where "expect" is needed — "I hope you will maybe refund"Use expect: "I expect a full refund within 14 days." Hope signals you'll accept inaction.
Threats / sarcasm to sound strong — "or I'll expose you online!!!"Replace with a stated next step + deadline: "I will wait until [date] before I contact a consumer protection agency."
Contractions in formal register — "I'd like / I'm writing / don't"Write them out: "I would like" / "I am writing" / "do not"
