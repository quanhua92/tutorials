Great-circle distance is a LOWER bound on fiber length. Real paths follow coastlines and political routes; measured RTT exceeds this by 10-30%. The effective 150 km/ms constant folds in routing + equipment overhead so cross-region RTTs land in the canonical 60-150ms band.

Active-passive is simple: one writer means no conflicts, but failover requires promotion (dead time). Active-active has zero-promotion failover (healthy region already serves traffic), but both regions can write the same record — see the conflict resolver below.

Cell-based architecture trades Nx operational surface area for bounded blast radius: a single cell failure affects 1/N of users, not 100%. Most teams should not build cells until they have exhausted two-region active-active.
