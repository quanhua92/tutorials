What to do: drag each slider. Bars scale to the visit count; the dashed red extension is the users lost to the next step. Defaults reproduce funnel_analysis.py Section 1 (100k → 62k → 37.2k → 11.16k, overall 11.16%).

② Drop-off visualizer — where users leak, ranked by absolute impact

What to do: this tracks the builder above. Red bars = users lost at each transition. The ranking prioritizes absolute users recovered (impact = entering × drop × 10%), not the worst percentage (funnel_analysis.py Section 2).

④ Segmentation — mobile vs desktop reveals where friction hides

What to do: toggle a segment. Mobile converts worse at every step (8.17% vs 16.72% overall) — the activate→purchase gap (25.0% vs 36.6%) is the loudest mobile-checkout signal (funnel_analysis.py Section 5).

⑤ Time-to-convert — P50 / P90 over the purchaser distribution

Histogram of visit→purchase time for the 11,160 purchasers. A long P90 (3.2 days) signals friction the step rates cannot see (funnel_analysis.py Section 3).

⑥ Simpson's paradox — aggregate “wins” while mobile regresses

What to do: the aggregate conversion rose 17.6% → 22.2% (+4.6 pp), but mobile fell 15.0% → 12.0%. The lift is a desktop traffic-mix shift, not product improvement (funnel_analysis.py Section 6).
