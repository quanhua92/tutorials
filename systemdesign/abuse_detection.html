A layered scoring pipeline that fuses velocity, pattern, reputation, and toxicity into one 0–1 risk score, then maps it (plus two hard rules) to a tiered action ALLOW → WARN → THROTTLE → CHALLENGE → BAN. ① Abuse score simulator — fuse four signals into one risk score

What to do: pick a scenario to load its signals, then drag the sliders. risk = 0.30·vel + 0.25·pat + 0.20·(1−trust) + 0.25·tox. Two hard rules override the score: vel≥0.95 AND pat≥0.80 → BAN (coordinated spam) and tox≥0.95 → BAN (severe toxicity). Notice frank and spambot land at the same risk (~0.73) but different actions — velocity alone is never a ban (abuse_detection.py Section 5).

② Reputation system visualizer — trust from age + history

What to do: drag the inputs. Trust blends four factors: trust = 0.35·min(1,age/365) + 0.25·engage + 0.20·verified + 0.20·0.8violations. The history term decays 20% per prior violation. Reputation risk fed into the fusion is 1 − trust — so a brand-new account is risky almost regardless of behavior (abuse_detection.py Section 4).

③ Content scanner — pattern regex catalog + toxicity lexicon

What to do: type a message (or load a scenario). Six weighted regex categories fire independently; the toxicity lexicon adds severities. Both sums cap at 1.0. In production the toxicity stage is a transformer classifier; the lexicon is its deterministic stand-in (abuse_detection.py Sections 2–3).

④ Action escalation flow — tier ladder, hard rules, repeat offenders

What to do: the risk score picks a tier; the two hard rules can jump straight to BAN. Then drag prior offenses: each repeat bumps the response one tier (warn before you ban). eve's borderline message is a WARN on its own, but four repeats reach BAN without the score ever changing (abuse_detection.py Section 6).
