📖 Read the full guide — diagrams, decision tables, common pitfalls, and a cheat sheet. This page is the interactive companion.
A RollingUpdate gradually replaces old v1 pods with new v2 pods, one reconcile pass at a time, never breaking two guardrails: total ≤ replicas+maxSurge and available ≥ replicas−maxUnavailable. Step through the pod grid and watch v1 (purple) drain as v2 (green, once ready) takes over. The new pods are orange while pending — a v2 pod only counts as available after its readiness probe passes. The canonical 5/1/1 trace is recomputed live in JS from the identical model as rolling_update.py.  |  ← Deployment/ReplicaSet

① The pod grid — v1 → v2, step by step

What to do: drag step to walk the rollout. Each pass the controller surges a v2 pod (room under maxSurge), then drains a v1 pod (room under maxUnavailable). With maxUnavailable=0 the drain is BLOCKED until the v2 pod clears readiness. Watch the available counter never drop below replicas−maxUnavailable.
v1 — old, serving v2 — pending (readiness not passed) v2 — ready, serving
Guardrails: total ≤ replicas+maxSurge and available ≥ replicas−maxUnavailable must hold at every step. If both knobs are 0 the rollout cannot start (K8s rejects it). Toggle bad image to watch a failed rollout HALT on ProgressDeadlineExceeded.

② maxSurge / maxUnavailable — the two knobs (5 replicas)

What this shows: the whole tuning surface. maxSurge buys speed with extra pods; maxUnavailable buys speed with lost capacity. Percent values round differently: maxSurge rounds UP, maxUnavailable rounds DOWN — so the default 25%/25% on 5 replicas is 2/1, not 1/1.
