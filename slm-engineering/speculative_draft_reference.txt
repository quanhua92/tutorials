speculative_draft_reference.txt - Web provenance log for SPECULATIVE_DRAFT.md.
Every formula, claim, and behavioural fact in this bundle is traced here to
>=2 independent sources. The .md's "## Sources" section is the reader-facing
summary of this file. Distinct URLs: 6.

[1] https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.17192
    Leviathan, Kalman, Matias 2023 "Fast Inference from Transformers via
    Speculative Decoding" (ICML 2023 Oral, official arXiv).
    Verifies: the foundational draft+verify pipeline. "decoding K tokens takes
    K serial runs of the model" (motivation for the 1-token/step memory wall);
    a small approximation/draft model proposes tokens and the large target
    verifies them "in parallel"; demonstrated "a 2X-3X acceleration" on T5-XXL
    "with identical outputs" (no quality loss). This is THE paper the
    draft->verify->accept/resample loop is built from. The accept rule
    (min(1, p/q)) and the speedup bound S = (1-a^(K+1))/((1-a)(1+K*cost)) both
    originate here. See also the author's ICML talk:
    https://yanivle.github.io/ai/2023/07/25/speculative-decoding-icml.html

[2] ../llm/SPECULATIVE_DECODING.md  (in-repo sibling guide)
    The big-model version of speculative decoding, in the ../llm/ section.
    Local path: /Volumes/data/workspace/tutorials/llm/SPECULATIVE_DECODING.md
    Verifies: THIS bundle is its INVERSE. There a big model speculatively
    decodes ITSELF and the focus is the rejection-sampling math (accept iff
    u <= min(1, p/q); resample from max(0,p-q)/Z) that preserves the FULL
    target distribution p exactly. HERE the SLM is the DRAFT serving a bigger
    TARGET. The accept/resample loop is structurally identical; only the
    perspective flips. Section A (arithmetic intensity ~1 FLOP/byte decode,
    ~K FLOP/byte spec), Section B (parallel verify + causal mask), and
    Section E (the speedup formula) are the direct lineage of this bundle's
    Section A/B/D. The speedup bound used here,
    S = E[tokens/forward] / (1 + gamma/kappa), is the same formula with
    gamma_cost = 1/kappa (kappa = draft_speed_advantage).

[3] https://cseweb.ucsd.edu/~yiying/cse291a-fall25/reading/spec-decoding.pdf
    Abhyankar, "Speculative Decoding" (UCSD CSE291A LLM-Inference lecture,
    Fall 2025) -- a classroom walkthrough of Leviathan 2023 + EAGLE.
    Verifies: the draft model "should be order(s) of magnitude smaller", e.g.
    "LLM = 7B, SSM = 68M" (the toy's draft-vs-target sizing claim). The draft
    is "also called small specialized model (SSM) or draft model", shares
    "the same vocabulary size as the LLM (target model)", and is "ideally
    trained on the same data". Confirms "2-3x speedup depending on token
    acceptance rate (TAR)" and that "as speculative depth increases, so does
    overhead; if TAR is too low, this overhead can dominate" (the draft-too-
    weak / gamma-too-large pitfalls). Walks the (1) drafting -> (2)
    verification two-step pipeline this bundle implements in Sections A-B.

[4] https://docs.vllm.ai/en/latest/features/speculative_decoding/
    vLLM Documentation -- "Speculative Decoding".
    Verifies: the GREEDY VARIANT this bundle implements. vLLM explicitly lists
    "Greedy Sampling Equality: Confirms that greedy sampling with speculative
    decoding matches greedy sampling without it." This is the exact claim
    asserted in Section C: accept token i iff target_argmax_i == draft_token_i,
    resample at the first mismatch by taking the target's greedy pick ->
    output is byte-for-byte the greedy target output (zero quality loss).
    Confirms production speculative decoding pairs "a target model" with "a
    lightweight draft mechanism that quickly proposes candidate tokens" which
    are "then verified by a larger" target (the NVIDIA framing mirrored at
    https://developer.nvidia.com/blog/an-introduction-to-speculative-decoding-for-reducing-latency-in-ai-inference/).

[5] https://research.google/blog/looking-back-at-speculative-decoding/
    Google Research Blog -- Leviathan, "Looking back at speculative decoding".
    Verifies: "Speculative decoding has proven to be an effective technique for
    faster and cheaper inference from LLMs without compromising quality."
    Author is Yaniv Leviathan (first author of [1]); this retrospective is a
    second independent statement from the same team confirming the lossless
    speedup claim and the draft+verify structure. Independently corroborates
    that the output distribution is preserved ("without compromising quality").

[6] https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.15077
    Li, Wei, Zhang, Zhang 2024 "EAGLE: Speculative Sampling Requires Rethinking
    Feature Uncertainty" (ICML 2024, official arXiv).
    Verifies: a draft-model VARIANT for context. EAGLE uses "a lightweight
    autoregressive head operating on the feature (second-to-top-layer) level"
    rather than a separate small model, and "achieved 2.7x-3.5x latency
    speedup on LLaMA2-Chat 70B with doubled throughput". Cited in Section D's
    "Published" figures to show the realistic speedup range (2.7x-3.5x) at
    alpha ~70-85%. (Medusa arXiv:2401.10774 is the sibling head-based variant.)
