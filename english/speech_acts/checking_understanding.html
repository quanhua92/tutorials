5Writing task — paraphrase-confirm line

Imagine a colleague just said: "We're pushing the deadline to Friday because QA found a blocker." Write one paraphrase-confirm line using So you're saying… or If I understand correctly,…. Then reveal the model.

Model (listener side — confirm by paraphrasing, don't just say "yes"):

So you're saying the deadline moves to Friday because of the QA blocker? /səʊ jʊə ˈseɪ.ɪŋ/ — neutral, conversational; ends with a rising tag so the speaker can confirm.

Slightly more formal variant:

If I understand correctly, we have until Friday because QA found a blocker. /ɪf aɪ ˌʌndəˈstænd kəˈrektli/ — hedges the paraphrase; the *correctly* does the politeness work that a Vietnamese *vâng* does silently.

Pattern: opener + restate the content in your own words + (optional) tag question. Never just say *"Yes, I understand"* — the paraphrase is the proof.

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
One token vâng / dạ signals attention, not comprehension → nods "yes" through an explanation without followingInsert a speaker-check every 3–4 sentences: Does that make sense? / Are you with me? Don't trust the nod — ask.
Nods "yes" to Does that make sense? without understanding (face-saving; "no" feels like failure)Say Not quite + paraphrase what you did get. In English that shows competence, not failure.
Skips paraphrasing → says "Yes, I understand" instead of restating the contentConfirm by paraphrasing the content: So you're saying the deadline moved to Friday? — gives the speaker a chance to correct you.
Uses Right? in both directions as speaker-check and listener-confirmRight? is a speaker tag only. As a listener, restate: So, in other words,… / If I understand correctly,…
Says "Yes, yes" rapidly to mean "stop explaining, I get it" (L1 impatience/face)English hears "Yes, yes" as rude dismissal. Say Got it or Makes sense once, calmly.
Drops finals on the check itself → "Does that make sens?" (no /s/), "If I understand correcly" (no /t/)Release every final — the /s/ in sense, the /-ktli/ in correctly carry the check's meaning. 🔗 Final Consonants.
Vietnamese /r/ (tap/trill) in right? instead of the English approximant /raɪt/Drill the English /r/: tongue tip curled back, no trill. Spot-check against the YouGlish clip for right.
/ð/ → /z/ in with me → "wiz me" (drops the tongue-between-teeth)Tongue-between-teeth for /ð/ in with. Minimal pair: with /wɪð/ vs wit /wɪt/. 🔗 TH Sounds.
Confuses Does that make sense? (you ask) with Makes sense. (you confirm) — same words, opposite directionLearn them as two chunks by direction. Does that…? = you ask them. Makes sense. = you confirm.
Silence after a paraphrase — waits for the speaker to keep goingAlways tag the paraphrase: So you're saying… is that right? — then they can say Exactly / Actually, no — …
Over-uses Are we on the same page? in casual chat → sounds stiffReserve same page for meetings/alignment. In casual chat: Does that make sense? / You know what I mean?
