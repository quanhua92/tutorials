==========================================================================
  SECTION A: WHY MUSIC IS HARDER THAN SPEECH
==========================================================================
Speech: single speaker, seconds-long, limited acoustic patterns.
Music : many instruments + vocals, minutes-long, harmony + rhythm.
Direct autoregressive generation on RAW audio is intractable: every
sample must be predicted in sequence, and there are millions of them.

Raw audio budget (CD quality, 44.1kHz stereo):
| clip            |  samples   | 16-bit PCM |  fp32     |
|-----------------|------------|------------|-----------|
| 1 s             |     88,200 |   0.18 MB |   0.35 MB |
| 10 s            |    882,000 |   1.76 MB |   3.53 MB |
| 1 min           |  5,292,000 |  10.58 MB |  21.17 MB |
| 3 min           | 15,876,000 |  31.75 MB |  63.50 MB |

==========================================================================
  SECTION B: NEURAL AUDIO CODECS - raw audio -> discrete tokens (RVQ)
==========================================================================
EnCodec / SoundStream compress raw audio to discrete tokens via
RESIDUAL VECTOR QUANTIZATION (RVQ):
  1. encoder strides the waveform (downsample 320x at 24kHz)
  2. each frame is quantized by N codebooks IN SERIES
     - codebook 1: coarse structure  (big residual removed)
     - codebook 2: residual of 1     (finer detail)
     - ... up to codebook 8:          (fine texture / noise floor)
  3. decoder reconstructs audio from the N indices per frame

EnCodec 24kHz config: stride = 320  ->  frame rate = 24000/320 = 75 frames/s
  with 8 RVQ codebooks:  600 tokens/s  (24000/75 = 320x downsample on the time axis)

Token budget vs raw audio (3-minute clip):
| representation                |   values   | vs raw stereo |
|-------------------------------|------------|---------------|
| raw 44.1kHz stereo (samples)  | 15,876,000 |           1x fewer |
| raw 24kHz mono (samples)      |  4,320,000 |           4x fewer |
| EnCodec frames (75Hz)         |     13,500 |        1176x fewer |
| EnCodec tokens (x8 codebooks) |    108,000 |         147x fewer |

==========================================================================
  SECTION C: ACE-STEP vs YuE - the two SOTA open-source paths
==========================================================================
Both turn text (genre/mood/instruments) + lyrics into a full song
(vocals + accompaniment). Very different compute profiles:

  ACE-Step (3.5B): FLOW-MATCHING. Denoises the whole song latent in
    PARALLEL over a fixed number of ODE steps (~27-60). Not token-by-
    token. => ~2.2 s/min on A100. Min VRAM 8 GB. Beats Suno v5 on
    melody/harmony/rhythm benchmarks. Supports remix/repaint/edit.

  YuE (7B): TWO-STAGE AUTOREGRESSIVE cascade. Stage 1 (semantic) turns
    lyrics -> semantic tokens one at a time; stage 2 (acoustic) expands
    them to codec tokens. Great lyric alignment, but slow (sequential).

Model comparison (verified from GitHub READMEs):
| model            | params | VRAM    | architecture              | license    |
|------------------|--------|---------|---------------------------|------------|
| ACE-Step         |   3.5B | 8-10    | flow-matching, parallel ODE steps (27-60) | Apache 2.0 |
| YuE              |     7B | 8-14    | two-stage cascade: semantic -> acoustic (autoregressive) | Apache 2.0 |
| Stable Audio Open |   1.2B | 6-8     | diffusion (DiT), latent   | CC-BY-NC (non-commercial) |
| MusicGen (Meta)  |   3.3B | 8-16    | autoregressive EnCodec tokens, mono | CC-BY-NC   |
| DiffRhythm       |     2B | 8-10    | diffusion over full-song latent | Apache 2.0 |

==========================================================================
  SECTION D: THE PIPELINE - text + lyrics -> full song
==========================================================================
Both ACE-Step and YuE share the same high-level flow; they differ in
stage 4 (how the latent/tokens are produced):

  [1] prompt encode : text tags (genre/mood/instruments) + lyrics
                       -> text embeddings (T5/CLAP-style)
  [2] lyrics align   : syllable/line timestamps (YuE explicit;
                       ACE-Step implicit via REPA alignment)
  [3] latent space   : audio codec (DCAE/EnCodec) frames the model
                       operates in (75 Hz, 8 codebooks)
  [4] GENERATE       : ACE-Step = parallel flow-matching (~27 steps)
                       YuE      = autoregressive cascade (semantic->
                                   acoustic, token-by-token)
  [5] codec decode   : tokens/latent -> raw waveform (codec decoder)
  [6] post           : vocals + accompaniment mixed -> stereo song

For a 3-min song the model produces 13,500 frames (108,000 tokens) in stage 4, then the codec decoder
turns them back into ~4.32M samples (24kHz mono) of audio.

Why ACE-Step is fast: stage 4 visits a FIXED ~27 ODE steps, each
touching the ENTIRE song latent at once. YuE must emit ~108,000 tokens
sequentially. Parallel (fixed steps) >> sequential (~100k steps).
