Killer gotchas (verbatim from two_heaps.py):
  1. Don't be clever on insert: always push to small, pop its top to large, then rebalance. Never if num < small[0] — the 3-step insert handles first elements and duplicates with zero branches.
  2. Negate for max: small stores -val; every max read is -small[0]. Forget the sign on either side and you silently get a min-heap.
  3. Lazy deletion keys on the VALUE: the median is a multiset property — you never need which copy expired. Value-keying dodges the "is this boundary 3 in small or large?" bug that breaks (val,idx) classifiers on duplicates.
  4. Prune before you peek: pop while delayed[top] > 0 before every median read and size check. Buried stale entries are harmless.
  5. Track logical sizes, not len(heap): the physical heap still holds lazily-deleted ghosts. Maintain ssz/lsz counters.
  6. Negate the timestamp in P355: -time flips heapq into newest-first order.
