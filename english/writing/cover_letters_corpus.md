## A. Open ‚ÄĒ name the role (the opener)

The first line of an English cover letter does one job: **tell the reader which
role and why you are writing**, in one sentence. No preamble ("My name is‚Ä¶ and I
am writing this letter to‚Ä¶"), no life story. Harvard OCS and the University of
Michigan Career Center both model the opener as *"I am writing to apply for the
[role]‚Ä¶"*. Two registers, from standard to slightly softer.

## B. Why-you / why-me ‚ÄĒ fit + motivation (the "because")

The English cover letter's second move is **why THIS role, why THIS company, why
ME**. This is the move a Vietnamese L1 writer most often skips ‚ÄĒ Vietnamese
application letters (ńĎ∆°n xin viŠĽác) are role-generic and motivation is assumed.

## C. Evidence ‚ÄĒ one concrete achievement (the proof)

## D. Close ‚ÄĒ confident ask + thanks (the closer)
