The two-kitchens idea: co-locating prefill (compute-bound, TTFT) and decode (memory-bound, ITL) on one GPU (🔗 SCHEDULER) makes a long prefill block every decode → ITL spikes 20ms→100ms. DistServe/Mooncake split them onto separate pools and transfer the KV over RDMA. Every number is recomputed in JS from the identical formulas in disaggregated_serving.py — bandwidths are real; the transport is a faithful simulation.  |  LMCACHE (KV transfer) · PAGED_ATTENTION · KV_CACHE (the paged bytes that move)

① Prefill vs decode — opposite computational profiles

Reading: arithmetic intensity AI = S/bytes. Prefill (S=512): AI=256 ≥ crossover 156 → COMPUTE-bound (saturates math units). Decode (S=1): AI=0 < 156 → MEMORY-bound (starved for weight BW). They stress DIFFERENT resources → co-location = interference. See DISAGGREGATED_SERVING.md §1.

② Co-location interference — the ITL spike (🔗 SCHEDULER baseline)

What to do: drag the sliders. Co-located: when a prefill arrives, the decode batch waits → ITL = prefill + decode (spike!). Disaggregated: prefill runs on a separate pool → decode ITL stays flat. See DISAGGREGATED_SERVING.md §2.

③ The disaggregated architecture — prefill pool → RDMA → decode pool

PREFILL POOL (compute-tuned)

• tensor parallel (low TTFT)
• large S-token chunks
• saturates math units
• PRODUCES KV

DECODE POOL (memory-tuned)

• data/pipeline parallel
• many 1-token streams
• saturates HBM bandwidth
• CONSUMES KV
Reading: after prefill, the KV is transferred over RDMA to the decode pool's block table (🔗 LMCACHE / PAGED_ATTENTION). The two pools are tuned independently — making TTFT better never hurts ITL. See DISAGGREGATED_SERVING.md §3.

④ The budget: is KV transfer cheaper than re-prefilling? (GOLD)

What to do: drag S and pick a network. Size_KV grows with S; Latency_transfer = Size_KV / BW. The red bar is the prefill roofline FLOOR (real ~80ms is higher). When the green bar stays under the red bar, transfer wins — disaggregate, don't re-prefill. At S=512, RoCE 400G: transfer 1.34 ms vs prefill floor 22.9 ms (~17×). See DISAGGREGATED_SERVING.md §6.

⑤ Co-located vs disaggregated — the end-to-end contrast

Reading: disaggregation trades a ~2% TTFT increase (the KV transfer) for the elimination of ITL spikes AND independent per-pool optimization. That trade is why DistServe, Mooncake, Splitwise, and DéjàVu all adopt it. See DISAGGREGATED_SERVING.md §7.
