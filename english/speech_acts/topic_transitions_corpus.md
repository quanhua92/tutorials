## A. Link via association (the "related thought" joiners)

These are the chunks that bridge from the **current topic to a related one** by
naming the association out loud. Pragmatically they are **topic-orienting
discourse markers** (Fraser, 2009; the JSTOR survey of English discourse
markers lists both `speaking of` and `that reminds me` in exactly this class).
They tell the listener "*this new thing belongs here because it connects to what
you just said*" ‚ÄĒ so the conversation stays cohesive instead of jumping.

## B. Shift to a new subject (the "I'm changing the topic" markers)

These are the chunks that **break with the current topic** rather than link to
it. They flag a topic boundary so the listener is not left guessing whether the
speaker is still on the same subject. `By the way` introduces a side point that
may be unrelated; `Changing the subject` and `Moving on` flag a deliberate
topic advance (the latter is the standard meeting-room signpost).

## C. Return to an earlier subject (the "getting back" markers)

These are the chunks that **resume a previous topic** ‚ÄĒ either the speaker's

are the mirror of the shifters: where `By the way` opens a side trip, `Anyway`
