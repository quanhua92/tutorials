========================================================================
  SECTION D: param cost (linear) vs inference compression (sublinear)
========================================================================
Two forces with vocab size V, for a fixed text corpus:

  (1) PARAM COST:    embed = V*H            -> grows LINEARLY in V.
  (2) COMPRESSION:   tokens(V) = tokens(V0)*(V0/V)**alpha  (BPE: alpha<1,
                       sublinear -- bigger vocab packs more chars/token but
                       with diminishing returns). Inference steps for a fixed
                       text DROP as tokens drop. Savings = 1 - tokens(V)/V0.

Model (illustrative; alpha = 0.40 is a typical BPE compression exponent):
| V        | embed params | embed vs V0 | tokens vs V0 | savings/seq | cost grows faster? |
|----------|--------------|-------------|--------------|-------------|---------------------|
|   32,768 |   67,108,864 |       1.00x |        1.000 |       0.0%  | no (still worth it) |
|   49,152 |  100,663,296 |       1.50x |        0.850 |      15.0%  | YES (tax > savings) |
|   65,536 |  134,217,728 |       2.00x |        0.758 |      24.2%  | YES (tax > savings) |
|  128,256 |  262,668,288 |       3.91x |        0.579 |      42.1%  | YES (tax > savings) |
|  256,000 |  524,288,000 |       7.81x |        0.439 |      56.1%  | YES (tax > savings) |

  * Param cost scales as V (linear); compression scales as V^(-alpha)
    (sublinear). So cost outruns compression -- past some point each extra
    10k tokens of vocab buys less compression than the params it costs.
  * This is WHY the SLM sweet spot is ~32k-64k: you capture most of the
    compression of a 128k vocab at a fraction of the param tax.

  [check] param cost grows linearly (2x V -> 2x embed): OK
  [check] alpha in (0,1) so diminishing returns hold: OK

========================================================================
  SECTION E: the SLM vocab decision -- the ~32k-64k sweet spot
========================================================================
Rule of thumb (web-verified, see _reference.txt):
  * 1-3B SLMs (SmolLM2, Qwen2.5-1.5B):  V ~ 49k-152k. SmolLM2 picks 49k
    deliberately; Qwen2.5 picks 152k because it must cover Chinese+code.
  * 7B+ models (Llama-3):               V ~ 128k-256k affordable -- the
    tax is a small % of a big N.

Decision drivers:
  - multilingual / heavy-code deployment  -> lean BIGGER (more coverage,
    fewer tokens/text, faster inference per sequence).
  - single-domain edge SLM (English tool)  -> lean SMALLER (buy back body
    capacity for attention/MLP). Tie embeddings.  (🔗 SHARED_EMBEDDINGS)

Lineage quantified (H=2048, tied):
| era            | model         | V       | embed     | rationale             |
|----------------|---------------|---------|-----------|-----------------------|
| big-model era  | Llama-3-8B    | 128,256 |   525.3M* | 128k vocab cheap at 8B; untied |
| SLM era        | SmolLM2-1.7B  |  49,152 |    100.7M | smaller vocab frees body capacity |
  (*525.3M is one table at H=4096; SmolLM2 row is at H=2048.)
