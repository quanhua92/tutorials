What this shows: bars are on a log scale (NL would be off-the-chart otherwise). Toggle whether the inner has a B-tree index and whether inputs are pre-sorted; the gold outline marks the cheapest applicable algorithm — exactly what the optimizer picks.
log₁₀ scale · slider is log₁₀(size)
Formulas (Selinger 1979): NL = N·M · Indexed NL = N·log₂M · Hash = N+M · Merge (sorted) = N+M · Merge+sort = N·log₂N + M·log₂M + N+M · Grace = 3·(N+M). At N=M=10⁶, NL hits 10¹² — impossible — while Hash stays at 2·10⁶.
