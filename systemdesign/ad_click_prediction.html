① CTR calculator — Beta-Binomial smoothing kills noisy small-sample rates

What to do: drag impressions / clicks. Raw CTR = clicks÷impressions is a maximum-likelihood estimate — noisy on small samples. Smoothing (clicks + α) / (impressions + α + β) shrinks it toward the prior mean α/(α+β). A 3-impression ad with raw 33% becomes 8.70% — the cold-start fix (ad_click_prediction.py Section 1).

④ Online learning — streaming SGD chases drift a batch model can't see

What to do: step through the drift stream. After ship, users get search ad fatigue (4 search misses). The online model pulls w_search down per impression; the batch model is frozen. Held-out log-loss: online 0.799 vs batch 1.039 (ad_click_prediction.py Section 4).

⑤ A/B test — is the CTR lift real or noise?

What to do: set the four counters. A two-proportion z-test asks if Model B's CTR beats Model A beyond chance. Ship if p < 0.05 and the effect is business-meaningful. Defaults: A 450/10000 (4.50%) vs B 540/10000 (5.40%) → z=2.93, significant (ad_click_prediction.py Section 5).
