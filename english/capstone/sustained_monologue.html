Topic: How to give effective feedback

Model (frame → 3 signposted points → recap/conclusion):

Frame — I'd like to cover three points — why feedback matters, how to structure it, and what to avoid. /aɪd ˈlaɪk tə ˈkʌvər θriː ˈpɔɪnts/ — preview the map

Point 1 — Let me start with why it matters. Feedback is the cheapest way to improve performance. /ˌlet mi ˈstɑːrt wɪð/ — launches point 1

Transition — Moving on to how to structure it. The SBI model works because it keeps things specific. /ˈmuːvɪŋ ˈɑːn tuː/ — shift to next section

Point 2 → 3 — That brings me to what to avoid. Turning to common mistakes, the biggest one is vague criticism. As I mentioned earlier, specificity is what makes feedback stick. chain + pivot + back-reference

Recap + conclude — To recap: feedback matters, structure it with SBI, and avoid vagueness. In conclusion, good feedback is specific, timely, and kind. That covers everything I wanted to say. /tə ˈriːkæp/ + /ɪn kənˈkluːʒn/ — close the loop

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Skips the frame entirely — dives into point 1 with no "I'd like to cover three points…"Always preview the map first: "I'd like to cover three points…" 20 seconds of frame saves the listener for 5 minutes.
Jumps between points with no transition — the listener loses the thread after 90 secondsFire a signpost before every point: "Moving on to… / That brings me to… / Turning to…". Rehearse one transition per gap.
Rambles and runs dry — front-loads everything into point 1, talk dies at 2 minutesBudget one idea per point. The frame ("three points") forces you to spread material across the whole talk.
Speeds up nervously as time runs out — finals dropped, intelligibility collapsesDo not speed up. A calm pace with audible finals signals control. Pause between points, never inside a chunk.
No concluding signpost — the talk trails off into "…so, yeah"Land it: "To recap…" then "In conclusion…" then "That covers everything I wanted to say."
Translates word-by-word from Vietnamese — slow, halting, loses the thread mid-sentenceRetrieve chunks, not words. "I'd like to cover three points" is one unit. Drill the 8 chunks until they fire as blocks.
Pro-drop / omitted subject → "Want to cover three points"Supply the subject + modal: "I'd like to…" The subject is load-bearing in a monologue.
Drops the final consonant in point /pɔɪnt/ → "poin", conclusion /kənˈkluːʒn/ → "conclu-zhun"Release every final: /t/ on point, start; the /n/ on conclusion. A dropped final kills the signpost.
No pausing between thought groups — runs everything together, breathlessPause between points (after the signpost), not inside. The pause is where you breathe and plan the next point.
Treats the talk as a list, not a story — no narrative staminaBorrow the story arc (setting → tension → turn → payoff) for stamina and engagement.
