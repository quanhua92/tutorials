## Why this is bundle #22 (read this first)

A Vietnamese speaker can open a conversation politely (after Phase 1 #11) and
keep it going (small talk, agreeing, checking understanding). But **how you
leave is what the other person remembers last.** Vietnamese L1 has two failure
modes at the exit:

1. **Too abrupt** ‚ÄĒ the learner simply stops talking and says *"bye"* or even
   just walks off. To a native ear this reads as rude, bored, or angry.
2. **Too prolonged** ‚ÄĒ the learner feels the abrupt exit is impolite, so they
   over-compensate with endless *"ok‚Ä¶ so‚Ä¶ yeah‚Ä¶ ok‚Ä¶ bye‚Ä¶ bye‚Ä¶ bye"*, looping
   the farewell.

Native English splits the difference with a **two-step close**: a
**pre-closing signal** ("I should let you go") that *announces* the end is
coming, then a **leave-taking** ("Take care") that actually ends it. This bundle
drills that two-step until it is automatic.

## 1. The mechanism: the two-step close

The single most important idea in this bundle. A native close is almost never
one turn ‚ÄĒ it is **signal ‚Üí farewell**:

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    PRE["PRE-CLOSING SIGNAL<br/>(announces the end is coming)<br/>'Well, I should let you go.'<br/>'I'd better get going.'<br/>'Look at the time.'"] --> APP{"appreciation?<br/>(optional but common)"}
    APP -->|"yes"| THX["'It was great talking to you.'"]
    APP -->|"skip"| LEAVE
    THX --> LEAVE["LEAVE-TAKING<br/>(actually ends it)<br/>'Take care.' / 'See you.'<br/>'Let's catch up soon.'"]
    style PRE fill:#fef3c7,stroke:#f59e0b,stroke-width:2px
    style THX fill:#dbeafe,stroke:#58a6ff,stroke-width:2px
    style LEAVE fill:#dcfce7,stroke:#27ae60,stroke-width:3px
```

The pre-closing does **two jobs at once**: it warns the other person ("we're
wrapping up") *and* it offers them a graceful acceptance turn ("yeah, me too,
take care"). Skip it and the leave-taking lands like a door slamming.

## 2. Pre-closing signals ‚ÄĒ "we're winding down"

These are the turns that **announce** the conversation is ending *before* the
goodbye. They are the chunk a Vietnamese learner most often skips.

| Chunk | When to use it | Pragmatic note |
|---|---|---|
| **Well, I should let you go.** | The other person seems busy, or you want to release them politely | Reframes *your* intent to leave as *consideration for them*. Pinned example. |
| **I'd better get going.** | You are the one who needs to leave | Owns the exit; informal. Cambridge `get going` = "to start to go or move." |
| **I won't keep you.** | The other person was doing you a favor / giving time | "I won't take up more of your time" ‚ÄĒ gracious. |
| **Look at the time.** | The hour is genuinely late; a soft, indirect exit | Idiomatic; the *time itself* is the excuse, not the speaker. |
| **Anyway,‚Ä¶** | Discourse marker that returns to / wraps the thread before closing | Almost always followed by a signal or farewell. |

> From `closings_corpus.md`:
>
> - **Well, I should let you go.** /wel a…™  É äd let juňź …°…ô ä/ UK ¬∑ /wel a…™  É äd let juňź …°o ä/ US
> - **I'd better get going.** /a…™d ňąbet.…ôr …°et ňą…°…ô ä.…™Ňč/ UK ¬∑ /a…™d ňąbet.…ö …°et ňą…°o ä.…™Ňč/ US
> - **I won't keep you.** /a…™ w…ô änt kiňźp juňź/ UK ¬∑ /a…™ wo änt kiňźp juňź/ US
> - **Look at the time.** /l äk …ôt √į…ô ta…™m/

**The Vietnamese trap:** there is no obligatory pre-closing slot in casual
Vietnamese ‚ÄĒ you can signal the end with a tone shift or simply stop. In English
the pre-closing is *expected*; its absence reads as rudeness or boredom.

## 3. Leave-taking ‚ÄĒ the goodbye itself

These follow the pre-closing signal. Note the **register ladder**:

| Chunk | Register | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Goodbye.** | Neutral ‚Üí formal | The "safe default," but sounds stiff in casual speech. |
| **Take care.** | Warm, all-register | Cambridge: *"Bye, Melissa." "Goodbye Rozzie, take care."* |
| **See you. / See ya.** | Casual | Cambridge `see you (later)` = "goodbye." |
| **Talk to you later.** | Casual | Hints at future contact. |
| **Talk soon.** | Casual, warm | Shorter cousin of "talk to you later." |
| **It was great talking to you.** | Appreciation turn | Often *precedes* the farewell ‚ÄĒ the bridge between signal and goodbye. |
| **Let's catch up soon.** | Future-plan + farewell | Cambridge `catch up` = "to talk to someone you have not seen for a while." Pinned example. |

> From `closings_corpus.md`:
>
> - **Take care.** /te…™k ke…ôr/ US ¬∑ /te…™k ke…ô/ UK ‚ÄĒ Cambridge-attested verbatim
>   with *"Bye, Melissa." "Goodbye Rozzie, take care."*
> - **See you.** /ňąsiňź juňź/ ¬∑ /ňąsiňź.j…ô/ ‚ÄĒ Cambridge `see you (later)` = "goodbye."
> - **Let's catch up soon.** /lets k√¶t É  Ćp suňźn/ ‚ÄĒ Cambridge `catch up` phrasal verb.

**The Vietnamese trap:** the word-level translation of *"tŠļ°m biŠĽát"* is
*"goodbye"*, and learners default to it everywhere. But *"goodbye"* is **too
formal** for casual chat ‚ÄĒ natives say *"see you"*, *"take care"*, *"talk
soon"*. Using *"goodbye"* with a friend sounds distant or even final (as if you
will never meet again).

## 4. Pronunciation & delivery notes

- **Weak forms matter.** In *"I should let you go"*, the function words reduce:
  *should* ‚Üí / É äd/, *you* ‚Üí /j…ô/ weak, *to* ‚Üí /t…ô/. A word-by-word *"a…™ juňź
   É äd let juňź tuňź …°o ä"* sounds robotic. ūüĒó Drill the weak forms in
  [SENTENCE STRESS](../pronunciation/SENTENCE_STRESS.md).
- **Linking.** *let you* ‚Üí /let Éu/ or /letŐ¨ju/ (American flap); *catch up* ‚Üí
  /k√¶ňąt É Ćp/ (the /t É/ glues to / Ć/). ūüĒó See [LINKING](../pronunciation/LINKING.md).
- **Final consonants.** *"great"* /…°re…™t/ drops its /t/ at your peril ‚ÄĒ
  *"It was great talking to you"* with a missing /t/ sounds unfinished. ūüĒó See
  [FINAL CONSONANTS](../pronunciation/FINAL_CONSONANTS.md).
- **Intonation.** Pre-closing signals fall at the end (*"I should let you
  go."* ‚Üė); leave-takings can have a slight rise-warmth (*"Take care."* ‚Üó‚Üė).
  A flat *"take care"* reads as cold.
- **"Anyway" as a wrap marker.** Stress the first syllable: /ňąen.i.we…™/. It is
  the most common discourse marker before a close.

## 6. Vietnamese ‚Üí English L1 pitfalls table

The "expert payoff." These are the specific interference traps a Vietnamese
speaker hits when closing a conversation ‚ÄĒ extend, don't replace, the seed rows
from the spec.

| Vietnamese trap (what you do) | English fix (what to do instead) |
|---|---|
| **Skips the pre-closing** ‚ÄĒ stops and says *"bye"* abruptly, or just trails off | Always run **signal ‚Üí farewell**. Lead with *"Well, I should let you go"* or *"Anyway, I'd better get going"* *before* the goodbye. |
| **Over-loops the farewell** ‚ÄĒ *"ok‚Ä¶ ok‚Ä¶ bye‚Ä¶ bye‚Ä¶ bye"* to avoid seeming rude | One signal + one farewell. Don't stack goodbyes; a single *"Take care"* then go. |
| **Defaults to *"goodbye"* everywhere** (word-for-word *"tŠļ°m biŠĽát"*) | In casual chat use *"see you"*, *"take care"*, *"talk soon"*. Reserve *"goodbye"* for formal/neutral. |
| **Misses the appreciation turn** ‚ÄĒ never says *"It was great talking to you"* | Slot in the appreciation after the signal: *"Well, it was great talking to you. Take care."* |
| **Drops final consonants** ‚Üí *"I should let you go"* sounds like *"I should leh you go"* | Release the /t/ in *let* and the /d/ in *should*. ūüĒó [FINAL CONSONANTS](../pronunciation/FINAL_CONSONANTS.md). |
| **Flat intonation on *"take care"*** ‚Üí reads as cold/dismissive | Add slight rise-warmth (‚Üó‚Üė); *"Take care."* is warm, not robotic. ūüĒó [INTONATION](../pronunciation/INTONATION.md). |
| **Translates *"gŠļ∑p lŠļ°i sau"* as *"see you again"*** (stiff) | Native default is *"see you"* or *"see ya"*, not *"see you again"*. Cambridge glosses `see you (later)` = "goodbye." |
| **No discourse marker before close** (Vietnamese tone-shift does the job) | Use *"Well,‚Ä¶"* or *"Anyway,‚Ä¶"* as the audible wrap marker before the signal. |
| **Says *"I will go now"* (word-for-word *"t√īi ńĎi ńĎ√Ęy"*)** | Unidiomatic. Say *"I'd better get going"* or *"I should get going"* ‚ÄĒ the `get going` idiom is the native way. |
| **Forgets the future-plan closer** with friends ‚ÄĒ ends flat | Add *"Let's catch up soon"* / *"Talk soon"* to signal warmth + continuity. |
