## A. Issue ‚ÄĒ state it factually (the opener)

The first move of a written complaint is to **name the problem in facts, not
feelings**: date + what happened + your stance, before any remedy. This is the
move Vietnamese writers most often get wrong in *both* directions ‚ÄĒ they either
vent emotion (which looks unreasonable) or under-state so much that there is no
clear issue at all. Five openers, from neutral to firm.

## The discipline ‚ÄĒ no emotion-leak

The one rule that separates a complaint that gets resolved from one that gets
ignored. The FTC states it directly: *"Don't write an angry, sarcastic, or
threatening letter. The person reading your letter probably isn't responsible
for the problem, but may be very helpful in resolving it."* engxam reinforces
it: *"be sure to stick with the facts and avoid putting emotions into your
letter."* The four moves above only work if the tone stays **firm + factual +
solution-oriented**.

| ‚úÖ Keep (firm + factual) | ‚ĚĆ Leak (emotion weakens the case) |
|---|---|
| I am dissatisfied with the service. | This is ABSOLUTELY UNACCEPTABLE!!! |
| I would like a full refund. | I want my money back NOW or else!! |
| As a result, I missed my flight. | You RUINED my whole trip, unbelievable. |
| I look forward to your prompt response. | If you don't reply I'll expose you online!!! |

> All four "keep" lines are verbatim corpus rows above. The "leak" column is the
> anti-pattern (what to delete from a draft), not an attested model ‚ÄĒ included
> here only as the contrast the discipline is defined against.
