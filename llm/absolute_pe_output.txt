========================================================================
  SECTION E: ABSOLUTE vs RELATIVE - the proof (contrast RoPE Section H)
========================================================================
Same fixed raw Q,K as RoPE's proof, but we ADD sinusoidal PE instead of
rotating. Claim: the Q.K score depends on BOTH m_q and m_k SEPARATELY,
NOT only on (m_q - m_k).

| m_q | m_k | relative = m_q-m_k |   Q.K score   |
|-----|-----|-------------------|---------------|
| 2   | 1   | 1                 | +7.055621 |
| 5   | 4   | 1                 | +4.901406 |
| 10  | 9   | 1                 | +7.261821 |
| 2   | 0   | 2                 | +5.189337 |
| 5   | 3   | 2                 | +4.768941 |
| 10  | 8   | 2                 | +6.810799 |

distance=1 scores: ['+7.0556', '+4.9014', '+7.2618']  -> NOT equal
distance=2 scores: ['+5.1893', '+4.7689', '+6.8108']  -> NOT equal

CONTRAST with RoPE (ROPE.md Section H): there, distance=1 always gave
+0.514498 and distance=2 always gave +0.285792 - identical regardless
of absolute position. Here they all DIFFER. Adding a position vector
cannot make absolute positions cancel in the dot product; only the
rotation trick can.

========================================================================
  SECTION F: EXTRapolation beyond training length
========================================================================
What happens at a position the model never saw?

SINUSOIDAL: smooth function of m -> just keep evaluating it.
LEARNED wpe: the table only has `max_len` rows.
  wpe has shape (32, 8) -> rows 0..31
  Position m=200 -> INDEX ERROR / no row. Out-of-range.
  This is why GPT-2/nanoGPT hard-cap context at block_size.

LENGTH GENERALIZATION: absolute (esp. learned) is weak here. RoPE +
YaRN/NTK-aware scaling generalize to longer contexts far more
gracefully. (Modern LLMs chose RoPE largely for this reason.)
