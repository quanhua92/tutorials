5Writing task

Write a client message — all four moves: empathy + ownership + solution + warm close. Then reveal the model answer.

Scenario: A customer emails furious that their order arrived broken. Write the agent's reply — empathy first, then ownership, then a concrete solution, then a warm close.

Model (all four moves, in order):

[empathy] Hi Mai, I completely understand how frustrating it is to receive a broken item — I'd be upset too. /aɪ kəmˈpliːtli ˌʌndərˈstænd/ — empathy FIRST, before any policy

[ownership] Let me sort this out for you. /let miː ˈsɔːrt ðɪs aʊt fər juː/ — first-person owner, not passive

[solution] I've gone ahead and refunded the order in full, and a replacement is shipping today. /aɪv ˈɡɑːn əˈhed ənd ˌriːˈfʌndɪd/ — present-perfect = already done

[warm close] Is there anything else I can help with? — and please reach out anytime if the replacement doesn't arrive by Friday. /ɪz ðer ˈeniθɪŋ els/ — never close on the action

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Skips the empathy step — opens with "We have received your request" / "Theo quy định, quý khách vui lòng…"Open with one empathy sentence first, always — I completely understand… / I'm sorry to hear you've had this trouble. Empathy is move 1, not optional.
Robotic copy-paste — the same "Dear customer, we apologise for any inconvenience" for every caseVary the opener to the specific feeling: frustrating for anger, disappointing for a missed expectation, stressful for time pressure. The emotion word makes it human.
Passive-voice overuse to avoid agency — "your request is being processed" / "the matter will be looked into"Switch to the first-person owner: Let me sort this out for you / I'll take care of this. The passive reads as evasive, not polite.
Jumps straight to policy without acknowledging the feeling — "According to our policy, refunds take 3–5 days"Empathy then policy: I completely understand this is frustrating. Per our policy, the refund takes 3–5 days, and I've gone ahead and initiated it today.
Over-promises to please — "I will fix everything immediately" / "This will never happen again"Name one specific, bounded action you can deliver: I've gone ahead and refunded the order / I've escalated this to our billing team.
Closes on the action — ends the message with the refund / the update, no warm sign-offAdd the warm close: Is there anything else I can help with? / Reach out anytime. Two words turn a transaction into a relationship.
Drops the article / preposition — "Let me sort this for you" / "I've escalated this our team" (no to)Drill the fixed chunk intact: let me sort this out for you (out + for not optional), escalated this to the team (to not optional).
Mixes sorry (adjective) and apologise (verb) — "I sorry for the problem" / "I apologise you"I'm sorry + (that-)clause: I'm sorry you've had this trouble. I apologise for + noun: I apologise for the delay. 🔗 📖 APOLOGY_EMAILS.
Translates tiếc / xin lỗi as "sorry" everywhere — "Sorry, the refund is processing" where no apology is owedReserve sorry for when you're at fault. For bad luck / system issues, use empathy without apology: I get how frustrating that is — let me sort this out for you.
/θ/ → /t/, /r/ → /z/, final-cluster deletion in trouble /ˈtrʌbl/, frustrating /ˈfrʌstreɪtɪŋ/, anything elseDrill the empathy chunks aloud: hold /bl/ in trouble, /str/ in frustrating, release /ls/ in else. 🔗 📖 FINAL_CONSONANTS.
