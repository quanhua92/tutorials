HyperLogLog (HLL) — interactive HYPERLOGLOG.md hyperloglog.py

Count distinct in O(1) memory. Hash each item, look at the position of the leftmost 1-bit (rho); the max rho per bucket ≈ log₂(cardinality). Stochastic averaging over m buckets + the harmonic-mean estimator give a standard error of 1.04/√m. Step the stream and watch the bucket bits pick a register and the leading 1 widen it. Re-run live in JS, identical to hyperloglog.py.  |  ← Count-Min Sketch: count frequencies

① Step the stream — bucket by p bits, register keeps the max rho

What to do: step. Each item's 32-bit hash splits into p bucket bits (pick the register) and a tail. rho = 1 + (leading zeros in the tail) — the green leading-1 marks it. The register becomes max(old, rho). Grey items are duplicates — same hash, same rho, so they never move a register (HLL is duplicate-invariant).

Relative error of the same pinned stream recomputed live for every precision p = 4..14 (memory m = 2^p). The dashed curve is the theoretical standard error 1.04/√m. Drag p to read the estimate at that memory budget. Small m is noisy (m=16 → SE 26%); Redis's m=16384 → 0.81%.

At large m the stream (20 distinct) is sparse → the small-range linear-counting correction takes over and the estimate goes near-exact. That is why production HLL is both fast AND accurate: pick m for the error you want, and memory never depends on the cardinality.

③ Memory — HLL O(m) vs exact O(n), regardless of cardinality

Exact count-distinct stores every distinct id (a set, O(n)). HLL stores m = 2^p one-byte registers — fixed. Slide p and watch HLL stay flat while exact grows with the distinct count.
