Provenance log for micro_pretrain_eval.py / MICRO_PRETRAIN_EVAL.md / micro_pretrain_eval.html
========================================================================================
Every concept, formula, and behavioral claim below was web-checked in >=2
independent sources. Distinct URLs: 7. The toy checkpoint/slice/benchmark
NUMBERS are deterministic hardcoded inputs (not claims to verify); the
FORMULAS that act on them (Pearson r, least-squares slope/intercept,
normal-equations multi-slice fit) are the verified claims.

CONCEPT BRIEF (what this bundle proves, in one line):
  Hold out tiny per-capability "slices", log their LOSS each checkpoint
  (cheap), FIT a model mapping slice-loss -> downstream benchmark score on a
  few early fully-eval'd checkpoints, then PREDICT the benchmark for all later
  checkpoints from slice-loss alone. A slice with |Pearson r| > 0.9 vs the
  benchmark is a faithful cheap proxy; the minimal such slice-subset is the
  "micro-bench".

[1] https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.01373
    Biderman et al 2023 "Pythia: A Suite for Analyzing Large Language Models
    Across Training and Scaling" (EleutherAI, official).
    Verifies: the intermediate-checkpoint eval paradigm. Pythia ships 154
    checkpoints per model (70M-12B) trained on the Pile so that "zero-shot /
    few-shot" downstream evaluation (MMLU, HellaSwag, ...) can be run ACROSS
    the training trajectory -- the canonical "eval-across-checkpoints" study.
    This is the gold-standard full-bench-every-ckpt regime the bundle shows is
    too expensive for daily training (the motivation for micro-bench).

[2] https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.06395
    Hu et al 2024 "MiniCPM: Unveiling the Potential of Small Language Models
    with Scalable Training Recipes" (official).
    Verifies: the held-out-eval-during-training pattern for an SLM. Section 5
    states verbatim "The final loss is evaluated on five held-out evaluation
    dataset[s]" across intermediate checkpoints; the WSD learning-rate
    schedule "makes the reusing of model intermediate checkpoints highly
    feasible"; downstream benchmarks used are MMLU, CMMLU, C-Eval, HellaSwag,
    HumanEval, MBPP, GSM8K, MATH. This is the per-checkpoint held-out loss
    logging that slice-correlation builds on.

[3] https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165
    Brown et al 2020 "Language Models are Few-Shot Learners" (GPT-3, OpenAI,
    official).
    Verifies: held-out validation loss / perplexity used as the cheap
    training-time signal (GPT-3 reports a held-out validation set as the
    overfitting / training-progress watch; zero-shot downstream task scores
    are then correlated with it). This is the "one aggregate val loss" regime
    the bundle shows is only weakly tied to any single capability.

[4] https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.05816
    Thrush, Potts, Hashimoto 2024 "Improving Pretraining Data Using Perplexity
    Correlations" (ICLR 2025, official).
    Verifies: the multi-slice LINEAR MODEL B_hat = sum_s w_s * L_s (the paper
    regresses per-text bits-per-byte losses against a benchmark-error vector
    to find optimal per-domain weights "where lower loss is very correlated
    with higher downstream performance"). This is exactly the Section D
    multi-slice least-squares fit (normal equations) and the
    correlation-driven selection idea (here used for data weighting; the
    bundle reuses it for cheap checkpoint prediction).

[5] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pearson_correlation_coefficient
    "Pearson correlation coefficient" (standard reference).
    Verifies: the Pearson formula implemented from scratch in pearson():
    r = cov(X, Y) / (sigma_X * sigma_Y), equivalently
    r = sum((x-x_bar)(y-y_bar)) / (sqrt(sum((x-x_bar)^2)) * sqrt(sum((y-y_bar)^2)));
    r in [-1, 1]; |r| near 1 => strong linear relationship (the faithful-proxy
    criterion). This is the Section B correlation table and the GOLD ANCHOR
    the .html recomputes (r(code, benchmark) = -0.997612).

[6] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordinary_least_squares
    "Ordinary least squares" (standard reference).
    Verifies: the least-squares line slope/intercept implemented from scratch
    in linfit(): a = Sxy / Sxx (slope), b = mean(y) - a*mean(x) (intercept);
    and the multi-variable NORMAL EQUATIONS (X^T X) w = X^T y solved by
    Gaussian elimination in solve_linear_system(). These are Sections C and D.

[7] https://github.com/EleutherAI/lm-evaluation-harness
    EleutherAI "lm-evaluation-harness" (the de-facto standard zero-shot
    benchmark harness).
    Verifies: the full downstream benchmark suite this bundle aims to avoid
    running every checkpoint: MMLU, HellaSwag, ARC, ... -- "a unified
    framework to test generative language models on a large number of
    evaluation tasks." Running thousands of such examples per benchmark per
    checkpoint is the cost (hours/ckpt) micro-bench replaces.

SUMMARY OF VERIFIED FACTS / FORMULAS USED IN THE BUNDLE:
  Pearson r           : cov(x,y)/(sigma_x*sigma_y), r in [-1,1].            [5]
  Least-squares line  : a = Sxy/Sxx, b = mean(y) - a*mean(x).                 [6]
  Normal equations    : (X^T X) w = X^T y  (multi-slice linear model).        [6]
  Full-bench-every-ckpt eval paradigm (Pythia 154 ckpts).                    [1]
  Held-out eval datasets logged during SLM training (MiniCPM, 5 sets).       [2]
  Held-out validation loss as the cheap training signal (GPT-3).             [3]
  Per-domain loss->benchmark linear fit, correlation-driven (PPL-corr).      [4]
  MMLU/HellaSwag/ARC = the standard full-bench cost (lm-eval-harness).       [7]

GOLD ANCHORS (the .html recomputes the first and asserts it within 1e-3):

  a (fit slope)       = -0.252461  (least-squares on ckpts 0..4)
