6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Face-wound reflex — justify ("Nhưng mà…" / "But…") or accuse ("Không phải lỗi của em") before acknowledgingForce the acknowledge-first rule: say "Thanks for the feedback" before any "but".
Stony silence when criticised — VN politeness says don't argue back, but a native manager reads silence as sulkingUse a commitment token: "I'll work on that" or "I hadn't thought of it that way."
Missing "thank you" uptake — Vietnamese thanks for gifts, not for criticismLearn the chunk whole: "Thanks for the feedback" is a formula, not a literal thank-you. It signals "received".
Treating feedback as a personal attack — hears "your work is late" as "you are lazy"Hear the SBI structure. Respond to the behaviour, not to an imagined insult.
Drops /θ/ → "tanks" for Thanks, "tought" for thoughtTongue-between-teeth drill for /θ/ in Thanks /θæŋks/ and thought /θɔːt/.
Drops the /kst/ cluster → "nest time" for next timeHold the cluster tight: /nekst/. Don't delete a consonant.
No contraction → "I will work on that" (stiff) instead of "I'll work on that"Drill the contraction I'll /aɪl/ as one syllable. Spoken English contracts.
Over-apologises — "Sorry, sorry, I'm so sorry" (VN xin lỗi stacking) → reads as grovellingOne acknowledge + one commit is enough. Replace the apology stack with "I'll work on that / Next time I'll…"
Asks for clarification as a challenge → "What do you mean?" (sounds hostile)Soften with the modal: "Could you say more about…?" / "Could you give me an example?"
