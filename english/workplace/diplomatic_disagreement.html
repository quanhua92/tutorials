5Writing task

Write a diplomatic disagreement. Use both "I see your point, however…" and "Have we considered…?". Then reveal the model answer.

Model (acknowledge → pause → counter; then question-form counter):

I see your point — shipping early gets us feedback faster. [acknowledge; pause]

However, I wonder if we could hold the launch one more week? [soft connector + tentative counter as question]

Have we considered the load-testing results before we go live? [inclusive group-question counter]

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
Suppresses disagreement entirely to preserve harmony (giữ thể diện)Use the acknowledge-then-counter pattern: "I see your point, however…" — disagree without breaking harmony.
States disagreement bluntly: "No, you're wrong" / "I disagree"Never open with the counter. Open with the acknowledgement, pause, then the counter.
Goes straight to the declarative ("We should…") which sounds like an orderRecast the counter as a hedged question: "I wonder if we could…?" / "Have we considered…?"
Under-uses the partial-agreement-before-but pattern (structurally unfamiliar)Drill the 8 chunks until acknowledge → connector → counter is automatic. Biggest register-climb to professional English.
Drops the down-toner actually → "I'm not sure I agree" alone sounds bluntAdd the trailing actually: "I'm not sure I agree, actually."
Runs acknowledge + counter together ("I-see-your-point-however…") → reads as sarcasmInsert a real pause: "I see your point… ↑" (beat) "…however…". The pause pays the hearer's face.
High, fast intonation on the counter → sounds confrontationalDrop the pitch on the counter's main stress, slow down: "…I wonder if we could…".
Confuses interesting with good → "That's an interesting take" said flatly reads passive-aggressiveSay interesting with a genuine slight rise, then the counter. The word is neutral; the tune decides.
Skips articles/copula under stress → "Is good idea, but…"Drill the full chunk: "That's an interesting take, but…". Defaults regress first under pressure.
/θ/ in worth → /t/ → "wort"Tongue-between-teeth for /θ/: worth /wɜːθ/. A dropped /θ/ turns a hedge into a mumble.
