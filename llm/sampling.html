The big idea (no code needed): picking the next word is like rolling a loaded die — one face per possible word. The model gives every face a score (logit); we convert scores into odds that sum to 1 (softmax), then we either always take the top face (greedy), flatten/sharpen the die (temperature), or blank out faces before rolling (top-k / top-p).
logit = a raw score for a word (bigger = liked more) softmax = scores → probabilities that sum to 1 prob = a share of the total, in [0,1]
temperature T = how flat the die is (high = flatter/random) top-k = keep k best faces, blank the rest top-p (nucleus) = keep the smallest set covering p of the mass cumsum = running total of the probabilities
Quick start: click a preset, then watch panel ①. top-k=0 / top-p=1.0 mean "no filtering" (off). Fixed 8-token vocab, logits = [2.3, 2.0, 0.4, 1.5, 0.1, 2.5, 0.7, 1.2] (same as sampling.py).

① The distribution after filtering — green = kept, red = masked to −inf

Bars = each word's probability. Drag top-p and watch the green nucleus shrink/grow; red bars are masked out. Drag temperature to sharpen/flatten. The grey ghost bars show the raw odds with no filter. Click draw in panel ③ to see which word is picked.
kept (in nucleus / top-k) masked to −inf (prob → 0) last draw (seeded) raw prob (no filter, T=1) for reference

② The top-p nucleus, sorted — cumsum on PROBABILITIES (not logprobs!)

Words sorted most-likely first. The cumsum column is a running total of the probabilities. We keep every word while the total is still under top-p (green), and we always keep the #1 word so the nucleus is never empty. This table is exactly what sampling.py Section E prints.
The #1 bug: if you cumsum the raw scores (logprobs) instead of the probabilities, the total stays negative and every word is kept — silent garbage. Why cumsum must be on probs →

③ The only random step — seeded categorical draw

Filtering (panels ①②) is fully deterministic. This is the only roll of the die. Fix the seed and you get the same word every time; change the seed and you get a different word — always inside the green nucleus.
