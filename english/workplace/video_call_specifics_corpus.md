## A. Mute issues

The single most-used video-call chunk on earth since 2020. The word *mute* as a
call-state verb (`You're on mute`, `I'll mute myself`) and the noun *mute button*
are now standard business English — Cambridge documents both the **`on mute`**
idiom ("having the mute button pressed on a device so that it does not produce
any sound") and the **call-verb** sense ("to make a person unable to be heard,
for example when using video conferencing software").

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| You're on mute. | your mic is muted; we can't hear you | UK /jɔː(r) ɒn ˈmjuːt/ · US /jʊr ɑːn ˈmjuːt/ | https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/mute | content *mute* /mjuːt/ ≈#2500; top call phrase (post-2020) | US/UK |
| I think you're on mute. | softer, hedged cue that the speaker is muted | UK /aɪ ˈθɪŋk jɔː(r) ɒn ˈmjuːt/ · US /aɪ ˈθɪŋk jʊr ɑːn ˈmjuːt/ | https://18kenglish.com/con-call-chinese-conference-call-english-2026/ (attests "I think you might be on mute — we can't hear you") + https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/mute | hedged variant of the top call phrase | US/UK |

> **Verification note:** `mute` /mjuːt/ (UK/US identical) and the `on mute`
> idiom + call-verb sense confirmed directly in the Cambridge Advanced Learner's
> Dictionary page (web-fetched 2026-06-23); `unmute` /ˌʌnˈmjuːt/ confirmed in

Every step is a fixed chunk.

## D. Reconnect

When a participant or the whole call drops, the recovery is a fixed two-step
chunk: name the loss → propose the rejoin.

| English chunk | meaning | IPA | source URL | frequency rank | accent |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| I think we lost him/her/them. | a participant's connection dropped | UK /aɪ ˈθɪŋk wi lɒst ðəm/ · US /aɪ ˈθɪŋk wi lɑːst ðəm/ | https://preply.com/en/blog/business-calls-in-english/ + https://www.speakconfidentenglish.com/english-phrases-online-meetings/ | high-freq call-drop phrase | US/UK |
