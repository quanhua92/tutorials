5Writing task — one message, three registers

Take one intent — “send me the Q3 report” — and write it at all three rungs. Then reveal the side-by-side models to compare your register choices.

Formal ↑

I hope this finds you well. I am writing to inquire about the Q3 report. Furthermore, please find attached the data you requested.

no contractions · Latinate words · hedged modal

Neutral

Hi Alex, Could you send over the Q3 report when you have a moment? Also, here's the data you asked for.

one contraction · modal "could" · first name

Casual ↓

Hey, quick one — got the Q3 report handy? Plus the data's attached. Cheers!

contractions · "hey"/"plus"/"cheers" · no modal

6L1 pitfalls & cheat sheet

Vietnamese → English pitfalls

Vietnamese trap (what you do)English fix (what to do instead)
No honorific word to grab onto → defaults over-formal: “Dear Sir” to a peer whose name you knowUse the person's first name: “Hi Alex,” not “Dear Sir”. Respect = right name, not stacked titles. Dear Sir/Madam is only when you genuinely don't know the name.
Translates em–anh intimacy → opens a work email with “Dear Brother” or skips the greetingMatch the channel: email → “Hi [Name],”; Slack → “hey” (lowercase) or nothing. Intimacy lives in the first name + contraction, not a kinship term.
Single you feels too blunt → over-hedges: “I am very sorry to bother you but I was wondering if perhaps you might possibly…”Hedge once. “Could you send the file by Friday?” is already polite (modal could). Stacking 4 softeners reads insecure, not respectful.
Treats tôi–bạn as “casual” → drops to “Hey, what's up, send me the file” to a new clientBạn is neutral peer, not casual. Map tôi–bạn to the neutral rung (“Hi [Name], could you…”); reserve “Hey” for nickname-level closeness.
Mixed register in one email — formal open + slang close (“…finds you well” … “cheers, lmk asap lol”)Hold one rung per message. Open formal → close formal (“Best regards”). Mixed register signals uncalibrated copy-pasted phrases.
Translates register word-by-word → “Furthermore, I would like to add that moreover…” (stacking markers like Vietnamese politeness particles)Use one connector per idea. Furthermore OR moreover OR in addition — never two. Stacking formal markers sounds robotic.
No contraction instinct → “I am writing to you. I do not have the file. It is not here.” in a casual SlackIn casual/neutral writing, contract by default: I'm, I don't, it isn't. Uncontracted forms are for formal email and emphasis.
Skips the sign-off match → signs a formal email “Cheers” (too casual) or a casual Slack “Yours sincerely” (too stiff)Match sign-off to opener: formal → Best/Kind regards, Sincerely; neutral → Best, Thanks; casual → Cheers, Thanks, (nothing).
